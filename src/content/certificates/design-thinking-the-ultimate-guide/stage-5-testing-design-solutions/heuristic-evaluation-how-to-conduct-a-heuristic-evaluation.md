## What Is a Heuristic Evaluation?

Heuristic evaluation is a usability inspection method where one or more usability experts review a design against a set of established usability principles (called heuristics). Experts identify potential usability problems based on their experience and knowledge of good design practices.

It is often used as an early step before testing with real users, helping to catch obvious issues quickly and cost-effectively.

## Key Advantages and Disadvantages

**Pros**:

- Can be performed at any stage of the design process, even on low-fidelity prototypes like sketches or wireframes
- Relatively quick and inexpensive compared to full usability testing with users
- Good for evaluating novel interfaces against best practices

**Cons**:

- Not as effective as testing with real users — experts may find issues that don’t actually affect users
- Experts are not the target audience, so it may miss real user problems or domain-specific issues
- Less useful for checking whether the solution truly addresses user needs

## Nielsen’s 10 Usability Heuristics

Jakob Nielsen’s widely used set of 10 heuristics serves as an excellent starting point:

1. **Visibility of system status** — Keep users informed about what is happening.
2. **Match between system and the real world** — Use familiar language and follow real-world conventions.
3. **User control and freedom** — Provide clear “emergency exits,” undo, and redo options.
4. **Consistency and standards** — Follow platform conventions so users don’t have to guess.
5. **Error prevention** — Design to prevent problems rather than just handling them.
6. **Recognition rather than recall** — Make options visible to reduce memory load.
7. **Flexibility and efficiency of use** — Support both novice and expert users with accelerators.
8. **Aesthetic and minimalist design** — Avoid irrelevant information.
9. **Help users recognize, diagnose, and recover from errors** — Provide clear, constructive error messages.
10. **Help and documentation** — Provide easy-to-find, task-focused help when needed.

## How to Conduct a Heuristic Evaluation

1. **Select evaluators** — Use 3–5 usability experts (ideally with different expertise). They should not be your end users.
2. **Brief the evaluators** — Give clear instructions and focus areas to ensure consistency.
3. **First evaluation** — Let evaluators freely explore the product to understand its scope and interactions.
4. **Second evaluation** — Have them systematically check the design against the chosen heuristics.
5. **Record problems** — Ask evaluators to note issues in detail.
6. **Debriefing session** — Collate findings, discuss problems, and suggest solutions.

## The Takeaway

Heuristic evaluation is a fast, cost-effective way to identify usability issues early in the design process using expert knowledge. While it cannot replace testing with real users, it serves as a valuable first step to catch low-hanging fruit and improve your design before investing in full usability tests.

Use Nielsen’s 10 heuristics as your foundation, involve multiple experts, and always follow up with real user testing for the best results.
