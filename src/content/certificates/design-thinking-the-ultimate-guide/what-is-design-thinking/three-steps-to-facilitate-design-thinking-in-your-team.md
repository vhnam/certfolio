## The Mindset Needed for Design Thinking

Design thinking often pushes teams into unfamiliar territory. Participants must be **open-minded, curious, collaborative and willing to challenge assumptions**. The process can feel chaotic, but this discomfort helps teams innovate, improve products and build stronger collaboration. Diversity in perspectives is essential because different experiences and viewpoints lead to richer problem-solving.

However, newcomers may feel confused or intimidated by the non-linear nature of the process. Facilitators can help teams adjust through structured preparation activities.

## Step 1: Explain the Design Thinking Process

Before starting a project, facilitators should clearly explain how design thinking works.

### The Five Modes of Design Thinking

- **Empathize** – Research and understand users’ needs.
- **Define** – Clearly state the users’ problems.
- **Ideate** – Generate and challenge ideas and assumptions.
- **Prototype** – Build early versions of solutions.
- **Test** – Evaluate solutions with users.

These stages are **not linear steps**. Teams can move back and forth between them, repeat stages, or work on multiple stages simultaneously.

Design thinking also combines:

- **Analytical thinking** from science
- **Empathy and holistic understanding** from ethnography and design

Common methods used include interviews, brainstorming, affinity mapping, sketching and usability testing.

## Step 2: Break the Ice

Icebreakers help teams overcome initial barriers when starting a design thinking project, especially when participants are unfamiliar with each other or the process.

Effective icebreakers should:

- Address existing team barriers.
- Avoid embarrassing or excluding participants.
- Build confidence and comfort within the group.
- Level the playing field so hierarchy does not dominate participation.

The goal is to create a **safe and collaborative environment** before beginning creative work.

## Step 3: Encourage Creativity

Many people do not consider themselves creative, especially those with technical or analytical backgrounds. Facilitators should help unlock creativity through engaging activities.

### Co-Create a Story

A group storytelling exercise where each participant adds to a story. This encourages imagination, relaxation and creative thinking.

### Visual Telephone

Participants alternately **draw and interpret images**, passing them around the group. The final result often differs significantly from the original idea, illustrating how communication and interpretation work in creative collaboration.

These exercises energize teams and prepare them for ideation sessions.

## Overcoming Common Barriers to Innovation

Design thinking helps teams move beyond common obstacles such as:

- Fear of failure
- Intimidation
- Low self-confidence
- Organizational hierarchy
- Creative blocks
- Blind spots
- Over-specialization

## Benefits for Teams

When teams adopt design thinking successfully, they often experience:

- Greater **inclusiveness**
- Stronger **team cohesion**
- More **collaboration and interaction**
- Increased **confidence in creativity**

## Key Takeaway

To prepare a team for design thinking:

1. **Explain the design thinking process.**
2. **Use icebreakers to reduce tension and build trust.**
3. **Encourage creativity through engaging exercises.**

Following these steps helps teams become more collaborative, confident and innovative—leading to better products, services and solutions.
