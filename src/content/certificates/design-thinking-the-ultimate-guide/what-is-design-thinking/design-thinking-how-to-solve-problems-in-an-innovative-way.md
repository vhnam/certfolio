## Why Traditional Thinking No Longer Works

Modern problems are more complex due to globalization, technological change, and interconnected systems. Traditional problem-solving methods are often too linear and technical.

Design thinking introduces a **non-linear, human-centered approach** that helps teams rethink problems and create innovative solutions.

Don Norman argues that design must evolve from designing **individual products** to designing **entire systems**, such as education, healthcare, and sustainability.

## Human-Centered Design as the Core Approach

Design thinking focuses on **human needs, experiences, and contexts** rather than purely technological or business considerations.

This approach balances:

- Human needs
- Business goals
- Societal impact
- Logic and creativity

As a result, solutions become more meaningful and relevant to people.

## What Problems Design Thinking Is Best For

Design thinking is especially useful for solving **complex and ambiguous problems**.

Examples include:

- Redefining value in products or services
- Improving human-centered innovation
- Increasing quality of life
- Changing markets and behaviors
- Adapting to rapid social or technological change
- Reinventing business models
- Addressing societal challenges such as health or education

## Situations Where Design Thinking Works Well

Design thinking is particularly effective when problems:

- Affect diverse groups of people
- Involve multiple systems
- Require multidisciplinary teams
- Cannot be solved with data alone
- Require creativity and inspiration

It is generally **less useful for simple problems with clear solutions**.

## A Holistic Way to Approach Challenges

Design thinking addresses problems that occur at intersections such as:

- Business and society
- Logic and emotion
- Creativity and rational analysis
- Human needs and economic demands

Because of this, it provides a **holistic perspective** that traditional methods often lack.

## Responding to Rapid Social and Technological Change

Modern society is constantly disrupted by:

- New technologies
- Changing consumer expectations
- Global economic shifts
- Increased connectivity

Design thinking helps organizations **adapt quickly**, rethink assumptions, and explore new possibilities.

## Key Takeaway

Design thinking is a **human-centered and flexible approach to innovation**. It helps individuals and organizations tackle complex challenges, adapt to rapid change, and create solutions that improve both human experiences and societal systems.
