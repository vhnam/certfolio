## Core Principles Across Design Thinking Frameworks

### Definition

- Design Thinking Framework: A structured representation of the design thinking process used to guide innovation and problem-solving.

Most frameworks contain **3–7 stages**, but they share the same underlying principles.

### Common Traits

- Empathy-first approach: Understanding users and their needs.
- Problem reframing: Looking at challenges from new perspectives.
- Divergent thinking: Generating many ideas without judgment.
- Convergent thinking: Narrowing and refining promising ideas.
- Prototyping: Creating tangible representations of ideas.
- Testing: Evaluating solutions with users.
- Iteration: Revisiting stages and refining solutions based on new insights.

### Collaboration

Design thinking processes are typically carried out by:

- Multidisciplinary teams
- Cross-functional groups
- Participants with diverse skills and perspectives

Goal:

- Produce solutions that are **desirable, feasible, and viable**.

## Stanford Design School 5-Stage Model

![Stanford Design School 5-Stage Model](../../../../assets/images/design-thinking-the-ultimate-guide/what-is-design-thinking/stanford-design-school-5-stage-model.avif)

### Phases

1. **Empathize:** Understand users and their experiences.
2. **Define:** Frame the core problem based on insights.
3. **Ideate:** Generate many potential solutions.
4. **Prototype:** Build representations of ideas.
5. **Test:** Evaluate solutions with users.

Characteristics:

- Non-linear and iterative.
- Stages are **modes of thinking**, not strict steps.

## Herbert Simon’s Traditional Design Process

![Herbert Simon’s Traditional Design Process](../../../../assets/images/design-thinking-the-ultimate-guide/what-is-design-thinking/herbert-simon-s-traditional-design-process.avif)

### Seven Stages

1. **Define:** Clarify the problem.
2. **Research:** Investigate context and constraints.
3. **Ideate:** Generate possible solutions.
4. **Prototype:** Build preliminary models.
5. **Choose:** Select promising solutions.
6. **Implement:** Execute the chosen solution.
7. **Learn:** Reflect on outcomes and insights.

Contribution:

- Provided the foundation for many modern design processes.

## AIGA: Head, Heart and Hand

![AIGA: Head, Heart and Hand](../../../../assets/images/design-thinking-the-ultimate-guide/what-is-design-thinking/aiga-head-heart-and-hand.avif)

### Concept

Design thinking relies on three complementary abilities.

- **Head:** Analytical thinking and problem-solving.
- **Heart:** Empathy and emotional understanding of users.
- **Hand:** Practical creation and experimentation.

Purpose:

- Combine intellectual, emotional, and practical skills for holistic design.

## IDEO DeepDive Methodology

![IDEO DeepDive Methodology](../../../../assets/images/design-thinking-the-ultimate-guide/what-is-design-thinking/ideo-deepdive-methodology.avif)

### Stages

1. **Understand:** Learn about the problem context.
2. **Observe:** Study users and behaviors.
3. **Visualize:** Generate ideas and concepts.
4. **Evaluate:** Assess potential solutions.
5. **Implement:** Develop and deploy solutions.

Purpose:

- Rapid immersion into complex problems for effective innovation.

## IDEO 3-Stage Design Thinking Process

![IDEO 3-Stage Design Thinking Process](../../../../assets/images/design-thinking-the-ultimate-guide/what-is-design-thinking/ideo-3-stage-design-thinking-process.avif)

### Stages

1. **Inspiration:** Identify problems or opportunities.
2. **Ideation:** Generate and develop ideas.
3. **Implementation:** Turn ideas into real solutions.

Additional methods:

- **Learn:** Analyze existing information and data.
  - Goal: Understand the context, industry standards, and existing solutions to identify patterns and constraints.
- **Look:** Observe real-life user behavior in their natural environment.
  - Goal: Discover the gap between what people say and what they actually do to uncover latent (unmet) needs.
- **Ask:** Engage directly with people to hear their stories.
  - Goal: Elicit personal experiences, motivations, and emotional drivers through interviews or surveys.
- **Try:** Experience the problem firsthand and simulate solutions.
  - Goal: Build quick prototypes and role-play scenarios to build empathy and test hypotheses early.

These methods support the innovation cycle.

## IDEO Human-Centered Design Toolkit

![IDEO Human-Centered Design Toolkit](../../../../assets/images/design-thinking-the-ultimate-guide/what-is-design-thinking/ideo-human-centered-design-toolkit.avif)

### HCD Stages

1. **Hear:** Understand users and define the problem.
2. **Create:** Explore ideas through experimentation and prototyping.
3. **Deliver:** Implement and scale the solution.

Purpose:

- Adapt design thinking for **social innovation and developing communities**.

## Double Diamond Model (Design Council)

![Double Diamond Model (Design Council)](../../../../assets/images/design-thinking-the-ultimate-guide/what-is-design-thinking/double-diamond-model-design-council.avif)

### Four Phases

1. **Discover:** Explore the problem space and user needs.
2. **Define:** Synthesize insights into a clear problem statement.
3. **Develop:** Generate and test potential solutions.
4. **Deliver:** Finalize and launch the solution.

Key Concept:

- Alternates between **divergent and convergent thinking**.

## Frog Design Collective Action Toolkit

![Frog Design Collective Action Toolkit](../../../../assets/images/design-thinking-the-ultimate-guide/what-is-design-thinking/frog-design-collective-action-toolkit.avif)

### Six Stages

1. **Clarify your goal:** Define the problem and objectives.
2. **Build your group:** Assemble a collaborative team.
3. **Seek new understanding:** Research users and context.
4. **Imagine new ideas:** Generate solutions.
5. **Make something real:** Prototype and test ideas.
6. **Plan for action:** Organize implementation.

Characteristics:

- Designed for **community-driven innovation**.
- Non-linear and iterative.

## Designing for Growth Framework

![Designing for Growth Framework](../../../../assets/images/design-thinking-the-ultimate-guide/what-is-design-thinking/designing-for-growth-framework.avif)

### Four Key Questions

1. **What is?:** Understand the current situation.
2. **What if?:** Imagine future possibilities.
3. **What wows?:** Identify solutions users value.
4. **What works?:** Implement solutions that succeed in the market.

Focus:

- Strategic innovation and business design.

## LUMA System of Innovation

![LUMA System of Innovation](../../../../assets/images/design-thinking-the-ultimate-guide/what-is-design-thinking/luma-system-of-innovation.avif)

### Three Design Skills

- **Looking:** Observing users and contexts.
- **Understanding:** Interpreting insights and defining problems.
- **Making:** Creating and testing solutions.

Characteristics:

- Flexible system for solving problems across industries.

## Key Takeaways

- Many design thinking frameworks exist, typically containing **3–7 stages**.
- Despite differences, they share core principles:
  - Empathy
  - Problem reframing
  - Ideation
  - Prototyping
  - Testing
  - Iteration
- Frameworks vary depending on:
  - Industry
  - Context
  - Organizational needs
- The most important aspect is **understanding the principles rather than rigidly following steps**.
