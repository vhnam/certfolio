## Understanding the Purpose of the Define Phase

The Define phase focuses on **making sense of the insights gathered during the Empathize phase**. Designers analyze research findings and synthesize them into a clear understanding of users’ needs. The goal is to transform research insights into a **well-defined design challenge**, usually expressed through a Point of View (POV) statement.

By completing this phase, design teams gain a clearer direction for the next stage of the process, which is **generating ideas and solutions**.

## Key Methods Used in the Define Phase

Several important tools help designers analyze research data and define the design challenge.

- **Affinity Diagrams** help organize large amounts of research data into meaningful groups or themes.
- **Personas** represent different user types, allowing designers to think about users as individuals with goals and motivations.
- **Empathy Maps** summarize research insights and provide a deeper understanding of users’ thoughts, feelings, and behaviors.
- **Point of View (POV) Statements** define the core design challenge based on user needs and insights.
- **How Might We (HMW) Questions** transform the problem statement into open-ended questions that guide ideation.
- **Stakeholder Maps** identify and analyze the individuals or groups who influence or are affected by the project.

Together, these methods help teams create a **clear, user-centered definition of the problem they want to solve**.

## Applying Define Methods in Real-World Situations

Practical exercises and discussions help designers apply these methods in real-life contexts. By working through real scenarios, designers learn how to:

- Analyze research findings
- Define meaningful design challenges
- Translate insights into actionable problem statements

These activities also help designers **document their process and develop case studies**, which can later be used in their professional portfolios.

## Preparing for the Ideate Phase

After completing the Define phase, designers are ready to move into the **Ideate phase**, where they generate potential solutions to the problem.

The insights, problem statements, and questions developed in the Define phase provide the **foundation for effective ideation**, ensuring that ideas remain focused on solving real user needs.
