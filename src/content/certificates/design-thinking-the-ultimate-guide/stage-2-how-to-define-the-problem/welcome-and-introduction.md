## Define Stage Overview

### Purpose of the Define Phase

After completing the **Empathize phase**, designers collect a large amount of user research data and insights. The next step is to analyze and synthesize this information in order to clearly define the design problem.

The **Define stage** focuses on transforming research findings into a clear understanding of:

- Who the users are
- What their needs are
- What problem the design should solve

This stage helps teams create a **focused problem statement** that guides the rest of the design process.

## Learning Goals of the Lesson

### Key Concepts and Methods

This lesson introduces several methods used to analyze and synthesize research findings:

- Affinity diagrams: Organize and group research insights to identify patterns.
- Personas: Represent key user types to maintain user-centered design decisions.
- Empathy maps: Visualize what users think, feel, say, and do.
- Analysis and synthesis methods: Additional approaches for interpreting research data.

### Problem Framing Techniques

Two important tools help translate research insights into actionable design directions:

- Point of View (POV) statement: Defines the user, their need, and the key insight.
- “How Might We?” questions: Turn problem statements into opportunities for ideation.

These techniques help designers move smoothly from the **Define stage** into the **Ideate stage**.

## Applying the Methods in Practice

### Portfolio Exercise

At the end of the lesson, learners complete the **second exercise in the Build Your Portfolio project**, where they will:

- Apply Define-stage methods
- Practice synthesizing research insights
- Create problem statements and ideation prompts

This exercise can be completed using:

- The provided project brief
- A real-world project the learner is already working on
