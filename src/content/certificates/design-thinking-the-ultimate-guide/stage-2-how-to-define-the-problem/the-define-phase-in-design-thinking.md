## Define Phase Overview

### Definition

- Define Phase: The stage in the design thinking process where designers **clarify the problem** by synthesizing insights from the Empathize phase and creating a **meaningful, actionable problem statement**.

This phase transforms large amounts of user research into a **clear design focus**, helping teams understand:

- Who the users are
- What their real needs are
- What problem should be solved

A well-defined problem ensures the design team **does not work blindly** and provides direction for the rest of the design process.

> “Two goals of the define mode are to develop a deep understanding of your users and the design space and, based on that understanding, to come up with an actionable problem statement.”
> – d.school, Bootcamp Bootleg

### Importance of the Define Phase

- Clarifies the design challenge
- Prevents teams from solving the wrong problem
- Provides focus for ideation and solution development
- Guides the entire design process

A strong problem definition acts as a **guiding light** throughout the project.

### Terminology

- Design Problem: The issue designers aim to solve.
- Design Challenge: Often used interchangeably with design problem in this course.

## Goal of the Define Phase

### Core Objective

- Problem Statement: A clear, meaningful, and actionable description of the user problem the design team will solve.

To achieve this, designers must:

1. Review insights gathered during the Empathize phase.
2. Analyze research findings.
3. Synthesize observations into clear user problems.

> “The define mode is when you unpack and synthesize your empathy findings into compelling needs and insights, and scope a specific and meaningful challenge. It is a mode of ‘focus’ rather than ‘flaring.’”
> – d.school, Bootcamp Bootleg

### Position in the Design Thinking Process

Define is the **second phase** in design thinking:

1. Empathize
2. Define
3. Ideate
4. Prototype
5. Test

Important characteristics:

- The process is **iterative and non-linear**
- Teams may revisit the Define phase when new insights appear
- Stages should be viewed as **modes of thinking rather than strict steps**

## Analysis and Synthesis

![Illustration of the circular path of analysis and synthesis.](../../../../assets/images/design-thinking-the-ultimate-guide/stage-2-how-to-define-the-problem/analysis-and-synthesis.avif)

### Purpose

During the Define phase, designers use **analysis and synthesis** to transform research data into actionable insights.

> “Knowledge comes by taking things apart, analysis. But wisdom comes by putting things together.”
> – John A. Morrison

## Analysis

### Definition

- Analysis: The process of **breaking complex information into smaller parts** to understand patterns and problems.

### When Analysis Happens

- During the Define phase while reviewing research findings
- When identifying patterns, correlations, variations, and outliers in data
- When evaluating whether more research is needed

### Benefits

- Helps designers understand the problem deeply
- Reveals trends and insights from user research
- Focuses the team on meaningful design challenges

## Synthesis

### Definition

- Synthesis: The process of **combining insights from analyzed data** to form coherent ideas, problem statements, or solutions.

### When Synthesis Happens

- Define phase: Organizing and interpreting research to create a problem statement
- Ideate phase: Combining ideas to identify promising solutions
- Prototype phase: Interpreting test results to choose the best solution

### Benefits

- Reveals connections and patterns in user insights
- Helps generate meaningful design directions
- Produces clear problem definitions and solution ideas

## Analysis + Synthesis = Insights

Analysis and synthesis are **core activities throughout the entire design thinking process**.

Designers often repeat the cycle:

1. Analyze information to break it into parts.
2. Synthesize insights to create meaning.
3. Analyze potential solutions.
4. Synthesize them into a final design.

Both processes are equally important for:

- Creating design options
- Making decisions
- Developing strong solutions

## Methods for Defining the Problem

### Common Techniques

Several tools help designers analyze and synthesize qualitative research:

- Affinity Diagrams
- Personas
- Empathy Maps

These methods help teams organize data, understand users, and identify insights.

### Additional Methods

Other useful analysis and synthesis techniques include:

- Storyboarding
- Story Madlibs
- User Stories
- Reframing
- 2×2 Diagrams
- Customer Journey Maps
- Card Sorting
- Value Proposition Canvas

### Choosing the Right Method

Different methods serve different purposes:

- Communicating research to stakeholders
- Discovering user insights
- Supporting team decision-making
- Exploring business models
- Prioritizing features

## Key Takeaways

- Define Phase: The second stage of design thinking where research insights are synthesized into a **clear problem statement**.
- Problem Statement: Provides focus and direction for the design process.
- Analysis: Breaks complex data into understandable parts.
- Synthesis: Combines insights into meaningful ideas and solutions.
- Analysis and synthesis occur **throughout all design thinking stages**.
- By the end of this phase, designers should have a **user-centered problem statement** that is:
  - Clear
  - Focused
  - Actionable
  - Inspiring for the design team
