---

## What Are “How Might We” Questions?

“How Might We” (HMW) questions are **short, open-ended questions derived from a Point of View (POV) statement**. They help transform a defined design challenge into opportunities for exploring solutions.

In the design thinking process, HMW questions are commonly used **between the Define and Ideate phases**. They take the insights gathered from research and turn them into prompts that encourage creative thinking and brainstorming.

Instead of proposing specific solutions, HMW questions **open up the design space** so teams can explore multiple ideas that address user needs.

## How HMW Questions Reframe a Design Challenge

After creating a POV statement that describes the **user, their need, and the insight behind that need**, designers convert it into one or more HMW questions.

For example, if a POV describes a city resident who needs occasional access to a shared car, possible HMW questions might include:

- How might we give people access to a shared car when they need it?
- How might we help people feel comfortable living without owning a car?
- How might we provide alternative transportation when a car isn’t necessary?

Each question **reframes the challenge in a slightly different way**, helping teams explore a wider range of possible solutions during ideation.

## Why the Phrase “How Might We” Works

The wording of HMW questions is intentionally designed to encourage exploration and collaboration.

- **How** suggests that the team does not yet know the answer and invites exploration.
- **Might** implies that several possible solutions may exist rather than a single correct one.
- **We** emphasizes collaboration and collective problem solving.

Together, these words create a mindset where teams **feel encouraged to explore possibilities rather than commit too quickly to one idea**.

## Best Practices for Creating HMW Questions

To make HMW questions effective in ideation sessions, designers should follow several best practices:

- **Start with a clear POV statement** so the questions remain grounded in user needs.
- **Generate multiple HMW questions** from a single POV to explore different aspects of the problem.
- **Avoid suggesting specific solutions** in the question itself.
- **Balance the scope** so questions are neither too broad nor too narrow.

A good HMW question should allow **many possible answers while still focusing on the user’s problem**.

## Expanding HMW Questions

Once basic HMW questions are created, designers can expand them to stimulate deeper thinking. Variations include:

- “In what ways might we…?”
- “What’s stopping us from…?”
- “What would happen if…?”

Follow-up questions such as **why, who, or when** can further explore ideas and uncover new opportunities for innovation.

## The Role of HMW Questions in Innovation

HMW questions are widely used in innovation practices at organizations such as **IDEO, Google, and Facebook**. The format encourages teams to rethink problems and explore new possibilities rather than jumping directly to solutions.

By reframing a problem through HMW questions, teams can identify new perspectives and generate more creative ideas.

## The Takeaway

“How Might We” questions help designers **reframe a POV statement into open opportunities for ideation**. They encourage teams to explore many possible solutions while staying focused on user needs.

Used effectively, HMW questions become a powerful tool that **bridges the gap between defining a problem and generating innovative solutions.**
