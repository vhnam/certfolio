title: The Key Benefits of Prototyping
---

## Innovation Through Making

### Key Insight from Tom Wujec

- Innovation: Not just thinking, but actively building and experimenting.
- Core idea: Real innovation happens through hands-on creation, not presentations.

Quoted verbatim:

> “One of the big problems in corporate North America and Europe is that people think that innovation is a thinking activity. You don’t innovate through powerpoint. You innovate by building things.”

## Breaking Fixed Thinking Patterns

### Mental Barriers to Innovation

- Habits: Reinforce repetitive ways of thinking.
- Frames of reference: Limit perspective to familiar contexts.
- Comfort zones: Prevent exploration of new ideas.

### Expanding Perspective

- Cross-industry inspiration:
  - Looking at other fields can reveal new approaches.
  - Encourages reframing problems in novel ways.

## Core Strengths of Prototyping

### Making Thinking Tangible

- Tangibility: Turns abstract ideas into visible, testable artifacts.
- Clarity: Helps teams better understand concepts.

### Enabling Collaboration

- Shared understanding:
  - Others can see and engage with your thinking.
- Contribution:
  - Invites feedback and co-creation.

### Supporting Iteration

- Knowledge discovery:
  - Reveals what you know vs. what you don’t know.
- Iterative refinement:
  - Continuous improvement through feedback loops.

### Unlocking Unexpected Solutions

- Fresh perspectives:
  - New participants can offer surprising insights.
- Openness:
  - Being receptive to feedback leads to better outcomes.
