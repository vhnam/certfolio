## Overview

### The Three Pillars

- Desirability:
  - Meets user needs (focus in early stages)
- Feasibility:
  - Can be implemented technically
- Viability:
  - Sustainable business model
- Shift in focus:
  - Early → desirability
  - Later → feasibility + viability

## Ensuring Feasibility

### Definition

- Feasibility:
  - Ability to implement the solution effectively

### Key Considerations

- Capabilities:
  - Skills, technology, resources required
- Distribution channels:
  - How users access and experience the solution

### Distribution Strategy

- Questions to explore:
  - Where and how users interact with the product
  - Why users would adopt it
- Actions:
  - List delivery methods and channels
  - Evaluate pros/cons and impact on UX and business

### Capability Assessment

- Identify needs:
  - Personnel, technology, financial resources
- Evaluate:
  - Internal vs external capabilities
- Decide:
  - Build in-house or outsource

## Ensuring Viability

### Definition

- Viability:
  - Long-term business sustainability

### Value Proposition

- Identify user value:
  - Convenience, safety, efficiency, etc.
- Determine:
  - How much users are willing to pay

### Revenue Sources

- Identify payers:
  - Users, partners, institutions
- Common models:
  - Subscription (membership)
  - Commission
  - Pay-per-use
  - Subsidized pricing
  - Product + refill model

### Stakeholder Incentives

- Analyze stakeholders:
  - Who benefits vs who loses
- Identify:
  - Incentives and disincentives
- Adjust solution:
  - Encourage participation and reduce resistance

## Testing in Real-World Context

### Live Prototypes

- Purpose:
  - Test specific aspects in real environments
- Process:
  - Define what to test
  - Plan logistics
  - Launch and iterate
  - Collect feedback

### Pilot Testing

- Purpose:
  - Test the full system end-to-end
- Focus:
  - Operations, logistics, and scalability
- Approach:
  - Launch with minimal changes
  - Monitor performance and feedback

### When to Use

- Use:
  - Complex (“wicked”) problems
- Skip:
  - Simple solutions (e.g., basic apps)

## Continuous Learning After Launch

### Ongoing Improvement

- Collect:
  - User feedback and stories
- Compare:
  - Before vs after impact

### Analysis vs Synthesis

- Analysis:
  - Gather detailed data
- Synthesis:
  - Identify patterns and insights
- Cycle:
  - Continuous iteration and improvement

## Key Takeaways

### Final Principles

- Balance:
  - Desirability + Feasibility + Viability
- Think systemically:
  - Consider delivery, stakeholders, and resources
- Validate in reality:
  - Use live prototypes and pilots when needed
- Keep learning:
  - Continuously improve post-launch
