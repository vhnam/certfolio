## Overview of the Prototyping Stage

### Purpose of Prototyping

- Prototyping: The stage where ideas are turned into tangible forms to explore, communicate, and test solutions.
- Goal: Improve solutions through iteration and user feedback.
- Key benefit: Makes abstract ideas concrete and easier to discuss.

### Role in Design Thinking

- After Define & Ideate: Prototyping follows problem definition and idea generation.
- Iterative process: Enables continuous refinement based on feedback.
- User-centered: Supports testing with real users to validate assumptions.

## Key Learning Areas

### Importance of Prototypes

- Clarification: Helps teams align on ideas.
- Communication: Makes concepts easier to share with stakeholders.
- Validation: Allows early testing before full development.

### What is a Prototype

- Prototype: A simplified representation of a product or solution used for testing and learning.
- Can vary in detail, fidelity, and purpose.

## Types of Prototypes

### Low-Fidelity vs High-Fidelity

- Low-fidelity prototypes:
  - Simple, quick, inexpensive
  - Focus on ideas and structure
  - Used in early stages
- High-fidelity prototypes:
  - Detailed and realistic
  - Closer to final product
  - Used for refined testing and validation

### When to Use Each

- Low-fidelity:
  - Early ideation
  - Rapid exploration
  - Quick feedback cycles
- High-fidelity:
  - Later stages
  - Usability testing
  - Stakeholder presentations

## Using Prototypes Effectively

### Core Applications

- Empathy:
  - Understand user needs through interaction with prototypes
- Ideation:
  - Explore and evolve ideas through tangible forms
- Testing:
  - Gather feedback and identify issues early

### Common Low-Fidelity Prototypes

- Sketches
- Paper prototypes
- Wireframes
- Storyboards
- Simple interactive mockups

## Best Practices

### Avoiding Pitfalls

- Don’t over-invest too early
- Avoid focusing on aesthetics instead of functionality
- Ensure prototypes answer specific questions

### Ensuring Design Viability

- Desirable: Meets user needs
- Feasible: Technically achievable
- Viable: Business-sustainable
