## Why Prototyping Matters

### Core Purpose

- Prototyping:
  - Helps learn from early mistakes
- Key idea:
  - We rarely get solutions right the first time

## Getting Started the Right Way

### Foundations

- Start with:
  - Deep understanding of users
  - Awareness of technological capabilities
- Focus:
  - Use prototypes to gather feedback and improve ideas

## Types of Prototypes

### Low-Fidelity vs High-Fidelity

- Low-fidelity:
  - Early stages
  - Explore, compare, and shape ideas
- High-fidelity:
  - Later stages
  - Refine and finalize solutions

## How Prototypes Are Used

### For Empathy

- Help teams:
  - Step into users’ shoes
- Enable:
  - Deeper understanding of user needs

### For Ideation

- Make ideas tangible:
  - Easier to compare and combine
- Support:
  - Exploration of multiple solutions

### For Testing

- Validate ideas:
  - Test with users
- Outcome:
  - Gather insights for iteration

## Common Low-Fidelity Methods

### Five Key Types

- Sketches
- Paper prototypes
- Lego prototypes
- Wireframes
- Wizard of Oz prototypes

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

### Six Key Mistakes

- Jumping to the first idea
- Becoming attached to prototypes
- Over-explaining instead of showing
- Prototyping without purpose
- Getting discouraged by failure
- Thinking prototyping wastes time

## Beyond Desirability

### Feasibility and Viability

- Desirable:
  - Meets user needs
- Feasible:
  - Technically achievable
- Viable:
  - Business-sustainable

## What’s Next

### Transition to Testing

- Next stage:
  - Testing prototypes
- Key relationship:
  - Prototype ↔ Test go hand in hand for iteration
