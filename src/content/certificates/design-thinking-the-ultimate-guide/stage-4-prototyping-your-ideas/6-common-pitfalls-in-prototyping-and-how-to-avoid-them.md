---
title: 6 Common Pitfalls in Prototyping and How to Avoid Them
---

## Overview

### Importance of Mindset

- Prototyping:
  - Essential for testing and improving ideas
- Risk:
  - Wrong approach → wasted effort and poor solutions
- Focus:
  - Avoid common misconceptions to maximize effectiveness

## 1. Jumping to the First Good Idea

### Problem

- Early commitment:
  - First idea may seem promising but is often incomplete
- Risk:
  - Investing in a suboptimal solution too soon

### Solution

- Explore broadly:
  - Generate multiple ideas
- Use low-fidelity prototypes:
  - Quickly test and compare alternatives
- Iterate:
  - Combine and refine ideas

## 2. Falling in Love with Prototypes

### Problem

- Endowment effect:
  - Overvaluing what you create
- Risk:
  - Ignoring flaws and negative feedback

### Solution

- Keep prototypes cheap and fast:
  - Use simple materials
- Avoid over-investment:
  - Match fidelity to purpose
- Stay flexible:
  - Be ready to discard ideas

## 3. Over-Explaining Instead of Showing

### Problem

- Too much talking:
  - Ideas remain abstract and untested

### Solution

- Bias toward action:
  - Show ideas through prototypes
- Benefit:
  - Clearer communication and faster feedback

## 4. Prototyping Without Purpose

### Problem

- Lack of focus:
  - Building prototypes without clear goals

### Solution

- Define key questions:
  - Each prototype should test a specific assumption
- Align design:
  - Build only what’s needed to answer that question

## 5. Fear of Failure

### Problem

- Discouragement:
  - Failed prototypes reduce motivation

### Solution

- Reframe failure:
  - Treat as learning opportunities
- Mindset:
  - Failure = insight for better iterations

Quoted verbatim:

> “I have not failed. I’ve just found 10,000 ways that won’t work.”

## 6. Seeing Prototyping as a Waste of Time

### Problem

- Short-term thinking:
  - Belief that prototyping slows progress

### Solution

- Long-term view:
  - Prototyping saves time by avoiding costly mistakes
- Value:
  - Faster path to better solutions

## Key Takeaways

### Principles for Effective Prototyping

- Explore multiple ideas before committing
- Keep prototypes simple and disposable
- Show instead of tell
- Always prototype with a clear purpose
- Embrace failure as learning
- Think long-term: prototype to move faster overall
