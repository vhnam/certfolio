## What is SCAMPER?

### Definitions

- SCAMPER: A structured ideation method that uses seven types of questions to improve or innovate existing products, services, or ideas.
- Purpose: Stimulate creative thinking by viewing problems from different perspectives.
- Approach: Uses “provocation lenses” to challenge assumptions and generate new possibilities.

### SCAMPER Acronym

- Substitute
- Combine
- Adapt
- Modify (Magnify / Minify)
- Put to another use
- Eliminate
- Rearrange (Reverse)

## How to Use SCAMPER

### Process

1. Select subject: Choose an existing product, service, or idea.
2. Apply lenses: Ask questions for each SCAMPER category.
3. Explore aspects: Consider features, processes, users, pricing, and touchpoints.
4. Evaluate ideas: Identify promising or viable solutions.
5. Develop further: Refine and expand selected ideas.

## SCAMPER Techniques Explained

### Substitute

- Definition: Replace parts of a product, process, or system.
- Focus: Changing materials, people, rules, or components.
- Guiding ideas:
  - Replace elements or participants
  - Change materials, processes, or context
  - Alter attributes (color, shape, sound, etc.)

### Combine

- Definition: Merge elements to create new solutions.
- Focus: Creating synergy between components.
- Guiding ideas:
  - Combine features, ideas, or products
  - Merge processes to increase value
  - Reduce costs through integration

### Adapt

- Definition: Adjust or borrow ideas from other contexts.
- Focus: Leveraging existing solutions in new ways.
- Guiding ideas:
  - Borrow from other industries or domains
  - Modify characteristics or context
  - Use historical or existing solutions

### Modify (Magnify / Minify)

- Definition: Change scale, emphasis, or attributes.
- Focus: Enhancing or reducing elements.
- Guiding ideas:
  - Increase size, speed, or impact
  - Add features or value
  - Simplify or reduce components

### Put to Another Use

- Definition: Use the product or idea in new contexts.
- Focus: Expanding use cases and audiences.
- Guiding ideas:
  - Identify new users or markets
  - Reuse in different scenarios
  - Adapt for accessibility or different needs

### Eliminate

- Definition: Remove unnecessary elements.
- Focus: Simplification and efficiency.
- Guiding ideas:
  - Remove non-essential components
  - Reduce cost, time, or effort
  - Streamline processes

### Rearrange (Reverse)

- Definition: Change order, structure, or direction.
- Focus: Reorganizing or reversing processes.
- Guiding ideas:
  - Reorder steps or layout
  - Reverse workflows
  - Change sequence or timing

## Key Takeaways

### Benefits of SCAMPER

- Structured creativity: Provides clear prompts for ideation.
- Lateral thinking: Encourages unconventional approaches.
- Versatility: Applicable to products, services, and processes.

### Best Practices

- Ask questions, not answers: Use prompts to explore possibilities.
- Apply broadly: Consider all aspects (users, features, business model).
- Iterate: Combine outputs from multiple SCAMPER lenses.
