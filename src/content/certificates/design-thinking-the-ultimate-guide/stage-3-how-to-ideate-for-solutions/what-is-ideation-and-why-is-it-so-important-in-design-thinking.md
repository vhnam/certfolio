## What is Ideation?

### Definitions

- Ideation: Process of generating a large number of ideas to solve a defined design problem.
- Goal: Expand the solution space using creativity and innovation.
- Divergent thinking: “Going wide” to explore many possible concepts before narrowing down.

### Key Characteristics

- Critique-free environment: Ideas are not judged during generation.
- Collaboration: Typically involves teams to increase diversity of ideas.
- Creative exploration: Encourages weird, unexpected, and unconventional ideas.

> “Ideation is the mode of the design process in which you concentrate on idea generation… going wide in terms of concepts and outcomes.”

## Role of Ideation in Design Thinking

### Position in the Process

- Third stage: Comes after Empathize and Define.
- Bridge phase: Transitions from understanding problems → creating solutions.
- Iterative use: Can occur at any point when more ideas are needed.

### Relationship with Other Stages

- Empathize: Understand users.
- Define: Frame the problem (POV).
- Ideate: Generate solutions based on insights.

## Principles of Effective Ideation

### Quantity Over Quality

- Core rule: More ideas = higher chance of finding strong solutions.
- Avoid early filtering: First ideas are often obvious or limited.

### Creative Mindset

- Challenge assumptions: Question what seems “obvious”.
- Think beyond norms: Explore unconventional directions.
- Embrace absurdity: Breakthrough ideas often start as unrealistic.

> “If at first the idea is not absurd, then there is no hope for it.” — Albert Einstein

### Divergent Thinking Benefits

![Illustration of the Double Diamond process](../../../../assets/images/design-thinking-the-ultimate-guide/stage-3-how-to-ideate-for-solutions/double-diamond-process.avif)

- Explore beyond obvious solutions
- Increase innovation potential
- Combine team perspectives
- Discover unexpected opportunities
- Create variety and volume of ideas

## Why Ideation Matters

### Core Value

- Solution generation: Central stage where ideas for solving user problems emerge.
- Innovation driver: Where creativity in design thinking is most active.

### Design Insight (Don Norman)

> Key insight:

> - Question the obvious
> - Challenge long-standing assumptions
> - Ask “stupid” questions to unlock breakthroughs

### Purpose of Ideation

- Transition: From problem understanding → solution creation.
- Exploration: Generate a wide range of possibilities before selecting one.

## Ideation Methods

### Common Techniques

- Brainstorm
- Braindump
- Brainwrite
- Brainwalk
- Mindmap
- SCAMPER
- Sketch / Sketchstorm
- Storyboard
- Analogies
- Provocation
- Bodystorm
- Gamestorming
- Co-creation workshops
- Prototype
- Creative pause

### Usage Notes

- Not all methods are required.
- Choose methods based on context and team needs.
- Combine techniques for better results.

## Key Takeaways

### Ideation Mindset

- Generate freely: No criticism during idea generation.
- Encourage participation: Every idea is valid.
- Focus on volume first, refinement later.

### Outcomes

- Wide range of ideas: From practical to unconventional.
- Shortlisted solutions: Selected for prototyping and testing.
