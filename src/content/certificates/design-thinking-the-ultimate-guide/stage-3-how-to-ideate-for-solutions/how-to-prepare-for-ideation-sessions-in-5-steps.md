title: How to Prepare for Ideation Sessions in 5 Steps
---

## Why Preparation Matters

### Purpose of Preparation

- Ideation sessions: Structured activities to generate a large quantity of ideas collaboratively.
- Goal: Enable innovative, user-focused solutions through guided creativity.
- Preparation: Ensures sessions are productive, engaging, and psychologically safe.

### Key Outcomes

- Creative environment: Encourages open, non-judgmental idea sharing.
- Equal participation: Removes hierarchy to unlock diverse perspectives.
- Focused ideation: Keeps ideas aligned with user needs and problem statement.

## Creating the Right Environment

### Essential Conditions

- Safe space: No criticism, negativity, or fear of judgment.
- Equality: All participants contribute equally regardless of role.
- Comfort: Physical and mental ease to encourage participation.
- Facilitation: A facilitator guides flow, captures ideas, and introduces constraints when needed.

### Session Setup

- Clear goals: Define objectives and expected outcomes.
- Time constraints: Set targets (e.g., number of ideas, time-boxed activities).
- Positive mindset: Encourage openness; avoid negative language (e.g., ban “no”).

## 5 Steps to Prepare for Ideation

### 1. Define a User-Centered Problem (POV)

- POV (Point of View): A clear, actionable problem statement based on user insights.
- Focus: Narrow problem scope to generate more relevant and higher-quality ideas.
- Role: Acts as a guiding anchor throughout ideation.

### 2. Use “How Might We” Questions

- HMW Questions: Reframe problems into open-ended opportunities.
- Purpose: Encourage solution-oriented thinking without biasing outcomes.
- Benefit: Balances focus with creative freedom.

> “How Might We…?” suggests possibility without prescribing solutions.

### 3. Change the Environment

- New setting: Use unfamiliar spaces to stimulate fresh thinking.
- Effect: Encourages out-of-the-box ideas and breaks routine patterns.

### 4. Create a Relaxed Atmosphere

- Comfort: Helps participants feel safe sharing unconventional ideas.
- Icebreakers: Warm-up activities to boost collaboration and creativity.
- Emotional state: Relaxed participants generate better ideas.

### 5. Encourage Creative Behaviors

#### Required Characteristics

- Adapt: Shift thinking as new inputs emerge.
- Connect: Link unrelated ideas to form new concepts.
- Disrupt: Challenge assumptions and norms.
- Flip: Turn dead ends into opportunities.
- Imagine: Visualize new possibilities and scenarios.
- Experiment: Explore ideas and take risks.
- Recognize patterns: Identify meaningful connections.
- Stay curious: Ask bold, even “silly” questions.

## Choosing Ideation Techniques

### Selection Principles

- Match techniques: Align with type of ideas needed.
- Team fit: Consider experience, energy, and creativity levels.
- Flexibility: Combine methods for better outcomes.

### Key Reminder

- Ideation process: Combines rational thinking with imagination.
- Best results: Come from engaged, relaxed, and open-minded teams.

## Key Takeaways

### Preparation Essentials

- Build on Empathize & Define: Use insights and artifacts (personas, scenarios).
- Foster openness: Create trust and psychological safety.
- Enable creativity: Use environment, facilitation, and structure.

### Success Factors

- Encourage participation: Everyone contributes ideas.
- Focus on quantity: Generate broadly before filtering.
- Maintain energy: Keep sessions fun, engaging, and dynamic.
