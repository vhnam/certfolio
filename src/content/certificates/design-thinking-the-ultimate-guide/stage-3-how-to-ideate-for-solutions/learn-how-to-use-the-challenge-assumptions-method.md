---

## What is Challenging Assumptions?

### Definitions

- Challenging Assumptions: An ideation technique that questions accepted beliefs about how things work.
- Purpose: Break fixed thinking patterns to unlock innovative solutions.
- Approach: Treat “truths” as perspectives that can be reinterpreted.

### Key Idea

- Assumptions: Often invisible constraints that limit innovation.
- Innovation: Comes from questioning what is considered “obvious” or “normal”.

## Why Challenge Assumptions?

### Role in Innovation

- Disrupt status quo: Enables radically different solutions.
- Expand possibilities: Opens new ways to achieve the same goals.
- Overcome stagnation: Helps teams move beyond habitual thinking.

### Design Thinking Context

- Supports ideation: Works alongside methods like SCAMPER and Worst Possible Idea.
- User-centered: Keeps focus on user needs while exploring alternatives.
- Encourages curiosity: Promotes asking “stupid” questions to gain insights.

> Key insight:

> - Question the obvious
> - Challenge long-standing beliefs
> - Creativity comes from rethinking fundamentals

## When to Use It

### Ideal Situations

- Stuck in thinking: When ideas are repetitive or limited.
- Idea fatigue: When ideation sessions lose momentum.
- Early exploration: To reframe problems and discover new directions.

### Trigger Questions

- Are these assumptions really necessary?
- Are they based on facts or habits?
- What if the opposite were true?

## How to Apply the Method

### Process

1. List assumptions: Identify beliefs about product, service, or problem.
2. Challenge assumptions:
   - Question their validity
   - Imagine alternatives or opposites
3. Reframe possibilities: Explore new ways of thinking.
4. Make it real: Find ways to turn new perspectives into solutions.

### Examples of Assumptions

- Constraints: Time, cost, or feasibility limits.
- Rules: “This is how it must be done.”
- User beliefs: What people need or expect.

### Guiding Questions

- How could this not be true?
- What if we did this twice as well in half the time?
- What if we removed this constraint entirely?

## Key Takeaways

### Mindset Shift

- From fixed → flexible thinking
- From certainty → exploration
- From assumptions → possibilities

### Benefits

- Unlocks new perspectives
- Drives disruptive innovation
- Helps uncover overlooked opportunities

### Core Principle

- No single “truth”: Every idea is shaped by perspective and can be reimagined.
