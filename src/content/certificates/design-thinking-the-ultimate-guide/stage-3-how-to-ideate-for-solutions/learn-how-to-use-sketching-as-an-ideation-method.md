## Role of Sketching in Design

### Definition

- Sketching: A form of drawing used to propose, explore, refine, and communicate design ideas.
- Sketchnoting: A method combining visuals and text to capture and retain information.

### Key Benefits

- Fast & cheap: Easy to produce, iterate, and discard.
- Improves communication: Reduces misunderstandings vs verbal/written explanations.
- Enhances thinking: Engages visual, analytical, and listening skills.
- Encourages exploration: Frees designers from software constraints.
- Invites feedback: Rough sketches feel unfinished, making stakeholders more open to critique.

### Paper Before Pixels

- Paper is low-cost and سريع for ideation.
- Software tools:
  - Limit creativity to predefined patterns.
  - Encourage premature refinement.
- Sketches:
  - Are free-form and exploratory.
  - Focus on ideas rather than execution.

## Sketches vs Prototypes

### Differences

- Sketches:
  - Exploratory, rough, and non-committal.
  - Used early in the design process.
- Prototypes:
  - Refined, testable, and detailed.
  - Used later for validation.

### Relationship

- Complementary, not interchangeable.
- Sketches → identify the right design.
- Prototypes → refine and validate the design.

### Design Funnel

1. Early stage: Generate many ideas via sketches (low cost).
2. Mid stage: Narrow down to promising solutions.
3. Late stage: Use prototypes and usability testing (high cost).

- Key insight: Cost of change increases over time, so explore widely early.

## Exploring and Refining Designs

### Getting the Right Design

- Generate multiple ideas simultaneously.
- Avoid focusing on a single solution too early.
- Compare alternatives to avoid biased feedback.

### Getting the Design Right

- Iterate on the selected design.
- Apply usability engineering:
  - Test
  - Evaluate
  - Refine
- Goal: Optimize without major structural changes.

## Ideas, Not Art

### Mindset

- Focus: Communicating ideas, not artistic quality.
- Sketching removes the pressure of “drawing well”.

### Five Basic Elements of Drawing

- Square (and rectangle)
- Triangle
- Circle (and oval)
- Line (straight or curved)
- Dot
- Insight: All drawings can be built from these simple elements.

### Principles

- Use minimal elements to convey meaning.
- Combine words + visuals for clarity.
- Stay 2D when possible to reduce complexity.
- Provide “just enough” detail for interpretation.

## Characteristics of Good Sketches

- Quick: Created rapidly.
- Timely: Produced when needed.
- Disposable: Easy to discard.
- Plentiful: Many variations explored.
- Minimalist: Focus on one idea at a time.

## Enhancing Sketch Communication

### Annotations

- Labels and explanations tied to elements.
- Use arrows, numbering, or proximity.
- Prefer contrasting colors.

### Arrows

- Show:
  - Flow
  - Movement
  - Sequences
  - Interactions

### Notes

- Provide deeper context:
  - Explain actions
  - Capture ideas
  - Record issues
  - Document thought process

## Building Sketching Skills

### Practice Methods

#### Scribble Sketching

- Capture essence quickly.
- Ignore non-essential details.
- Add minimal annotations.

#### Sampling with Cameras

- Take photos/videos of:
  - Inspiring designs
  - Frustrating experiences
  - Real-world interactions

### Design Memory Bank

- Store:
  - Successful designs → inspiration
  - Failed designs → critical thinking
- Use collected references to remix ideas.

## Visual Library

### Concept

- Repeated drawing builds mental “icon library”.
- Enables faster sketching during real work.

### Techniques

- Practice common objects repeatedly.
- Use references (e.g., icon libraries, images).
- Create personal symbol sets for:
  - Ideas
  - Alerts
  - Actions
  - Deadlines

### Outcome

- Faster ideation
- Better communication
- Increased confidence in sketching

## The Take Away

### Core Principles

- Sketch first in any design problem.
- Explore broadly before committing.
- Focus on ideas, not polish.
- Use sketches to:
  - Think
  - Communicate
  - Collaborate

### Workflow

1. Sketch: Generate many ideas.
2. Select: Identify best direction.
3. Prototype: Build and test.
4. Refine: Iterate toward optimal solution.

- Key mindset: Sketch, iterate, and explore continuously.
