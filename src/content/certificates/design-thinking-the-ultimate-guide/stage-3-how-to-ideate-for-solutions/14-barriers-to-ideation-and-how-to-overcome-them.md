# Ideation Barriers in Design Thinking

## Overview of Ideation

### Definition

- Ideation: The process of generating, developing, and communicating ideas.
- Role: A core activity in Design Thinking focused on innovation and problem-solving.

### Key Insight

- Design Thinking aims to remove barriers that block creativity and innovation.

## The 14 Barriers to Ideation

### 1. Inexperienced Facilitation

- Lack of facilitation skills leads to poor structure and weak outcomes.
- Requires understanding of:
  - Group dynamics
  - Problem focus
  - Process guidance

### 2. Uninitiated Team

- New participants may feel confused or uncomfortable.
- Requires:
  - Warm-up exercises
  - Clear explanation of ideation process

### 3. Unfriendly Space

- Physical environment affects creativity.
- Avoid:
  - Rigid layouts
  - Hierarchical setups
- Ensure:
  - Open, flexible, well-lit space

### 4. Unclear Goals

- Missing clear objectives or Point of View.
- Leads to unfocused ideation.
- Goals should come from Empathize and Define phases.

### 5. Egos and Hierarchy

- Authority suppresses participation.
- Creativity requires equality among team members.

### 6. Closed-Mindedness

- Rejecting others’ ideas limits innovation.
- Best ideas often come from combining perspectives.

### 7. Limiting Ideas

- Settling too early on “good enough” ideas.
- Reduces diversity and exploration.

### 8. Early Judgment and Devil's Advocate

- Criticism too early blocks idea generation.
- Judgment should be deferred in early stages.

### 9. Lack of Imagination or Being too Serious/Boring

- Over-reliance on logic reduces creativity.
- Fear of unconventional ideas limits exploration.

### 10. Old Pattern Thinking

- Resistance to change.
- Overdependence on past solutions.

### 11. “Man-with-a-Hammer” Syndrome

- Applying familiar solutions to all problems.
- Limits innovation and exploration.

### 12. Groupthink

![Illustration showing how a group can fall into group think and ignore other points of view.](../../../../assets/images/design-thinking-the-ultimate-guide/stage-3-how-to-ideate-for-solutions/groupthink.avif)

- Conforming to group norms.
- Suppresses diverse thinking.

### 13. Cognitive Bias

- Automatic judgments based on past experiences.
- Influences decision-making negatively.

### 14. Idea Killers

![Poster of Idea Killers (phrases).](../../../../assets/images/design-thinking-the-ultimate-guide/stage-3-how-to-ideate-for-solutions/idea-killers.avif)

#### Examples

- Yes, but...
- It’s not possible.
- We don’t have time/budget.
- That’s not realistic.
- Management won’t agree.

#### Impact

- Shut down ideas prematurely.
- Reduce team confidence and creativity.

## Techniques to Overcome Barriers

### Encourage Open Ideation

- Defer judgment.
- Promote equal participation.
- Build on others’ ideas.

### Structured Methods

#### Brainwriting

1. Write ideas individually.
2. Pass ideas to others.
3. Expand collaboratively.
4. Review as a group.

#### Worst Possible Idea

- Generate intentionally bad ideas.
- Transform into useful insights.

### Facilitation Best Practices

- Prepare team before sessions.
- Use warm-ups.
- Manage dynamics actively.
- Create safe, creative environments.

## The Take Away

### Core Principles

- Identify and remove ideation barriers.
- Encourage quantity and diversity of ideas.
- Maintain a judgment-free environment.

### Success Factors

- Skilled facilitation
- Open-minded team
- Supportive environment
- Continuous practice
- Outcome: Stronger ideation and innovation results.
