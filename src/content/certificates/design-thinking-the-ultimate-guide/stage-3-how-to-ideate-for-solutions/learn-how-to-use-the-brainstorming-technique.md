## Brainstorm: Core Group Ideation Method

### Definitions

- Brainstorm: Group activity to generate ideas freely without judgment.
- Goal: Explore many possibilities and spark creativity through collaboration.

### Key Characteristics

- Free thinking: No criticism during idea generation.
- Collaboration: Participants build on each other’s ideas.
- Idea capture: Use notes, sketches, or boards to document ideas.

### Basic Process

1. Define goal: Set a clear problem or question.
2. Gather diverse team: Include varied perspectives.
3. Generate ideas: Encourage free and open thinking.
4. Build on ideas: Combine and expand concepts.
5. Document & evaluate: Capture all ideas for later analysis.

## Rules for Effective Brainstorming

### Core Principles

1. Set time limit: Typically 15–60 minutes of focused ideation.
2. Stay focused: Use POV, goals, or “How Might We” questions.
3. Defer judgment: No criticism (verbal or non-verbal).
4. Encourage wild ideas: Unconventional thinking leads to breakthroughs.
5. Aim for quantity: More ideas increase chance of strong solutions.
6. Build on ideas: Use “and” instead of “but”.
7. Be visual: Sketch or use sticky notes to express ideas.
8. One conversation at a time: Maintain clarity and focus.

### Mindset

- Safe space: Participants feel free to share anything.
- Playfulness: Encourage curiosity and experimentation.

## Individual vs Group Ideation

### Approach

- Combine modes: Alternate between individual and group sessions.
- Benefit: Balances deep thinking with collaborative expansion.

### Methods Overview

- Brainstorm: Group discussion.
- Braindump: Individual idea generation.
- Brainwrite: Silent collaborative writing.
- Brainwalk: Movement-based collaboration.

## Braindump: Individual Idea Generation

### Definitions

- Braindump: Silent, individual process to quickly externalize ideas.

### Purpose

- Clear mental clutter: Free cognitive space.
- Empower individuals: Gives voice to quieter participants.

### Process

1. Brief participants: Share problem, goals, insights.
2. Write ideas individually: One idea per note.
3. Timebox: 3–10 minutes.
4. Share ideas: Present without discussion.
5. Cluster ideas: Group similar concepts.
6. Select ideas: Use methods like voting or categorization.

## Brainwrite: Silent Collaborative Ideation

### Definitions

- Brainwrite: Participants write ideas and pass them to others to build upon.

### Key Characteristics

- Silent collaboration: No verbal discussion during idea generation.
- Equal participation: Reduces dominance of outspoken individuals.
- Iterative building: Ideas evolve through multiple contributors.

### Process

1. Write ideas: Individually for a few minutes.
2. Pass cards: Share ideas in cycles (3–10 rounds).
3. Build on ideas: Expand others’ concepts.
4. Present results: Share final ideas with group.
5. Select ideas: Evaluate and refine.

## Brainwalk: Movement-Based Ideation

### Definitions

- Brainwalk: Similar to brainwriting but involves physical movement between idea stations.

### Benefits

- Energy boost: Keeps participants engaged and active.
- Fresh perspectives: Movement encourages new thinking.
- Avoid fixation: Prevents getting stuck on one idea path.

### Key Characteristics

- Rotate positions: Move between ideas instead of passing notes.
- Dynamic flow: Encourages rapid idea switching and iteration.

## Key Takeaways

### Choosing Techniques

- Mix methods: Combine brainstorming with braindump, brainwrite, and brainwalk.
- Adapt to context: Choose based on team, goals, and energy levels.

### Success Factors

- Facilitation: Skilled guidance improves outcomes.
- Safe environment: No judgment encourages creativity.
- Structured freedom: Balance rules with open thinking.
