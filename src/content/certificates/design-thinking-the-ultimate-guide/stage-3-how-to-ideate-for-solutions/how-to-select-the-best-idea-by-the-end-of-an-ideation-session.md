title: How to Select the Best Idea by the End of an Ideation Session
---

## Convergent Stage

### Definition

- Convergent stage: Phase where ideas are evaluated, compared, ranked, clustered, and reduced to a few strong options.
- Goal: Identify the most promising ideas or combinations of ideas.

### Key Principles

- Stop ideation when reaching critical mass of ideas.
- Focus on pruning rather than generating more.
- Keep unused ideas for future sessions.
- Aim to identify potential “winning” ideas.

## Idea Selection Methods

### Dot Voting

#### Process

1. Write each idea on a separate sticky note.
2. Give participants 3–4 votes each.
3. Participants place dots on preferred ideas.
4. Optionally use colors for different criteria (like/dislike).
5. Count votes to shortlist ideas.

#### Benefits

- Equal participation.
- Quick prioritization.
- Flexible voting criteria.

### Four Categories Method

![Illustration of four categories method.](../../../../assets/images/design-thinking-the-ultimate-guide/stage-3-how-to-ideate-for-solutions/4-categories-method.avif)

#### Categories

- Rational choice: Most practical solution.
- Most likely to delight: High user appeal.
- Darling: Team’s favorite idea.
- Long shot: Risky but innovative idea.

#### Usage

- Select 1–2 ideas per category.
- Ensures balance between feasibility and innovation.

### Bingo Selection

![Illustration of Bingo Selection showing different types of prototypes.](../../../../assets/images/design-thinking-the-ultimate-guide/stage-3-how-to-ideate-for-solutions/bingo-selection.avif)

#### Concept

- Group ideas based on different formats or applications.

#### Examples

- Physical prototype
- Digital prototype
- Experience prototype

#### Benefit

- Encourages diversity in implementation approaches.

### Idea Affinity Diagram

#### Process

1. Collect all ideas.
2. Group similar ideas together.
3. Identify patterns and themes.
4. Remove duplicates.

#### Outcome

- Clear idea clusters.
- Easier identification of strong themes.

## Idea Selection Criteria

### Evaluation Questions

- User needs: Does it solve a real problem?
- Goals: Does it meet project objectives?
- POV fit: Aligns with problem statement?
- HMW alignment: Answers “How Might We” questions?
- Innovation: Adds new value?
- Feasibility:
  - Budget available?
  - Technology feasible?
  - Resources sufficient?
- Business viability:
  - Can it be approved?
  - Can it be implemented?

### Purpose

- Ensure ideas are relevant, feasible, and valuable.

## Evaluation Frameworks

### Now Wow How Matrix

![ Illustration of a How, Now, Wow 2 by 2 matrix.](../../../../assets/images/design-thinking-the-ultimate-guide/stage-3-how-to-ideate-for-solutions/now-wow-how-matrix.avif)

#### Categories

- Now: Easy to implement, low innovation.
- Wow: Innovative and feasible.
- How: Innovative but hard to implement.

#### Axes

- X-axis: Innovation
- Y-axis: Implementation difficulty

#### Usage

- Place ideas on matrix.
- Prioritize “Wow” ideas.

### Six Thinking Hats

#### Thinking Modes

- White Hat: Facts and data.
- Yellow Hat: Benefits and optimism.
- Black Hat: Risks and critique.
- Red Hat: Emotions and intuition.
- Green Hat: Creativity and alternatives.
- Blue Hat: Process control.

#### Purpose

- Evaluate ideas from multiple perspectives.
- Reduce bias and improve decision quality.

## Lean Validation Approach

### Hypotheses

- Customer hypothesis: Who is the user?
- Problem hypothesis: What is the problem?
- Solution hypothesis: How to solve it?

### Process

1. Test assumptions early.
2. Validate or invalidate hypotheses.
3. Pivot if necessary.

### Goal

- Build solutions based on real needs and evidence.

## From Ideas to Prototyping

### Next Steps

1. Select strongest ideas.
2. Ensure diversity among selected concepts.
3. Move to prototyping.
4. Test and validate solutions.

### Principle

- Avoid selecting similar ideas only.
- Explore multiple directions through prototypes.

## The Take Away

### Core Workflow

1. Generate ideas (divergent phase).
2. Select ideas (convergent phase).
3. Prototype selected ideas.
4. Test and refine.

### Key Insights

- Use structured methods to filter ideas.
- Balance feasibility and innovation.
- Evaluate ideas from multiple perspectives.
- Move quickly from selection to testing.
- Outcome: Better decision-making and stronger design solutions.
