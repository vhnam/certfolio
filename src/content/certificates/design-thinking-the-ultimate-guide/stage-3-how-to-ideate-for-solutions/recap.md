## Lesson Goals

### Objective

- Ideation: Generate many possible solutions to a defined POV (Point of View) problem.
- Outcome: Select a few promising ideas to move into the Prototype phase.

## How to Ideate Effectively

### Key Principles

- Quantity over quality: Explore as many ideas as possible.
- Safe environment: Encourage openness and remove fear of judgment.
- Diverse methods: Use multiple ideation techniques beyond brainstorming.

### Methods & Tools

- Brainstorming: Most common ideation technique.
- Alternative methods: Provide different types of insights and outcomes.
- Convergence tools:
  - Dot voting: Prioritize ideas.
  - Bingo selection: Explore different implementation forms.

### Transition to Prototyping

- Sketching:
  - Use storyboards to visualize ideas.
  - Helps prepare for prototyping stage.

## Practical Application

### Real-World Practice

- Activities:
  - Open-ended questions
  - Discussion forums
  - Portfolio exercises

### Benefits

- Apply theory to real scenarios.
- Build case studies for portfolio.
- Develop practical design experience.

## Next Stage: Prototype

### Focus

- Learn prototyping techniques.
- Visualize and refine selected ideas.
- Test solutions with users.

### Role in Design Thinking

- Prototype: Fourth stage of Design Thinking.
- Bridges ideation and testing.

## The Take Away

### Core Insights

- Generate many ideas before selecting.
- Use structured methods to converge.
- Practice ideation in real contexts.
- Prepare ideas for prototyping and testing.
