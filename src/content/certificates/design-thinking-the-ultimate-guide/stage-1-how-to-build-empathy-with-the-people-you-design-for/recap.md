## Understanding the Empathize Stage

### The Role of Empathy in Design Thinking

- Empathy: The ability to understand users by seeing the world through their perspective.
- Empathize Stage: The first stage of the design thinking process.

This stage focuses on understanding:

- What users experience
- How they feel and behave
- Why they act the way they do

Empathy helps designers set aside personal assumptions and focus on **real user needs**.

## Why Empathy Is Important

### Benefits of Empathic Design

Designers who build strong empathy for their users can:

- Understand users’ needs, motivations, and behaviors.
- Identify real problems users face.
- Create more meaningful and effective design solutions.

Empathy ensures that design decisions are **human-centered rather than assumption-driven**.

## Techniques for Building Empathy

### Research Methods for Understanding Users

Designers can use various techniques to build empathy, including:

- User observations
- User interviews
- The What–How–Why method
- Story share-and-capture
- Other qualitative research methods

These techniques help designers gather insights about users’ experiences and contexts.

## Applying Empathy in Practice

### Real-World Design Exercises

Practical exercises help designers apply empathy techniques in real projects.

Benefits of these exercises include:

- Developing hands-on research skills
- Understanding user needs through real experiences
- Creating materials that can become **portfolio case studies**

This practical work strengthens both design skills and professional portfolios.

## The Next Stage: Define

### From Empathy to Problem Definition

After gathering insights during the Empathize stage, the next step is the **Define stage**.

In this stage designers:

- Analyze and synthesize research insights.
- Identify patterns in user needs and behaviors.
- Define the core problems that need solving.

This step prepares the foundation for generating solutions in later stages of the design thinking process.
