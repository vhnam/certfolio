## Introduction to Empathy in Design Thinking

### Empathy in Design

- Empathy: The ability to understand users’ perspectives, feelings, motivations, and behaviors.
- Empathic design: A design approach that begins by deeply understanding the people you design for.

In the design thinking process, the **Empathize stage** is the starting point. Designers focus on understanding users before attempting to solve problems. This ensures that solutions address real needs rather than assumptions.

## Why Empathy Matters in Design

### Importance of Empathy

- Helps designers see the world from the user’s perspective.
- Reveals users’ real needs, motivations, and behaviors.
- Prevents designers from relying on assumptions.
- Leads to more meaningful and user-centered solutions.
- Strengthens the overall design thinking process.

Empathy is essential because successful products and services are built on **a deep understanding of the people who use them**.

## Techniques for Building Empathy

### Empathy Research Methods

Designers can use various research techniques to understand their users better.

Common empathy-building techniques include:

- User interviews
- Cultural probes
- Observational research
- Other qualitative research methods

These techniques help designers gather insights about:

- User needs
- User motivations
- User behaviors
- Context in which users interact with products

### Becoming a More Empathic Designer

Designers improve empathy by:

- Actively listening to users.
- Observing real user behavior.
- Asking thoughtful questions.
- Interpreting research insights carefully.

Effective empathy research also involves learning how to **generate meaningful insights from collected data**.

## Applying Empathy in Practice

### Portfolio Project Exercise

This lesson includes the first **Build Your Portfolio** exercise.

Students will:

1. Select a design brief or a real project.
2. Apply empathy-building techniques learned in the lesson.
3. Conduct research with real users.
4. Use the insights to better understand user problems.

This practical exercise helps transform theoretical empathy techniques into **real design practice**.
