---
title: Help Users Skim Contents with Titled Sections
---

## Design Problem

### Problem Description

- Information overload: Large amounts of content overwhelm users.
- High scanning effort: Users must read extensively to find relevant information.
- Lack of structure: Content without clear divisions is hard to navigate.

### Key Insight

- Users prefer scanning over reading when searching for information.

## Design Solution

### Definitions

- Titled sections: Content divided into sections, each labeled with a clear, descriptive title.
- Information scent: The clarity of a title in indicating what content lies beneath.
- Content chunks: Logical groupings of related information.

### Core Principles

- Break content into manageable chunks.
- Use clear titles to signal meaning بسرعة.
- Guide users visually through the interface.

## Why Use Titled Sections

### Benefits

- Faster scanning: Users quickly locate relevant sections.
- Reduced cognitive load: Less need to read unnecessary content.
- Improved navigation: Clear structure enhances usability.

### Visual Strategy

- Use larger/bold text for titles to attract attention.
- Leverage contrast and spacing to separate sections.
- Place titles close to their content for clarity.

## Implementation Guidelines

### Process

1. Divide content: Group information into logical chunks.
2. Assign titles: Use concise and descriptive labels.
3. Style titles: Define font, size, color, and visibility.
4. Position properly: Place titles near corresponding content.
5. Use spacing/borders: Separate sections visually when needed.

### Layout Considerations

- Keep titles short (often 1–2 words for navigation).
- Ensure accessibility (e.g., color contrast).
- Use whitespace to improve readability.

## Potential Problems

### Challenges

- Weak titles: Poor wording reduces clarity and usability.
- Ambiguity: Users may not know where to find information.
- Overload persists: Titles alone may not be enough for complex content.

### Mitigation

- Ensure strong information scent in titles.
- Combine with other patterns (tabs, accordions) if needed.
- Avoid misleading or exaggerated headings.

## Key Takeaways

- Titled sections help users scan and navigate large content efficiently.
- Clear, concise titles are critical for usability.
- Visual hierarchy (size, contrast, spacing) enhances effectiveness.
- Strong information scent ensures users find what they need quickly.
