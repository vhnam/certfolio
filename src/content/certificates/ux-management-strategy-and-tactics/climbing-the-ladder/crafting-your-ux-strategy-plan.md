---
title: Crafting Your UX Strategy Plan
---

A UX strategy plan defines how an organization will grow its UX capabilities while aligning UX activities with business goals. Instead of jumping directly into UX practices like usability testing or design improvements, organizations should first establish a clear business case, understand their current UX processes, and define a roadmap for future growth.

## Build the Business Case for UX

The first step in creating a UX strategy is demonstrating the **business value of UX**.

Key actions include:

- Clarify what the **return on investment (ROI)** of UX currently is and what it could become.
- Create a **cost–benefit analysis framework** and standard template for estimating UX project costs and benefits.
- Identify **Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)** that reflect user experience performance.
- Start with **small measurable metrics** rather than trying to measure everything at once.
- **Share and review UX metrics regularly**, ideally on a monthly basis.

Typical UX metrics might include:

- Success rate in usability testing
- Conversion or engagement metrics
- Task completion rates

Tracking and communicating these metrics ensures that UX performance remains visible to stakeholders and supports ongoing investment in UX.

## Map the Current State of UX

Before defining improvements, organizations must understand **how UX currently functions**.

This involves assessing the organization’s existing UX environment, including:

- Current **usability and UX practices**
- The **product development lifecycle**
- Team **management structures and staffing**
- The organization’s **UX maturity level**
- **UX readiness and Agile experience**
- Available **UX resources, tools, and techniques**

The purpose of this assessment is to answer a simple but important question:

**How are things actually done today?**

Mapping the current state helps reveal strengths, gaps, and opportunities for improvement.

## Customize the Strategy for Your Organization

A UX strategy should never be copied from another company or framework.

Every organization operates within a unique environment shaped by:

- Leadership and company culture
- Founders and executive priorities
- Industry and market conditions
- Organizational structure and internal processes

For this reason, a UX strategy must be **tailored specifically to the organization’s context**, rather than replicating practices from previous companies or external frameworks.

## Define the Ideal State and UX Roadmap

After understanding the current state, organizations can define the **ideal future state of UX**.

This is typically expressed as a **UX maturity roadmap**, which provides detailed guidance for developing UX competency.

The roadmap may include:

- Required **UX roles and staffing plans**
- How UX should be **positioned within the organization**
- Specific **capabilities and competencies** needed for UX growth
- Clear **next steps and action plans** for implementing UX improvements

This roadmap defines where the organization wants to go and how it plans to get there.

## From Strategy to Action

A well-crafted UX strategy ultimately connects three key elements:

1. **Business value** – demonstrating how UX contributes to measurable outcomes
2. **Current state understanding** – analyzing how UX work is currently performed
3. **Future roadmap** – defining the steps needed to grow UX maturity

By aligning UX initiatives with business objectives and organizational realities, companies can build a sustainable UX program that evolves with the organization over time.
