---
title: "Interview: The Importance of Balancing Research and Design"
---

Many organizations mistakenly believe that UX is primarily about design. However, effective UX is fundamentally grounded in **research-driven insights about user behavior**. To achieve strong UX maturity, organizations must balance research and design and integrate both across product development teams.

## UX Is More Than Visual Design

UX is often misunderstood as a purely creative or visual task similar to graphic design. In reality, UX is **a research-based discipline** that focuses on understanding how users behave and interact with products.

Key points:

- UX design decisions should be **informed by research**, not personal opinions.
- Research helps uncover **real user behaviors and needs**.
- UX research should be recognized as **a fundamental role within the UX team**.

Treating UX purely as design risks creating solutions based on assumptions rather than real user insights.

## Embed UX Research Across Teams

UX research is most effective when it is **integrated throughout the organization**, rather than isolated within a separate department.

Instead of placing research in a centralized team that validates decisions after the fact, organizations should:

- Integrate research into **product development processes**
- Involve researchers in **business and product decisions**
- Ensure insights influence **requirements, design, and development**

This approach allows UX research to **inform decisions continuously**, rather than acting as a final checkpoint.

## The Limitations of Outsourcing or Marketing Research

Some organizations attempt to rely on **external research agencies or internal marketing teams** for user insights.

However, these approaches have limitations:

### Outsourced Research

- Can be useful for early UX efforts.
- May lack continuous involvement with product teams.
- Does not always support long-term UX maturity.

### Marketing Research

Marketing research often focuses on:

- Market validation
- Customer opinions
- Brand perception

UX research, by contrast, focuses on **behavioral insights**, such as how people actually use products and interact with interfaces.

Because of this difference, marketing insights alone are not sufficient for designing effective user experiences.

## Behavioral Research Drives Better Design

UX research emphasizes **behavioral observation**, rather than simply collecting user opinions.

Examples include:

- Watching users interact with an interface
- Observing workflows in real environments
- Studying how people complete tasks

These behavioral insights provide designers with a clearer understanding of **where usability problems occur and how to improve the experience**.

Design solutions become clearer and more effective when designers have direct access to these insights.

## Collaboration Between Researchers and Designers

Separating research and design into isolated roles can create communication gaps.

More effective UX processes encourage:

- Close collaboration between **UX researchers and designers**
- Shared responsibility for understanding user needs
- Continuous interaction throughout the design process

In many cases, designers themselves should participate in research activities.

## Direct User Exposure Builds Empathy

One of the most powerful ways to improve UX design is to involve designers directly in research.

When designers observe users firsthand—whether in usability labs or field studies—they gain:

- Stronger empathy for users
- Better understanding of real-world contexts
- Clearer insights into usability problems

Experiencing user struggles directly often leads to **faster and more effective design solutions**.

## Research as an Organizational Process

For UX to deliver strong results, research must become **a shared organizational process**, not a specialized activity performed by a small group.

This requires:

- Training teams to understand the value of UX research
- Encouraging cross-team participation in research activities
- Integrating research insights into decision-making processes

When research becomes embedded across teams, organizations can achieve higher UX maturity and deliver better user experiences.

## Research-Driven Design Improves ROI

Combining research with design also produces measurable business benefits.

For example, studies have shown that organizations that:

- Conduct field research
- Develop personas based on real user data

can achieve significantly higher returns from redesign efforts.

By grounding design decisions in research insights, organizations create **more effective products and stronger business outcomes**.
