---
title: Tactics for Encouraging UX Growth
---

## UX Growth Starts with Evangelization

### Education as a Core Responsibility

- ~90% of UX work is **evangelization and education**
- Without continuous education:
  - UX remains misunderstood
  - No visible progress in maturity

### Breaking UX Isolation

- Anti-pattern:
  - UX working alone, disconnected from the organization
- Solution:
  - Actively engage teams
  - Bring UX into shared conversations and activities

## Embedding UX Across the Organization

### Participation Over Documentation

- Key tactic: **Attendance, attendance, attendance**
- Involve all roles:
  - Developers
  - Product managers
  - Executives
- Activities:
  - User testing sessions
  - Field studies
  - Design studios
  - Wireframe reviews

### Power of Direct Exposure

- Live user interaction:
  - Creates empathy
  - Drives mindset shifts
- Recordings help, but:
  - Live experience is more impactful

## UX as a Strategic Partner

### Supporting Product Owners

- UX role:
  - Guide Product Owners with user insights
  - Bring empathy into decision-making
- Especially important when:
  - POs lack time to engage with users

### Cross-Functional Collaboration

- Example model:
  - “Two in a box” → designer + developer pairing
- Outcome:
  - Better alignment
  - Faster, more informed decisions

## Moving Beyond UI Thinking

### Hire for Strategy, Not Just UI

- Anti-pattern:
  - Hiring for visual polish only
- Risk:
  - Beautiful UI solving wrong problems

### UX ≠ UI

- Misconceptions:
  - “Make it look usable”
  - “Make it like iPhone”
- Reality:
  - UX requires:
    - Research
    - Validation
    - Clear definition of success

### Quantifying UX

- Define:
  - What “good UX” actually means
- Use:
  - Measurable criteria
  - Usability benchmarks

## Building Reproducible UX Excellence

### From One-Off Design to System

- Goal:
  - Consistency and scalability
- Focus:
  - Repeatable processes
  - Team capability building

### Evidence-Based UX

- Avoid:
  - Assumption-driven personas
- Invest in:
  - Field research
  - Real user data
- Impact:
  - Higher ROI (e.g., validated personas outperform assumptions)

## “Right First Time” vs Fix Later

### Anti-Pattern: Ship Now, Fix Later

- Risks:
  - Poor adoption
  - Negative perception
  - Loss of trust

### Preferred Approach: Right First Time

- Mitigate risk early through:
  - Research
  - Prototyping
  - Testing
- Agile UX enables:
  - Fast validation
  - Correct decisions early

## Growing UX Maturity Systematically

### Maturity Awareness

- Identify current level:
  - Conduct UX maturity audits
- Define:
  - Clear roadmap for improvement

### Process & Capability Building

- Establish:
  - UX processes
  - Pattern libraries
  - Style guides
- Enable:
  - User testing
  - Field studies
  - Measurement systems

### Aligning the Organization

- Bring together:
  - Business
  - Product
  - UX
- Avoid:
  - Treating UX as an afterthought
  - Misalignment across teams

## Key Principles

### Strategy Over Aesthetics

- UX is about:
  - Long-term capability
  - Not just visual output

### Reproducibility Over One-Time Success

- Build systems that:
  - Scale
  - Deliver consistent results

### Proactive Over Reactive

- Solve problems early:
  - Don’t rely on future fixes
- Strong UX:
  - Prevents issues before release
