## UX Maturity Requires Evangelization

### Making UX Visible & Accessible

- UX maturity depends on:
  - Educating teams
  - Making UX work understandable and accessible
- Without evangelization:
  - UX remains siloed
  - Organization cannot mature effectively

### Clear & Memorable UX Strategy

- UX strategy should be:
  - Simple
  - Clear
  - Easy to communicate (“elevator pitch”)
- Communicate through:
  - Before/after UI comparisons
  - Research and testing practices
  - Validation and outside-in design principles

## UX as a Business Value Driver

### Aligning UX with Business

- UX is not just design → it is a **business value tool**
- Strong organizations:
  - Clearly articulate UX’s impact on long-term success
  - Avoid short-term thinking
- Key idea:
  - UX must be tied to **ROI and strategic outcomes**

## Building a UX-Driven Culture

### Culture = Beliefs + Actions

- Culture is defined by:
  - What organizations **say**
  - What they actually **do**
- Misalignment:
  - Superficial UX understanding (“lip service”)
  - Resistance to real UX practices

### Outside-In Thinking

- UX must be grounded in:
  - Real user observation
  - Field research
- Key principle:
  - “Get out of the building”
- Insight:
  - Real innovation comes from unexpected user behaviors and pain points

## The 6 C’s of UX Culture

### 1. Clear Beliefs

- Define what the organization truly believes about UX

### 2. Compelling Stories

- Stories = “currency of empathy”
- Use:
  - User stories
  - ROI stories
  - Competitor insights

### 3. Consistent Trade-offs

- Balance UX with:
  - Business
  - Engineering
- Avoid:
  - Constantly sacrificing UX

### 4. Collective Celebrations

- Celebrate:
  - UX wins
  - Product success
- Reinforces:
  - Motivation
  - Cultural adoption

### 5. Constant Communication

- Encourage:
  - Informal conversations
  - Cross-team collaboration
- Avoid:
  - Process bottlenecks
  - Over-reliance on formal channels

### 6. Commitment to Employees

- Support UX teams through:
  - Proper org structure
  - Work-life balance
  - Empowerment
- Key insight:
  - Org placement > budget in UX effectiveness

## Organizational Enablement for UX

### Empowering UX Teams

- UX success requires:
  - Access to users
  - Freedom to conduct research
- Anti-pattern:
  - Blocking user access → kills empathy and innovation

### Leadership & Belief Systems

- Change starts with:
  - Leadership belief in UX
- Impact:
  - Enables cross-team support
  - Reduces resistance
  - Unlocks user access

## Practical UX Evangelization Techniques

### Tangible & Shared Artifacts

- Persona rooms:
  - Visualize user research
  - Share real-world insights
- Journey maps:
  - Show end-to-end experience
  - Highlight pain points and opportunities

### Informal Learning

- Brown-bag sessions:
  - Casual knowledge sharing
  - Continuous education
- Workshops:
  - Design Thinking
  - UX methods

### Collaborative Design Practices

- Design studios:
  - Cross-functional idea generation
  - Rapid sketching and iteration
- Benefits:
  - Inclusive participation
  - Better ideas from diverse roles

## Key Principles

### Align Say vs Do

- UX maturity requires:
  - Consistency between beliefs and actions

### Focus on Long-Term Growth

- UX culture is:
  - Continuous
  - Iterative
  - Evolving over time

### Accelerate Learning

- Goal:
  - Reduce maturity timeline (e.g., 10 years → 2–3 years)
- Approach:
  - Learn fast
  - Avoid repeat mistakes
  - Continuously adapt and calibrate
