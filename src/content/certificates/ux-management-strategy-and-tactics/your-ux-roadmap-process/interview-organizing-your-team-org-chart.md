## Where UX Teams Can Sit in an Organization

### Common Organizational Placement Options

UX teams can be positioned in several different departments within an organization.

Common placements include:

- Product management
- Marketing
- Product development (engineering)
- Centralized or independent UX team

Each location has advantages and disadvantages depending on the organization’s structure and culture.

## UX Under Product Management

### Advantages

Placing UX within product management can be beneficial because product managers control many key decisions.

Benefits include:

- Direct influence on product features and priorities
- Close alignment with product strategy
- Integration with product roadmaps and release planning

Product managers act as gatekeepers for what enters the product, so UX collaboration with them can increase the likelihood that UX insights influence decisions.

### Challenges

However, product managers may sometimes perceive UX as disruptive.

Concerns may include:

- UX introducing scope changes
- Additional research delaying development timelines
- Fear that UX changes could impact product delivery

Product managers often carry responsibility for product success and may worry that UX changes introduce additional risk.

UX teams must demonstrate that **user-centered design reduces risk rather than increasing it**.

## UX Under Marketing

### Advantages

In some organizations, marketing plays a strong role in shaping product direction.

Benefits of being under marketing include:

- Direct connection to customer insights
- Alignment with customer-facing strategy
- Access to market research and customer feedback

Marketing teams often interact directly with customers and may already collect valuable insights.

### Challenges

However, marketing research often focuses on:

- Customer preferences
- Demographic segmentation
- Feature requests

UX research focuses more on:

- User behavior
- Workflows and tasks
- Real interaction with products

Marketing methods often include:

- Surveys
- Focus groups
- A/B testing

While useful, these approaches may not reveal deeper usability problems.

UX methods emphasize:

- Behavioral research
- Field studies
- Usability testing

The difference between **customer opinions and user behavior** can create tension between marketing and UX teams.

## UX Under Development

### Advantages

Embedding UX within development teams can create strong collaboration with engineers.

Benefits include:

- Close communication with developers
- Faster feedback during implementation
- Shared problem-solving mindset

Developers often enjoy solving complex problems, which can create strong collaboration with UX designers.

### Challenges

However, development teams are typically focused on:

- Delivery schedules
- Technical feasibility
- Sprint commitments

This can lead to conflicts when UX advocates for:

- Additional research
- Design iterations
- User testing

UX priorities may conflict with development deadlines, especially during agile sprint planning.

Another common issue is **featuritis**, where engineers add many features without considering the overall user experience.

## Centralized UX Teams

### Advantages

A centralized UX team operates independently from product, marketing, or development.

Benefits include:

- Greater objectivity in UX decisions
- Strategic oversight across multiple products
- Consistency in UX practices across the organization

This structure allows UX teams to influence multiple departments rather than serving a single function.

### Challenges

Centralized teams may be perceived as:

- Distant from development teams
- Less connected to day-to-day product work
- Detached from operational decisions

To succeed, centralized UX teams must actively build relationships with other departments.

## Building Relationships with Developers

### Importance of Collaboration

Strong relationships with developers are essential for successful UX implementation.

Effective collaboration includes:

- Frequent communication
- Involving developers early in design discussions
- Ensuring prototypes and designs translate correctly into implementation

When developers feel ownership of UX solutions, collaboration improves.

## Strategic Role of UX

### UX Beyond Development

UX should not be perceived as a function of development or quality assurance.

If UX is embedded too deeply in development teams, other departments may view UX as:

- A technical implementation role
- A support function for engineering

However, UX has broader strategic value.

### UX as a Strategic Function

UX should influence:

- Product strategy
- Business decisions
- Customer experience

In many organizations, UX leadership may benefit from reporting closer to executive leadership rather than being limited to development teams.

## Key UX Strategy Principles

### Aligning UX With Organizational Conditions

UX strategies must adapt to the unique conditions of each organization.

Important considerations include:

- Organizational culture
- Team structures
- Product development processes

UX should grow organically within the organization rather than being imposed as an external process.

### Planning, Scaling, and Growing UX

Successful UX strategy follows three stages:

1. Plan: Understand current UX maturity and organizational conditions.
2. Scale: Introduce UX practices across teams and processes.
3. Grow: Expand UX influence across the organization.

UX maturity can be evaluated using scorecards to identify strengths and weaknesses in current UX practices.

## Importance of UX Staffing and Structure

The success of UX initiatives depends heavily on:

- Hiring the right UX professionals
- Clearly defining UX roles
- Positioning UX teams effectively within the organizational structure

These factors determine how effectively UX can influence product decisions and improve user experiences.
