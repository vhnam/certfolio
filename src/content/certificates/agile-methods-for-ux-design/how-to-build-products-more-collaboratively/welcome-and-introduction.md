## Lesson Overview

### Key Focus

- Collaborative design: Agile teams involve multiple roles in research and design.
- Early feedback: Sharing work-in-progress helps teams iterate faster.
- Shared goals: Teams need alignment to avoid chaos.
- Customer participation: Users and customers can actively contribute to design.
- Agile planning: Traditional long-term roadmaps often fail in agile environments.

## Collaboration in Agile Teams

### Cross-Team Involvement

- Team collaboration: Designers and researchers should involve developers, product managers, and other teammates in research and design activities.
- Shared understanding: Working together helps the team understand problems, goals, and user needs.
- Faster problem solving: Multiple perspectives help identify better solutions more quickly.

### Benefits of Collaborative Work

- Better decision-making
- Faster feedback cycles
- Greater alignment across the team
- Stronger understanding of user problems

## Early Feedback and Work in Progress

### Sharing Designs Early

- Work-in-progress sharing: Teams regularly present unfinished designs or research insights.
- Continuous feedback: Teammates review and comment before solutions are finalized.
- Rapid iteration: Early feedback reduces the risk of building the wrong solution.

### Agile Feedback Practices

- Informal design reviews
- Team critique sessions
- Quick prototype testing with users

## Aligning Teams Around a Shared Goal

### The Chaos Anti-Pattern

- Complete chaos: Agile teams without clear alignment may constantly change direction.
- Lack of shared understanding: Different team members may pursue different priorities.

### Maintaining Alignment

- Shared goals: Teams must clearly understand what success looks like.
- User-centered focus: Decisions should always connect to user needs and outcomes.
- Regular communication: Frequent discussions help keep everyone aligned.

## Participatory Design with Customers

### Participatory Design

- Participatory design: A design approach where users or customers actively participate in the design process.
- Co-creation: Designers collaborate with users to generate ideas and solutions.

### Benefits

- Deeper understanding of user needs
- Higher quality design ideas
- Stronger user validation during development

## Planning the Future in Agile

### Problems with Traditional Roadmaps

- Long-term predictions: Traditional roadmaps try to predict outcomes far into the future.
- Limited flexibility: They make it difficult for teams to adapt when new insights appear.
- Uncertainty in agile environments: Agile teams often work with evolving information.

### Agile-Friendly Planning

- Flexible planning tools: Agile teams use approaches that adapt to learning and feedback.
- Outcome-oriented planning: Focus on goals and problems rather than fixed timelines.
- Avoid rigid tools: Planning tools like Gantt charts are typically unsuitable for agile work.
