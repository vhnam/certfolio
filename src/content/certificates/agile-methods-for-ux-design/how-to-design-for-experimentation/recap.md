## Letting Go of Perfection

One of the biggest mindset shifts in agile design is accepting that deliverables don’t have to be perfect before going live. This aligns with core design practice: test with users, gather feedback, and iterate. The goal isn’t perfection — it’s continuous improvement.

## Release Strategically, Not All at Once

Shipping a feature doesn’t mean exposing it to everyone immediately. Teams can:

- Release to beta testers
- Roll out to a small segment of users
- Experiment in controlled live environments

This approach reduces risk while enabling real-world validation.

## Build the Minimum Viable Version

When developing new features:

- Focus on the **minimum viable** solution.
- Run experiments to validate assumptions.
- Decide whether to **pivot or persevere** based on evidence.

This keeps teams from overbuilding and wasting effort.

## Techniques to Avoid Overdesigning

Two practical methods help teams stay focused:

- **Finding the core** – Identify the essential elements of a complex feature.
- **Starting with one** – Build and test a single critical aspect before expanding.

These techniques encourage learning early and often.

## The Agile Quality Mindset

High-performing teams understand that:

- “Good enough” means it **solves a real user problem**.
- It must be **usable and learnable from**.
- Improvement happens through iteration.

Perfection isn’t required at launch — progress and learning are.
