## Experimentation in Agile

- Agile sprints: Treated as experiments to test assumptions quickly.
- Small builds: Focus on building small increments to learn fast.
- Time efficiency: Avoid wasting time and resources through rapid validation.

### Designing for Experiments

- Partial features: Sometimes necessary to ship incomplete functionality for learning.
- Unfinished products: Used intentionally to validate ideas.
- Risk: Poorly designed experiments can harm users or the product.
- Goal: Learn quickly **without** damaging user experience.

## Designing Without Perfection

- Perfectionism concern: Designers often worry about shipping incomplete work.
- Agile mindset: Prioritize validated learning over polished perfection.
- Practical focus: Deliver value incrementally while maintaining quality standards.

## Minimum Viable Product (MVP)

- MVP (Minimum Viable Product): The smallest version of a product that allows teams to validate core assumptions.
- Common misconception: MVP does not mean low quality or unfinished thinking.
- MVP definition technique: A structured method to define what should and should not be included in the first release.

### Defining an MVP

1. Identify core assumptions: What must be true for the product to succeed?
2. Define essential features: Include only what is necessary to test those assumptions.
3. Exclude non-essential elements: Delay enhancements that do not support validation.
4. Validate and iterate: Use feedback to refine and expand incrementally.
