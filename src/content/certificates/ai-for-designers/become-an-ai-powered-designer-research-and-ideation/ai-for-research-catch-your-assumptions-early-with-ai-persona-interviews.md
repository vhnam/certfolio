---

## Core Concept

AI personas allow designers to **simulate user conversations** and explore ideas quickly, but they must always be treated as **starting points—not truth**.

## What AI Personas Are Good For

### Exploration & Early Insights

- Simulate interviews to surface:
  - Themes
  - Pain points
  - Assumptions
- Useful when:
  - Planning research
  - Identifying gaps
  - Generating hypotheses

→ Output = **educated guesses**, not validated findings.

### Practicing Research

- Test interview scripts
- Improve question flow and follow-ups
- Refine pacing before talking to real users

→ Helps maximize value of real research time.

### Pattern Detection

- Run multiple simulations
- Look for **repeated themes**, not completeness

→ Goal is not full coverage, but:

- Identify **high-value areas to investigate**

## Synthetic vs Research-Backed Personas

### Synthetic AI Personas

- Created from prompts or market research
- Used for:
  - Exploration
  - Hypothesis generation
  - Early direction

### Research-Backed Interactive Personas

- Built from real user data
- Made interactive with AI
- Used for:
  - Quick team decisions
  - Building shared empathy
  - Early usability checks

→ More reliable, but still require validation.

## How Teams Can Use Interactive Personas

- Developers: Ask quick UX questions during implementation
- Teams: Build shared understanding of users
- Designers: Spot obvious usability issues before testing

→ Acts as a **fast feedback layer**, not final validation.

## Limitations of AI Personas

- Cannot replace real users
- Cannot capture:
  - Real behavior
  - Context
  - Emotional nuance
- Do not achieve true research saturation

→ AI outputs = **signals to investigate**, not conclusions.

## The Take Away

- Use AI personas to:
  - Explore ideas quickly
  - Refine research strategy
  - Identify what to test
- Always:
  - Validate with real users
  - Treat outputs as hypotheses

### Final Principle

- AI augments research speed
- Humans ensure research truth

→ Use AI to **inform decisions**, not make them.
