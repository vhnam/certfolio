---
title: "AI for Problem Solving: Define the Right Problem Faster"
---

## The Problem: Speed Without Depth

- AI dramatically accelerates:
  - Data processing
  - Pattern detection
- But creates risk of:
  - Superficial insights
  - Overconfidence in outputs

→ Faster analysis does **not guarantee better understanding**

## Where AI Falls Short

### Lack of Contextual Understanding

- AI identifies patterns but cannot:
  - Fully grasp human emotions
  - Interpret cultural context
  - Understand intent behind words

→ Insights may be technically correct but **contextually wrong**

### Distance from Raw Data

- Teams rely on:
  - Summaries instead of transcripts
  - Keywords instead of real stories

→ Leads to:

- Reduced empathy
- Missed nuances in tone and behavior

⚠️ Direct exposure to user data is critical for meaningful insights

### Over-Reliance on AI Outputs

- Risk of:
  - Accepting AI results at face value
  - Skipping validation steps
- AI can:
  - Hallucinate patterns
  - Misgroup themes

→ Outputs should be treated as **hypotheses, not conclusions**

## Impact on Team Dynamics

### Loss of Collaborative Sensemaking

- AI reduces need for:
  - Group analysis sessions
  - Discussion and debate

→ Teams lose:

- Diverse perspectives
- Shared understanding

### Reduced Critical Thinking

- Heavy AI usage can:
  - Shortcut deep analysis
  - Limit questioning of assumptions

→ Weakens the overall research quality

## The Risk to Research Quality

### Shallow Insights

- AI favors:
  - Frequency
  - Surface-level patterns
- But qualitative research requires:
  - Depth
  - Interpretation
  - Meaning-making

→ Important insights may be **overlooked or oversimplified**

### Bias & Misinterpretation

- AI reflects:
  - Training data biases
  - Prompt framing

→ Can distort findings if not carefully reviewed

## The Role of AI (Done Right)

### AI = Assistant, Not Decision-Maker

- Best used for:
  - Organizing data
  - First-pass analysis
  - Highlighting patterns
- Not for:
  - Final insights
  - Strategic decisions

### Human Responsibility

- Researchers must:
  - Validate findings
  - Interpret meaning
  - Connect insights to context

→ Humans turn data into **actionable knowledge**

## Key Takeaways

- AI is strong at:
  - Speed
  - Scale
  - Pattern recognition
- AI is weak at:
  - Context
  - Empathy
  - Judgment

→ Over-reliance leads to **shallow and risky insights**

## Final Insight

- AI finds patterns
- Humans understand people

→ Real qualitative insight comes from **human interpretation supported by AI, not replaced by it**
