## Shifting from AI User to AI Designer

### Role Transition

- AI user: Uses AI to automate tasks, analyze data, and support workflows.
- AI designer: Creates AI-powered solutions for end users.
- Perspective shift: Focus moves from “how AI helps me” to “how AI helps users.”

## Learning Objectives

### Designer Responsibilities

- Offering AI-driven solutions: Design products that integrate AI to solve user problems.
- Ethical responsibility: Ensure AI systems are fair, transparent, and user-centered.

### Understanding AI in Design

- Challenges: Complexity, bias, trust, and implementation risks.
- Opportunities: Automation, scalability, personalization, and innovation.

## Analyzing AI Systems

### Key Considerations

- Systematic analysis: Evaluate AI use cases, risks, and constraints.
- Major concerns:
  - Bias and fairness
  - Transparency and explainability
  - User trust and control

### Use Cases

- Real-world applications: Learn from practical implementations of AI in design.
- Insight-driven design: Use examples to inform better decision-making.

## Ethical AI Design

### Principles

- User-centered AI: Prioritize user needs and well-being.
- Responsible design: Avoid harm, ensure inclusivity, and maintain accountability.
