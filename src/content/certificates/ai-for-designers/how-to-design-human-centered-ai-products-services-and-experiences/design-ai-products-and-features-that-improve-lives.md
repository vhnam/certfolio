title: Design AI Products and Features That Improve Lives
---

## AI as an Enabler, Not the Goal

- AI offers **powerful opportunities to improve user experiences**, but it is not the end goal.
- Designers must:
  - Start with **user research and real problems**
  - Use AI only when it **meaningfully improves the experience**
- Key mindset: _Don’t add AI because you can—add it because it helps users_

## The Designer’s Role

- Designers act as a **bridge between technology and user needs**
- Even though engineers build AI systems, designers decide:
  - How AI is applied
  - Where it adds value
  - How users interact with it

→ AI success depends on **good design decisions, not just good models**

## Key Opportunities to Use AI

### 1. Increasing Efficiency

- AI can:
  - Learn from user behavior
  - Detect patterns
  - Offer proactive suggestions

#### Examples

- Smart suggestions (e.g., opening apps at the right time)
- Faster input methods (voice typing, predictive text)
- Route optimization (saving time, fuel, and effort)

→ Goal: **Save time, reduce effort, and optimize resources**

### 2. Reducing or Eliminating Mundane Tasks

- Ideal for tasks that are:
  - Repetitive
  - Low-effort
  - Require little decision-making

#### Examples

- Transcriptions, scheduling, reporting
- Generating visuals or content via prompts
- Detecting anomalies (e.g., accounting errors)

#### Important Constraint

- Automation is **not always desirable**:
  - Some tasks bring joy (e.g., journaling)
  - Some require human accountability (e.g., medical decisions)

→ Designers must decide:

- _What to automate_
- _What to keep human-controlled_

### 3. Supporting User Tasks

- AI enhances the ability to:
  - Complete tasks faster
  - Make better decisions
  - Access previously difficult capabilities

#### Examples

- Forecasting (e.g., financial planning)
- Predictive maintenance (detecting issues early)
- AI-assisted customer support

→ Goal: Make the product **“invisible”** while helping users achieve outcomes

### 4. Improving Safety and Health

- AI can process real-time data to:
  - Detect risks
  - Prevent harm
  - Trigger alerts

#### Examples

- Fall detection and emergency alerts
- Health monitoring (e.g., gait analysis)

→ Goal: **Protect users and enhance well-being**

## Understanding Users Through AI

- AI can analyze:
  - Behavior patterns
  - Usage trends
- Important distinction:
  - AI understands **patterns**, not human psychology

→ Designers must interpret insights carefully and responsibly

## AI in Experimentation & Optimization

- AI enables:
  - Testing multiple design variations
  - Measuring performance at scale

→ Helps designers:

- Iterate faster
- Make data-driven decisions

## The Limits of Automation

- Not everything should be automated
- Consider:
  - Emotional value of tasks
  - Need for human control
  - Risk and accountability

→ AI should **assist, not replace, meaningful human actions**

## Key Takeaways

- AI creates opportunities to:
  - Increase efficiency
  - Reduce repetitive work
  - Support complex tasks
  - Improve safety

- But:
  - AI must be **intentional and user-centered**
  - Poor use of AI can harm experience or remove value

## Final Insight

- AI is a **design material**, not just a feature
- Great designers:
  - Start with user problems
  - Think holistically
  - Then apply AI to **enhance—not define—the solution**
