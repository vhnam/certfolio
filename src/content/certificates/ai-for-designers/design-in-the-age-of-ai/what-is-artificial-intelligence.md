## Artificial Intelligence Explained

### Definition of Artificial Intelligence

- Artificial Intelligence (AI): Computer systems that perform tasks typically requiring human intelligence.
- Cognitive functions: Abilities such as perceiving, reasoning, learning, problem-solving, interacting with environments, and creativity.
- AI as a discipline: A branch of computer science focused on creating intelligent agents.
- AI as a capability: A machine’s ability to perform cognitive tasks associated with human thinking.

### Key Traits of AI

- Autonomy: Ability to perform tasks in complex environments without constant human guidance.
- Adaptivity: Ability to improve performance over time by learning from experience.

## Types of Artificial Intelligence

### Narrow AI (Weak AI)

- Narrow AI: Systems designed to perform specific tasks within defined limits.
- Characteristics:
  - Operates within clear boundaries.
  - Built for particular problems or functions.
  - Cannot transfer knowledge broadly across domains.

Examples:

- Voice recognition systems
- Recommendation algorithms
- Image recognition systems

### General AI (Strong AI)

- General AI: Hypothetical systems capable of performing any intellectual task a human can.
- Capabilities would include:
  - Learning and adapting across domains
  - Understanding and reasoning broadly
  - Applying knowledge flexibly

Note: General AI does **not currently exist** and remains largely theoretical.

## Common AI Capabilities in Everyday Products

### Sense: AI Perceives the World

AI systems that interpret inputs such as images, sounds, or language.

Examples:

- Face recognition unlocking smartphones
- Speech-to-text dictation
- Photo apps identifying objects or people
- Email clients recognizing intent or language

### Predict: AI Anticipates Outcomes

AI that estimates what will happen next using patterns in data.

Examples:

- Traffic predictions in navigation apps
- Spam filtering
- Fraud detection
- Content recommendations (Netflix, Spotify)

### Create: AI Generates Content

AI that produces new media or information.

Examples:

- Text generation tools like ChatGPT
- Image generation tools like Midjourney
- Photo editing features such as object removal
- Automatic captions or subtitles

### Decide: AI Chooses Between Options

AI that selects actions based on goals, rules, or probabilities.

Examples:

- Navigation apps choosing fastest routes
- Social media ranking content feeds
- Ad platforms deciding which advertisement to show
- Dynamic pricing systems (ride-sharing or airlines)

### Act: AI Performs Tasks Autonomously

AI systems that execute actions with minimal human involvement.

Examples:

- Robot vacuum cleaners
- Driver-assistance features in cars
- Smart thermostats adjusting temperature
- Warehouse automation robots

## Machine Learning and Deep Learning

![An illustrated infographic that demonstrates how machine learning and deep learning fits in with artificial intelligence.](../../../../assets/images/ai-for-designers/design-in-the-age-of-ai/ai-definitions.avif)

### Relationship Between AI, Machine Learning, and Deep Learning

- Artificial Intelligence: The broad field of building intelligent systems.
- Machine Learning: A subset of AI focused on enabling systems to learn from data without explicit programming.
- Deep Learning: A subset of machine learning using neural networks with multiple layers to detect complex patterns.

Hierarchy:

- AI
  - Machine Learning
    - Deep Learning

### Machine Learning

- Machine Learning: Teaching computers to learn patterns from data rather than programming explicit instructions.
- Used for:
  - Prediction
  - Pattern recognition
  - Decision-making

### Deep Learning

- Deep Learning: Uses deep neural networks to model complex relationships in large datasets.
- Common applications:
  - Image recognition
  - Natural language processing
  - Speech recognition

## Key Understanding for Designers

### AI Produces Probable Outputs

- AI systems do not generate perfect answers like calculators.
- They produce **probable outputs based on learned patterns**.
- Results may be:
  - Highly accurate
  - Plausible but incorrect

### AI in Everyday Life

AI already powers many widely used tools:

- Voice assistants (e.g., Siri, Alexa)
- Customer service chatbots
- Navigation apps (Google Maps, Waze)
- Writing assistants (Grammarly)
- Email predictive text
- Recommendation systems (Netflix, Spotify)
- Translation tools (Duolingo)

## Key Takeaways

- Artificial Intelligence can be understood as both:
  - A **computer science discipline**, and
  - A **machine capability** to perform human-like cognitive tasks.
- Current AI systems are **Narrow AI**, designed for specific tasks.
- AI systems rely on **autonomy** and **adaptivity**.
- **Machine learning** enables AI to learn from data.
- **Deep learning** is a specialized machine learning approach using neural networks.
- AI is already deeply integrated into everyday digital products and services.
