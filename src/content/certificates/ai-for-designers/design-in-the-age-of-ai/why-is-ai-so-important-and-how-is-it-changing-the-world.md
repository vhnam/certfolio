## Designing for AI

### AI as a New Interaction Paradigm

- Intent-based interaction: Users describe the outcome they want instead of giving step-by-step commands.
- Command-based interaction: Traditional interfaces where users control systems through explicit instructions.

AI-powered products change how people interact with technology. Instead of navigating rigid menus or commands, users can communicate goals and let the system determine how to achieve them.

### Design Implications

- Designers must rethink user expectations.
- Interfaces must support ambiguity and flexible input.
- Systems should clearly communicate AI capabilities and limitations.

## Designing with AI

### AI as a Design Tool

AI can assist designers throughout the design workflow by helping with ideation, research, and content generation.

Common uses include:

- Generating design ideas
- Summarizing research data
- Creating content drafts
- Exploring multiple design variations quickly

### Limitations of AI

- Limited understanding of human context
- Difficulty solving complex problems independently
- Lack of real empathy and human judgment

Because of these limitations, AI works best as a **support tool**, not a replacement for designers.

## Human Skills That Remain Essential

Designers still provide critical capabilities that AI cannot replicate.

Key human-centered skills include:

- Empathy: Understanding real user needs and emotions.
- Creativity: Generating original ideas and novel solutions.
- Systems thinking: Connecting different disciplines and constraints.
- Context awareness: Interpreting social, cultural, and psychological factors.

## The Future Role of Designers

Designers should view AI as a **collaborative partner** that enhances productivity and creativity.

Designers who learn how to work effectively with AI tools will:

- Work faster and more efficiently
- Explore more ideas during the design process
- Focus more on strategic and human-centered aspects of design
