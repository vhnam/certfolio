## AI as an Exoskeleton for Your Process

- Treat AI as a **capacity amplifier**, not a replacement.
- AI handles:
  - Repetitive, rule-based, scalable tasks
- You handle:
  - Strategy, intent, and decision-making
- Goal: **Free up time for high-value design thinking**

## Map Your Workflow, Not Your Tools

- Focus on **stages of work**, not specific tools.
- Tools can change → workflows should remain stable.
- Ask:
  - Where is the work repetitive?
  - Where is it rule-based and low-risk?
  - Where does it NOT require empathy?

These are your **automation entry points**.

## Apply the Four Automation Signals

Use AI when tasks are:

- **Repetitive** → recurring workflows
- **Rule-based** → clear instructions
- **Low-risk** → easy to fix errors
- **Low-empathy** → no deep human judgment

Avoid automation when tasks involve:

- Emotions
- Ethics
- Strategic decisions

## Build, Test, Iterate Your Workflow

- Start with **one task** to automate
- Measure:
  - Time saved
  - Quality impact
- Gradually expand across the workflow

Small improvements compound into:

- More time
- Better focus
- Higher-quality outcomes

## Stay Flexible and Keep Exploring

- AI tools evolve rapidly → your workflow should adapt
- Schedule time to:
  - Test new tools
  - Improve existing steps
- Replace tools only when they **clearly improve outcomes**

## Key Takeaways

- Think in **systems, not tools**
- Use AI to handle **execution**, not **decision-making**
- Protect human responsibility in:
  - Strategy
  - Ethics
  - User understanding
- The goal is not efficiency alone, but **more meaningful design work**
