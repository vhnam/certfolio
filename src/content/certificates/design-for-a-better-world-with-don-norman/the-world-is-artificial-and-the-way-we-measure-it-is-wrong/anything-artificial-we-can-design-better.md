## Artificiality in Everyday Life

### What Is Artificiality

- Artificiality: The concept that many aspects of our world are **human-made rather than naturally occurring**.
- Designed environments: Cities, buildings, roads, and infrastructure created intentionally by humans.
- Human influence: Many systems around us exist because people designed or modified them.

When observing everyday surroundings, most visible elements—such as **houses, streets, cars, and urban environments**—are artificial creations shaped by human decisions.

## Artificial Systems Beyond Physical Objects

### Social and Cultural Structures

Artificiality also applies to **social systems and human institutions**, not only physical artifacts.

- Laws: Rules created and enforced by societies.
- Country boundaries: Political divisions established by people.
- Cultural customs: Social behaviors and traditions developed over time.
- Social systems: Structures that organize how people live and interact.

These systems evolved over centuries as societies attempted to **organize life and achieve collective goals**.

### Time and Environmental Concepts

Some concepts we treat as natural are also human-defined systems.

- Seasons: Defined by Earth’s position relative to the Sun, but categorized and interpreted by humans.
- Work schedules: Standard working hours determined by societal norms.
- Calendar systems: Human-created frameworks for organizing time.

Different regions interpret and structure these systems differently—for example, some areas have **four seasons**, while others have **two or none**.

## Artificiality Implies Design

### Human-Made Means Human-Designed

If something is artificial, it was **created through human decisions and design processes**, even if it evolved gradually.

- Long-term design: Many systems developed incrementally over centuries.
- Collective design: Not always created by professional designers but shaped by societies.
- Continuous evolution: Systems change as societies adapt to new needs.

Because these systems are designed, they can also be **rethought and improved**.

## The Role of Designers in Improving the World

### Designers as Agents of Change

Designers have the ability to influence and improve artificial systems.

- Sustainable design: Creating solutions that support environmental sustainability.
- Participatory design: Involving communities and stakeholders in the design process.
- Humanity-centered design: Considering the well-being of all people and the planet.

These approaches help designers address complex global challenges by **rethinking the systems humans created**.

## Key Takeaways

- Much of the world around us is **artificial and human-made**.
- Artificial systems include **physical environments, social institutions, and cultural norms**.
- Anything that is designed can be **redesigned and improved**.
- Designers can help improve society by applying methods such as **sustainable design, participatory design, and humanity-centered design**.
