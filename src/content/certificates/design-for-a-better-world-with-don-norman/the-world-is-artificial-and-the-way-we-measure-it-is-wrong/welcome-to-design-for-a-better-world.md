## Introduction: Why Design Matters for the Future

The world faces complex challenges—climate change, inequality, fragile healthcare systems, political conflict, and environmental collapse. These problems require collaboration across disciplines, and designers have an important role to play.

In this course, **Don Norman** introduces the idea that design must evolve beyond improving products and interfaces. Instead of focusing only on usability and convenience, designers must help address **systemic global problems** and contribute to creating a more sustainable and equitable world.

The course builds on Norman’s book _Design for a Better World_, encouraging designers to think bigger about their responsibilities and impact.

## From Human-Centered to Humanity-Centered Design

Traditional **human-centered design** focuses on solving problems for individual users. While valuable, Norman argues that this perspective is no longer enough.

He proposes **humanity-centered design**, which expands the scope of design to consider:

- All people, not just individual users
- Society and social systems
- The environment and ecosystems
- Long-term sustainability

Design decisions influence supply chains, manufacturing, resource extraction, and waste. Because of this, designers must consider the **entire system and long-term consequences** of what they create.

## The Role of Designers in Solving Global Challenges

Designers often work within organizations that limit their influence. They may sit in the middle of corporate structures or depend on clients’ demands. However, Norman argues that designers can still create change by:

- Understanding **history, economics, politics, and social systems**
- Collaborating across disciplines
- Thinking at a **systems level**
- Challenging traditional definitions of success

By expanding their knowledge beyond design alone, designers can contribute more meaningfully to addressing global issues.

## What You Will Learn in This Course

The course combines theory with practical exercises to help you apply humanity-centered design to real-world challenges.

### Lesson 1: Understanding the Current System

You will explore how the world we live in—our technologies, institutions, and infrastructures—is **artificial and designed by humans**. Because of this, it can also be redesigned.

You will also examine why many current systems and design practices are **unsustainable**, and how new ways of measuring success may lead to better outcomes.

### Lesson 2: Sustainability and Circular Design

This lesson introduces examples of **sustainable and circular design**, demonstrating how products and systems can be created with environmental responsibility in mind.

### Lesson 3: Humanity-Centered Design in Practice

You will learn how humanity-centered design works in complex systems and how **incremental modular design** can help address large-scale problems step by step.

### Lesson 4: Designing for Behavior Change

Designers can influence how people behave. This lesson explores how design can help **encourage positive behavioral change**, which is essential for addressing global challenges.

### Lesson 5: The Future of Design and AI

The final lesson focuses on how designers can contribute practically to shaping a better world and examines the **role of artificial intelligence** in the future of design.

## Building a Portfolio Case Study

Throughout the course, you will work on a **portfolio case study** to apply what you learn.

Your task is to identify an individual, organization, or project that is already addressing a global challenge through design. You will then analyze:

- What problem they are solving
- How their solution works
- How they apply humanity-centered design principles
- Whether they use **incremental or modular approaches** to complex problems

The final case study should be concise—around **two to three pages**—and may include descriptions, diagrams, and references.

## Learning Through Community and Practice

The course encourages active participation and collaboration. You can:

- Share your case study progress with other learners
- Ask questions and exchange ideas in discussions
- Learn from examples of real-world design initiatives

Each lesson also includes additional resources and references for deeper exploration.

## The Goal of the Course

By the end of the course, you will understand how design can move beyond improving products to **transforming systems and addressing global challenges**.

With the tools and perspectives introduced in this course, designers can help create solutions that are **sustainable, equitable, and meaningful**—and ultimately contribute to building a better world.
