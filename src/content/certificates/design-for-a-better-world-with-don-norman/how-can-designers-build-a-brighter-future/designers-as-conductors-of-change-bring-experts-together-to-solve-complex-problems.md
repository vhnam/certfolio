title: "Designers as Conductors of Change: Bring Experts Together to Solve Complex Problems"
---

## Designers’ Superpower

### Core Idea

- Design methods: A set of approaches that can be applied to many complex problems, not tied to specific content.
- Human-centered focus: Emphasis on people’s lives, experiences, and real needs.

### Key Strengths

- People-centered thinking:
  - Focus on ensuring solutions are appropriate for users.
- Collaboration:
  - Bring together content experts and specialists.
- Coherence:
  - Ensure solutions are aligned and focused on human needs.

### Designer as Conductor

- Conductor metaphor:
  - Designers coordinate diverse experts like an orchestra conductor.
- Role:
  - Facilitate collaboration.
  - Align efforts toward meaningful, people-focused outcomes.

## Skills Designers Need to Evolve

### Expanded Skillset

- Management: Coordinate complex, multi-stakeholder projects.
- Persuasion: Influence stakeholders and drive alignment.
- Conciliation: Resolve conflicts across disciplines.
- Problem-solving: Navigate complex societal challenges.

### Broader Knowledge

- Society: Understand social systems and human behavior.
- Politics & government: Navigate policy and institutional constraints.
- Business & economics: Understand impact and feasibility.

## Designers Solving Societal Problems

### Applying Design Principles

- Root cause focus:
  - Identify fundamental problems, not just symptoms.
- Iterative experimentation:
  - Test ideas in small steps.
  - Refine through continuous feedback.

### Collaboration at Scale

- Work with:
  - Multiple disciplines
  - Policymakers
  - Existing initiatives and projects

## Designing Change Despite Resistance

### Challenges

- Self-interest conflict:
  - People may resist changes that negatively impact them.
- Complexity:
  - Societal problems involve many interconnected factors.

### Design Strategies

1. Empower users:
   - Let people feel in control of decisions.
2. Shared benefit:
   - Show how solutions benefit everyone.
3. Trade-off design:
   - Balance losses with gains in other areas.

## Key Design Approach for Societal Impact

### Principles

- Human-centered:
  - Focus on people’s experiences and needs.
- Participatory:
  - Let people lead and be involved in decisions.
- Experimental:
  - Use small, testable steps.
- Flexible:
  - Adapt based on feedback and outcomes.

## Takeaways

- Designers’ role:
  - Act as facilitators and leaders of change.
- Core capability:
  - Apply design methods to complex societal issues.
- Requirement for success:
  - Expand beyond craft skills into broader domains.
- Ultimate goal:
  - Create coherent, people-centered solutions for a better world.
