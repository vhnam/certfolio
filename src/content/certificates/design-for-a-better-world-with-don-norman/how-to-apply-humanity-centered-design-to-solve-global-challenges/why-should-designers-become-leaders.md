---
title: Why Should Designers Become Leaders?
---

## The Challenge of Designer Influence

### Designers Are Often Ignored

- Low position in hierarchy: Designers are rarely decision-makers.
- Limited influence: Ideas may be overlooked in large, expert-driven projects.
- Need for change: Designers must step into leadership roles to create impact.

## Expanding the Role of Designers

### Beyond Traditional Design

- Not just aesthetics or usability:
  - Must understand complex systems
  - Must engage with multiple disciplines
- Learn new domains:
  - Work with experts (engineering, public health, etc.)
  - Build cross-functional partnerships

### Strength of Designers

- Human-centered perspective:
  - Focus on real human needs and experiences
- Beginner’s mindset:
  - Ask “why” questions
  - Avoid expert bias
- System thinking:
  - Consider long-term and system-wide impact

## Designers vs Project Managers

### Project Managers

- Focus on execution:
  - Planning, budgeting, timelines
  - Resource and operations management

### Designers

- Focus on people:
  - Ensure solutions meet human needs
  - Advocate for users and communities

### Collaboration

- Complementary roles:
  - Designers + project managers must work together
- Shared goal:
  - Deliver solutions that are both effective and meaningful

## Designers as Leaders

### Moving Up the Hierarchy

- Designers must:
  - Take broader responsibility
  - Influence decision-making
- Think like leaders:
  - Focus on what is best for the entire project
  - Not just design outputs

### Political Skills

- Gain trust:
  - Be a team player
  - Support other disciplines
- Communicate effectively:
  - Frame ideas as beneficial for everyone
- Align perspectives:
  - Understand and integrate different viewpoints

## Working in Complex Projects

### Cross-Disciplinary Collaboration

- Multiple experts involved:
  - Engineering, public health, management, etc.
- Designers act as:
  - Coordinators
  - Translators between disciplines

### Participatory Design

- Design _with_ people:
  - Involve communities in decision-making
- Democratize design:
  - Give people a voice in shaping solutions

## Promoting Incremental Approaches

### Problem with Large-Scale Solutions

- Experts tend to:
  - Solve everything at once
  - Create rigid, complex systems
- These approaches often fail due to inflexibility

### Designer’s Role

- Advocate incremental modular design:
  - Small, adaptable steps
  - Continuous learning and iteration
- Help teams:
  - Step back from premature solutions
  - Focus on root causes

## Practical Actions for Designers

### What Designers Should Do

- Bring actionable ideas
- Tell a compelling “bigger story”
- Understand all stakeholders’ perspectives
- Facilitate collaboration
- Support teams and build trust
- Focus on root problems
- Promote incrementalism
- Take a system-wide perspective

## Key Takeaways

### Leadership is Essential

- Designers must move beyond execution to influence decisions.

### Human-Centered Focus

- Always prioritize people and community involvement.

### Collaboration Over Control

- Work with experts, not against them.

### Incremental Change Over Big Plans

- Advocate flexible, iterative approaches to solve complex problems.
