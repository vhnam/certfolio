## Expanding Design Beyond Individuals

### From Human-Centered to Humanity-Centered

- All systems are interconnected:
  - Natural systems and human-made systems influence each other
- Design scope must expand:
  - From individual users → entire humanity and ecosystem
- Goal:
  - Create solutions that support both people and the environment

## Core Principle: Community Participation

### Designing _With_ People

- Community involvement is essential:
  - People must actively participate in shaping solutions
- Designers’ role:
  - Advocate for users and ecosystems
  - Ensure voices are heard in decision-making

## Tackling Complex Problems with Incrementalism

### Small Steps Over Big Plans

- Incrementalism:
  - Break large problems into manageable steps
- Benefits:
  - Reduces risk
  - Enables learning and adaptation
- Approach:
  - Act → evaluate → adjust → repeat

## Incremental Modular Design

### Combining Iteration and Structure

- Projects are divided into:
  - Small steps → further broken into modules
- Modules:
  - Have defined inputs and outputs
  - Can change internally without affecting the whole system

### Key Advantage

- Flexibility:
  - Systems evolve without breaking
  - Teams can continuously improve parts independently

## The Role of Designers

### Bridging People and Systems

- Designers specialize in:
  - Understanding human needs
  - Addressing real-world problems
- Key responsibilities:
  - Advocate for people and ecosystems
  - Translate across disciplines
  - Ensure solutions remain humanity-centered

## Key Takeaways

### Design for Systems, Not Just Users

- Consider the full ecosystem and long-term impact

### Work Incrementally

- Solve complex problems through small, iterative steps

### Empower Participation

- Involve communities as active contributors

### Designers as Connectors

- Bridge perspectives and keep solutions grounded in human needs

## What’s Next

### Facing Real-World Challenges

- Upcoming focus:
  - Resistance to change
- Designers must learn:
  - How to navigate and overcome human barriers to implementation
