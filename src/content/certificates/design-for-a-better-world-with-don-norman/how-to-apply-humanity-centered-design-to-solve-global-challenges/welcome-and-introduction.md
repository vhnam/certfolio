## Humanity-Centered Design

### Evolution of Design Terminology

- User-centered design: Focused on usability of systems for “users,” especially in early computing.
- Human-centered design: Shifted language to emphasize people instead of abstract “users.”
- People-centered design: Alternative naming to reinforce human perspective.
- Humanity-centered design: Expands scope to society, ecosystems, and global challenges.
- Design thinking: Umbrella term often used to describe similar approaches and methods.

### Why the Evolution Happened

- Early systems (e.g., Unix editors) lacked feedback → caused user errors and frustration.
- Poor design in high-risk systems (e.g., nuclear control rooms, aviation) led to serious consequences.
- Initial focus was too narrow (individual interaction), missing system-level and societal complexity.

### Core Principles of Human-Centered Design

- Focus on people: Understand real human needs and behaviors.
- Solve the right problem: Address root causes, not surface symptoms.
- Think in systems: Consider interactions between components, environments, and stakeholders.
- Iterate continuously: Test, refine, and improve solutions over time.

### Shift to Humanity-Centered Design

- Moves beyond individuals → focuses on groups, societies, and global systems.
- Addresses complex socio-technical challenges (e.g., sustainability, public systems).
- Requires broader system thinking and interdisciplinary collaboration.

### Relationship with Design Thinking

- Shared foundation:
  - Human focus
  - Iterative processes
  - Problem framing
- Design thinking adds:
  - Creative methods for ideation
  - Techniques for generating innovative solutions
- Both aim to produce:
  - Novel
  - Feasible
  - Impactful outcomes

### Key Takeaways for Designers

- Design is not just about usability → it is about societal impact.
- Terminology evolution reflects expanding responsibility.
- Effective design requires:
  - Systems thinking
  - Continuous iteration
  - Cross-disciplinary methods
- The ultimate focus is:
  - People → Society → Humanity
