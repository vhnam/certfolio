## Challenges of Incrementalism in Real Projects

### Funding Constraints

- Incremental funding: Requires securing small amounts repeatedly for each step.
- Funding difficulty: Harder to obtain multiple small funds than one large investment.
- Political timing: Large funding opportunities may be rare and time-sensitive.

### Core Tension

- Flexibility vs funding:
  - Incrementalism: Flexible but hard to fund.
  - Large projects: Easier to fund but inflexible.

## Incremental Modular Design

### Definition

- Incremental modular design: A hybrid approach combining incrementalism, Agile principles, and modular architecture to maintain flexibility while securing large-scale funding.

### Core Components

- Incrementalism: Solve problems through small, iterative steps.
- Minimum Viable Project (MVP): Deliver small but functional outcomes.
- Modular design: Break systems into independent components with defined inputs and outputs.

## Core Principles

### Incremental Development

- Small steps: Progress through manageable increments.
- Continuous evaluation: Assess results after each step.
- Directional learning: Adjust future steps based on outcomes.

### Minimum Viable Project

- MVP: Smallest functional unit that delivers a usable result.
- End-to-end functionality: Each step must work from start to finish.
- Proof of progress: Demonstrates value and validates direction.

### Modular Architecture

- Modules: Independent units with:
  - Defined inputs
  - Defined outputs
- Encapsulation: Internal workings can change without affecting the system.
- Replaceability: Modules can be improved or swapped independently.

## Process of Incremental Modular Design

1. Define goals: Align stakeholders (funders, communities, teams) on shared objectives.
2. Democratize design: Involve users through participatory design.
3. Build MVPs: Deliver small, functional increments.
4. Modularize system: Structure solutions into independent modules.
5. Iterate and improve: Continuously refine modules and overall system.

## Advantages of Incremental Modular Design

![We can adapt to different social and technical conditions when we have a clear goal and use multiple smaller projects divided into standalone modules.](../../../../assets/images/design-for-a-better-world-with-don-norman/how-to-apply-humanity-centered-design-to-solve-global-challenges/incremental-modular-design.avif)

### Flexibility

- Adaptive requirements: Adjust goals based on real-world feedback.
- Iterative learning: Improve continuously through testing and evaluation.

### System Evolution

- Module replacement: Update parts without breaking the whole system.
- Gradual improvement: Transition from manual to automated solutions over time.

### Local Adaptation

- Context sensitivity: Modify internal workings to fit local cultures and conditions.
- Independence: Maintain consistent inputs/outputs while adapting implementation.

## Practical Implementation Insights

### Working System Approach

- Partial implementation: Combine fully built modules with temporary or manual ones.
- Progressive enhancement: Replace temporary solutions with optimized modules over time.
- Early validation: Ensure the system works even in incomplete stages.

## Key Takeaways

### Balanced Strategy

- Combine large funding with incremental execution.
- Avoid rigid, all-at-once solutions.

### Design Philosophy

- Focus on small, testable progress.
- Maintain flexibility through modular systems.

### Outcome

- Achieve scalable, adaptable solutions for complex socio-technical problems.
