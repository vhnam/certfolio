Design has the power to shape not only products and services, but also the future of our societies. This course introduces humanity-centered design, an approach championed by Don Norman that expands traditional human-centered design to address complex global challenges such as climate change, inequality, conflict, and waste. You will learn how to apply Design Thinking, systems thinking, ethics, and empathy to understand large socio-technical systems and design solutions that create meaningful and lasting impact.

Throughout the course, you will explore practical methods for tackling complex problems across industries. You will learn how to mobilize stakeholders, overcome resistance to change, and lead initiatives that drive systemic transformation. The course also introduces circular design principles and sustainable strategies that help organizations create long-term value while reducing harm to the environment and society.

You will develop essential skills such as creative problem solving, ecosystem thinking, and ethical decision-making. At the same time, you will learn how to work alongside AI as a powerful tool for ideation, prototyping, and simulation—while using uniquely human capabilities like empathy and critical thinking to guide responsible outcomes.

By the end of the course, you will have a practical framework for designing solutions that benefit people, organizations, and the planet. With guided projects, templates, and an optional portfolio case study, you will gain the confidence to apply humanity-centered design in real-world contexts and contribute to building a more sustainable and equitable world.
