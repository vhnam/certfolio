## Lesson Overview

### Purpose of the Lesson

- Behavioral Change in Design: Understand how designers can influence positive human behavior.
- Global Impact: Explore how human behavior contributes to major world challenges.
- Designer’s Role: Learn how design can reduce resistance and enable meaningful change.

### Key Learning Areas

- Resistance to Change: Why individuals and organizations avoid changing behaviors.
- Reactive vs Preventive Behavior: Why people respond more to disasters than prevention.
- Climate Action Shift: Why society has started taking climate change more seriously.
- Design Strategies: Methods designers can use to overcome resistance to change.
- Human Behavior & Global Issues: Dual role of behavior as both a problem and solution.
- Beyond Climate Change: Opportunities for designers to impact broader societal challenges.
- Case Study Development: How to build the next layer in your case study.

## Human Behavior and Change

### Resistance to Change

- Behavioral Inertia: People prefer familiar habits over uncertain alternatives.
- Organizational Barriers: Companies avoid risks associated with change.

### Reactive vs Preventive Actions

- Disaster Response Bias: People act after visible consequences rather than before.
- Prevention Neglect: Long-term risks are often ignored due to lack of urgency.

### Climate Change Awareness

- Shift in Behavior: Increased global action driven by visible impacts and awareness.

## Designer’s Role in Behavior Change

### Strategies to Overcome Resistance

- Design Interventions: Create systems that encourage gradual behavior shifts.
- Motivation Design: Align solutions with user incentives and values.

### Opportunities for Impact

- Beyond Climate: Apply behavior change strategies to various global and social issues.
- System-Level Thinking: Address complex problems through human-centered design.

## Case Study Development

### Next Layer of Case Study

- Application: Integrate behavioral insights into your project.
- Expansion: Build deeper context around human impact and design decisions.
