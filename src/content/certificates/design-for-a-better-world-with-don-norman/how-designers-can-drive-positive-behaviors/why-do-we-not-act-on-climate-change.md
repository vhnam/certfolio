---
title: Why Do We Not Act on Climate Change?
---

## Human Perception and Inaction

### Limits of Human Understanding

- Complex Systems: Climate and global systems are nonlinear, interconnected, and difficult to grasp.
- Simplified Mental Models: Humans rely on simple cause-effect thinking.
- Delayed Causality: Actions do not always produce immediate visible results.

### Cognitive Biases

- Immediate Feedback Bias: People expect quick, visible outcomes.
- Sensory Dependence: Trust is placed on what can be directly seen or felt.
- Limited Attention: The brain prioritizes immediate and salient information.

## Reactive vs Preventive Behavior

### Disaster Response Pattern

- Reactive Behavior: Humans respond quickly after disasters occur.
- Preventive Failure: Lack of action before crises despite predictions.

### Example: Pandemic Preparedness

- Predictions Ignored: Warnings about global pandemics were known.
- Lack of Preparation: Minimal preventive action taken.
- Crisis Response: Action only occurred after the crisis began.

## Short-Term Thinking and Power Structures

### Incentives Against Change

- Short-Term Rewards: Systems reward immediate gains over long-term benefits.
- Risk Avoidance: Change threatens existing power, wealth, and influence.
- Organizational Resistance: Leaders and companies resist disruptive changes.

### Power Dynamics

- Political and Corporate Power: Decision-makers benefit from current systems.
- Fear of Loss: Change may reduce control, profit, or status.

## Climate Change as a Turning Point

### Visibility Drives Action

- Tangible Effects: Fires, floods, and extreme weather make climate change visible.
- Emotional Impact: Direct experience increases urgency and response.

### Shift Toward Action

- Increased Awareness: People begin to recognize real consequences.
- Behavioral Change: Growing willingness to act as impacts become undeniable.

## Key Takeaways

### Human Behavior Patterns

- Short-Term Focus: Preference for immediate, visible problems.
- Linear Thinking: Difficulty understanding complex, systemic issues.
- Reactive Nature: Action occurs after, not before, crises.

### Implications for Change

- Behavior-Centered Design: Solutions must align with how humans perceive and act.
- Long-Term Thinking: Shift from short-term gains to sustainable outcomes.
- Visibility Matters: Making problems tangible increases engagement and action.
