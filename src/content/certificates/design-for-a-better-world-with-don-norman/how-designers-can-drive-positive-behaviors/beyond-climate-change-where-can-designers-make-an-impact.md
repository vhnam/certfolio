## Design Beyond Sustainability

### Core Insight

- Design Scope: Design methods can be applied to **any complex global issue**, not just sustainability.
- Root Cause Focus: Designers analyze underlying problems, not just surface symptoms.
- Human-Centered Systems: Solutions must consider both people and the larger systems they operate in.

## Addressing Global Inequities

### Key Challenges

- Inequality: Gaps in access to healthcare, sanitation, infrastructure, and digital systems.
- Resource Constraints: Many regions lack electricity, clean water, and stable infrastructure.
- Context Mismatch: Solutions are often designed for developed contexts and fail elsewhere.

### Design Opportunities

- Appropriate Solutions: Create tools that work under real-world constraints (low resources, limited infrastructure).
- Local Adaptation: Design for specific cultural and environmental contexts.
- Accessibility: Ensure systems are usable for underserved populations.

## Collaboration and Systems Thinking

### Multi-Disciplinary Approach

- Cross-Field Collaboration: Work with healthcare, public health, education, and policy sectors.
- Conflicting Perspectives: Balance different stakeholder needs and priorities.
- System Integration: Align solutions across complex ecosystems.

### Designer’s Role

- Facilitator: Bring different stakeholders together.
- Mediator: Reconcile conflicting goals.
- Advocate: Focus on what benefits people, not just organizations.

## Real-World Impact Areas

### Healthcare and Public Health

- Low-Resource Design: Develop tools that work without electricity or clean water.
- Preventive Systems: Improve sanitation, disease prevention, and healthcare access.

### Communication and Infrastructure

- Information Access: Help farmers and fishers access market data and make informed decisions.
- Financial Systems: Enable alternative ways to transfer money in underserved regions.
- Connectivity Challenges: Design for poor or unstable network conditions.

## Key Takeaways

### Power of Design

- Broad Applicability: Design can address diverse global challenges.
- Human Focus: Prioritize real human needs and constraints.
- System Awareness: Understand and design within complex systems.

### Opportunity for Impact

- Endless Possibilities: Many global problems remain open for design intervention.
- Meaningful Change: Designers can significantly improve lives through thoughtful, inclusive solutions.
