## Visualization

### Role of Visualization in UI Design

- Visualization: The process of turning design ideas into tangible, shareable representations.
- Conceptual models: Show what matters most to users and define the structure of a system.
- Visualization: Demonstrates how those concepts connect and function in a real interface.

Visualization bridges thinking and implementation by helping teams clearly see how a product should work.

### Benefits of Strategic Visualization

Strategic visualization improves both the design process and team collaboration.

- Clarity: Makes complex ideas easier to understand.
- Control: Helps designers structure and refine solutions.
- Communication: Enables clearer discussion with teammates and stakeholders.
- Collaboration: Aligns designers and developers around a shared understanding.
- Scalability: Supports systems that grow naturally as products evolve.

### Key Visualization Techniques

#### Sketching

- Sketches: Quick, informal drawings used to capture ideas rapidly.
- Purpose: Explore concepts freely without worrying about details.
- Benefit: Encourages creativity and rapid iteration.

#### Choosing the Right Visualization

Designers should select visualization methods based on the situation.

- Early ideas: Use rough sketches.
- Structured design thinking: Use diagrams or maps.
- Team collaboration: Use visual artifacts that communicate structure clearly.

Selecting the right visualization helps maintain efficiency and clarity throughout the design process.

#### Design Maps

- Design maps: Visual representations that connect user stories, objects, and interface elements.
- Purpose: Translate conceptual models into structured UI design.
- Benefit: Creates a smooth handoff between design and development.

Design maps help teams understand how features, objects, and interactions fit together.
