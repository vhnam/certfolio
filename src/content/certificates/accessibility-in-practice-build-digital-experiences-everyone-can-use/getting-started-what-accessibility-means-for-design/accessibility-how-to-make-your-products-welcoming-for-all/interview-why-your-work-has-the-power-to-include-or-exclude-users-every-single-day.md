## Interview: Experiences of a Visually Impaired Consultant

### Definitions

- **Legally Blind**: A level of vision loss that, in this case, refers to being totally blind and requiring screen readers for navigation.
- **Screen Readers**: Software that reads digital text aloud, used on phones, computers, and web applications.
- **Braille Electronic Device**: A note-taker used for writing and reading Braille electronically.
- **Web-based Apps**: Applications that run in a browser which often present more accessibility hurdles than traditional static websites.

### Real-World Access Barriers

People with visual impairments often face significant physical and social obstacles before even accessing digital services.

- **Physical Shopping**: Requires human assistance to find products, read labels, and check prices.
- **Social/Government Ignorance**: Examples include postal workers refusing to help a blind person fill out forms, despite legal obligations under the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA).
- **Lack of Empathy**: Accessibility issues often stem from a broader cultural lack of empathy and ignorance regarding disability rights.

### Evolution of Assistive Technology

The process of accessing information has shifted from manual, multi-step procedures to more direct digital access.

1. **Manual Era**: Required physical books, human readers, or writing out information in hard-copy Braille.
2. **Early Digital Scanning**: Involved scanning physical pages, using translation software, and printing them into Braille—a four-step process prone to software errors.
3. **Mobile Era**: Current ability to buy, download, and read books immediately using a screen reader on a smartphone.

### Current Digital Accessibility Challenges

While technology has improved, modern web design has introduced new "junk" and cognitive loads for screen reader users.

- **Unlabeled Controls**: Buttons and links without descriptive text that fail to inform the user of their function.
- **Poor Focus Management**: Pages that "jump" unexpectedly, forcing users to relocate their position at the top of the page.
- **Complex Web Apps**: Modern dynamic applications (e.g., Facebook, banking sites) are often less friendly to screen readers than older, simpler websites.

## Braille Literacy and Employment

### Statistics and Trends

- **Literacy Rates**: Today, approximately 70% of blind people do not read Braille.
- **Employment Correlation**: 90% of employed blind adults are Braille-literate, suggesting a strong link between Braille skills and career success.

### Compatibility and Progress

- **Technological Advances**: Modern screen readers and frameworks (e.g., WCAG 2.1/2.2) provide better support for dynamic web applications.
- **Remaining Barriers**: Inconsistent keyboard navigation and improper focus management continue to hinder the user experience.

Quoted verbatim:

> **The Cost of Inaccessibility**
>
> - "Poor accessibility makes you think harder, work harder, and listen harder."
> - "Developers and designers don't even realize [the biases] are in there."
> - "Designing for humans never goes out of style, which makes your career future-proof and protects you against AI."
