## Accessibility Standards

- WCAG 2.2: Builds on WCAG 2.1 with 9 new success criteria
- Focus: Mobile users and people with cognitive disabilities
- Adoption: Global standard used by most countries and organizations

> “The power of the Web is in its universality. Access by everyone regardless of disability is an essential aspect.”
> — Tim Berners-Lee

## Disability Prevalence

### United Kingdom

- 25% of population (16.8M) had a disability (2023/24)
- 24% of working-age population (10.4M) disabled (Q2 2025)
- Increased from 18% in 2002/03

### United States

- 28.7% of adults (~70M) have a disability
- ~13% of total population affected (2024)

### European Union

- 23.9% of population (107M people) aged 16+ have disabilities (2024)

## Legal Case

- 19% of US population (US Census 2012)
- Ratio: 1 in 5 (US & UK), 1 in 7 (EU)
- Accessibility is increasingly treated as a protected human right
- Accessibility is required by federal law
- Lawsuits: 250+ businesses sued in the US (2015–2017)

## Accessibility Lawsuits

### Volume

- 4,187 digital accessibility lawsuits in 2024
- 4,000+ lawsuits annually since 2021
- 25,000+ cases from 2018–2025
- 8,800 ADA cases in 2024; ~9,100 projected in 2025

### Trends

- 77% target eCommerce websites
- 25% cite accessibility widgets/overlays as barriers
- 77% target companies under $25M revenue

## Legislation

### USA

- American Disabilities Act (1990)
- Telecom Act (1996)
- Section 508 (1998, effective 2001)
- Section 508 Refresh (2018)
- 21st Century Telecom Act (2010)

### Europe

- European Accessibility Act
- EU Charter of Fundamental Rights
- European Disability Strategy 2010–2020
- EU eGovernment Action Plan 2016–2020
- Digital Agenda for Europe
- ICT Standardisation Rolling Plan (2017)

## United Nations

- UN Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities

Covers:

- Computers and operating systems
- ATMs, ticketing, check-in machines
- Smartphones
- TV and audiovisual media
- Telephony services
- Transport services (air, bus, rail, water)
- Banking services
- E-books

## Sweden & Norway

- Sweden: Discrimination Act (2008) prevents discrimination, including due to inadequate accessibility
- Norway: Anti-discrimination law (2013) under ICT authority (Difi)
- EU Directive 2016/2102: Web & mobile accessibility (effective 2018)

## Moral Case

- Universal access and interoperability underpin web standards (responsive design: any device, any resolution)
- Corporate Social Responsibility
- People with disabilities face discrimination:
  - Employment (earn ~70% less, US Census 2015)
  - Access to services (public & private)
  - Access to online content
- Many individuals have health issues or multiple disabilities

## Business Case

- Avoid lawsuits
- Encourages clean code and usable design
- Supports SEO and usability
- Supports internationalization
- Supports mobile-friendly content
- Responsive design + HTML5, CSS3, ARIA improve accessibility
