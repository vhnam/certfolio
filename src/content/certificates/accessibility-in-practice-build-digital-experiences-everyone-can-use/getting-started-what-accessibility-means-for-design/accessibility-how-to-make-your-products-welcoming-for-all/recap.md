## Definitions

- **Accessibility (A11y)**: Making digital products useful, usable, and searchable for everyone, particularly those with disabilities.
- **Assistive Technology (AT)**: Tools such as screen readers (e.g., JAWS, Apple VoiceOver) used by individuals with disabilities to interact with digital content.
- **Alt Text (Alternative Text)**: Text descriptions for images that allow non-visual browsers or screen readers to convey the meaning of graphical information.
- **Design Affordances**: Visual or structural cues that indicate how a user can interact with a design, such as expand/collapse icons.
- **Audio Clutter**: A state of poor accessibility where a screen reader user is forced to hear redundant, repetitive, or meaningless information.

## The Core Value of Inclusive Design

Accessibility is a legal, moral, and business imperative. Designing for the most challenged users results in a superior product for the entire population.

- **Universal Benefit**: Accessibility features help everyone in limiting environments, such as being in bright sunshine, a quiet library without headphones, or when experiencing high levels of fatigue.
- **Legal Compliance**: Digital access is governed by the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) and Section 508 in the US, the European Accessibility Act (EAA), and UN mandates.
- **Economic Impact**: Only 37.6% of screen reader users are employed full-time, underscoring the critical need for accessible job applications and workplace tools.

## Types of Disabilities

Understanding the primary categories of disability is essential for determining accessibility states:

- **Blindness**: Includes legally blind users who rely on screen readers and a minority who use Braille readers.
- **Low Vision**: Users who can still see the screen but may require extreme zoom or high contrast.
- **Color Blindness**: Affects approximately 8% of men; design must not rely on color alone to convey information.
- **Deaf and Hard of Hearing (HOH)**: Requires all video content to be captioned accurately.
- **Cognitive**: Includes conditions like dyslexia or memory impairments; design should minimize cognitive load.
- **Motor/Mobility**: Users who may have limited hand function and interact via specialized devices.

## Impact on Performance and SEO

Usability and accessibility are closely related "sisters" that directly influence a site's technical health and visibility.

- **Usability Benefits**: Leads to optimized code, simplified layouts, and device compatibility.
- **SEO Optimization**: Google’s algorithm favors sites with fewer access barriers and longer "time on site." Searchable graphical information through alt text and captions improves overall rankings.
- **Overlap Areas**: Key areas to optimize for both include video transcriptions, semantic HTML, descriptive title tags, and logical content ordering.

## Accessibility Quality Goals

To achieve high-quality accessibility, designers and developers must move beyond automated "checkers" and focus on user-centric goals.

- **Provide Consistent and Pro-active Alt Text**:
  1. Never leave images or spacers without alt tags.
  2. Describe what the image _means_ rather than just what it _shows_.
  3. Use empty alt tags (`alt=""`) for purely decorative images so screen readers know to ignore them.
- **Enable Content Access**: Ensure all video content has synchronized close captioning and maintain a clear heading structure (H1, H2, H3).
- **Reduce Tedium in Navigation**:
  - Address usability issues like text size and color contrast (minimum 4.5:1 for standard text).
  - Prevent "lost loops" where poor headings or navigation structures trap users in repetitive cycles.
  - Avoid "hidden content" caused by untagged mobile elements like hamburger menus.

Quoted verbatim:

> **The Universal Design Principle**
>
> - "Invariably when we design something that can be used by those with disabilities, we often make it better for everyone."

> **The Impact of Poor Accessibility**
>
> - "Poor or non-compliant accessibility forces users into dead-ends, redundant navigation, lost loops, audio clutter and being locked out of content."
