## Multistability

### Definition

- Multistability: A perceptual phenomenon where a single ambiguous image can be interpreted in **multiple meaningful ways**.
- Perceptual switching: The experience of alternating between different interpretations of the same visual stimulus.
- Ambiguous stimuli: Images or shapes that allow more than one valid interpretation.

When viewing such stimuli:

- The visual system **switches between interpretations**.
- Only **one interpretation is perceived at a time**.
- The shift between interpretations often occurs **spontaneously and unconsciously**.

### Characteristics of Multistable Perception

- Interpretation depends on:
  - Attention
  - Visual focus
  - Context
  - External cues (e.g., text or suggestions from others)
- Once one interpretation is identified, it can be difficult to intentionally switch to another.
- After recognizing multiple possibilities, the perception may begin to alternate between them.

Multistability is most commonly studied in **visual perception**, but it can also occur in:

- Auditory perception
- Olfactory perception

## Examples of Multistability

### Arrow or Face Illusion

- The image can be interpreted as:
  - **White arrows**, or
  - **Black shapes resembling faces or statues**.

The interpretation depends on how the viewer organizes the visual elements.

### Necker Cube

- Necker cube: A classic ambiguous cube illusion.

Possible interpretations include:

- A cube facing **south-west**.
- A cube facing **north-east**.
- A **2D pattern of intersecting lines with a central diamond**.

Observers typically alternate between the two cube orientations.

### Duck–Rabbit Illusion

- A famous ambiguous figure that can appear as:
  - A **duck**, or
  - A **rabbit**.

Interpretation often depends on fixation:

- Looking at the **left side** tends to produce the duck perception.
- Looking at the **right side** tends to produce the rabbit perception.

## Perceptual Control and Bias

Although viewers can try to influence perception by:

- Fixating on specific areas
- Thinking about one interpretation
- Reading descriptive labels

The switching between interpretations is largely **outside conscious control**.

External influences can bias perception:

- Descriptive text
- Other people's suggestions
- Contextual cues

However, once multiple interpretations are discovered, the visual system tends to **alternate between them automatically**.

## Stochastic Nature of Multistability

- Stochastic perception: Perceptual changes occur randomly and are difficult to predict.
- Multistable switching is therefore **unpredictable**.

This randomness explains why:

- The brain suddenly shifts between interpretations.
- The timing of these switches varies across observers and situations.

## Importance in Gestalt Psychology

Multistability illustrates a core Gestalt principle:

- Perception is an **active interpretation** performed by the brain.

It demonstrates that:

- The same visual stimulus can generate multiple perceptions.
- The brain determines **how visual information is organized and interpreted**.
