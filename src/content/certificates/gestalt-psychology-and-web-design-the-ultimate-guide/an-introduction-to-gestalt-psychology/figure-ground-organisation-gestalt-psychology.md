## Figure–Ground Organization

### Definition

- Figure–ground organization: A Gestalt perceptual principle describing how humans distinguish a **main element (figure)** from the **background (ground)** in a visual scene.
- Selective attention: The ability to focus on relevant visual elements while filtering out less important parts of the environment.
- Perceptual filtering: The visual system prioritizes certain elements so cognitive effort is directed toward meaningful information.

This mechanism allows people to quickly identify **important objects within complex scenes**.

### How Figure–Ground Perception Works

When observing a visual scene:

- One element is perceived as the **figure** (foreground).
- The remaining visual information becomes the **ground** (background).

Characteristics of figure perception:

- Appears **closer or in front** of other elements.
- Receives the majority of **visual attention**.
- Is interpreted as the **main subject** of the scene.

The interpretation can change depending on where attention is directed.

Example:

- In a black-and-white circular image, the **black region may appear as the figure** while the white area becomes the background.
- Shifting focus can reverse this perception, making the **white region appear as the figure**.

### Role of Attention

Figure–ground distinction depends heavily on **visual attention**.

Key aspects:

- Attention determines which element becomes the figure.
- Elements outside the focus of attention are treated as background.
- Changing attention can **reverse the interpretation** of the same image.

This dynamic perception allows the visual system to **manage complex environments efficiently**.

## Significance of Figure–Ground Organization

### Importance for Human Survival

The ability to isolate objects from their surroundings has historically been essential.

Benefits include:

- Detecting threats (e.g., predators) quickly.
- Identifying relevant objects within complex environments.
- Reducing the time and effort needed to interpret visual scenes.

Without this perceptual ability:

- Every element in the environment would compete equally for attention.
- Visual processing would become slow and inefficient.

### Everyday Applications

Figure–ground perception supports many everyday tasks, such as:

- Locating buttons on machines
- Finding objects within cluttered environments
- Reading text against a background

## Classic Example: Faces–Vase Illusion

One of the most famous demonstrations of figure–ground perception is the **faces–vase illusion**.

In this image:

- The viewer may see **a vase** in the center.
- Alternatively, the viewer may see **two faces** in profile.

The change occurs because of **edge assignment**:

- Edges assigned inward produce the **vase interpretation**.
- Edges assigned outward produce the **two-face interpretation**.

This illusion demonstrates how **attention determines which elements are perceived as figure or ground**.

## Importance in Gestalt Psychology

Figure–ground organization illustrates how perception is structured by the brain.

Key insights:

- The visual system organizes scenes into **foreground and background elements**.
- Attention strongly influences what people perceive.
- Meaning in visual scenes can change depending on **where focus is directed**.
