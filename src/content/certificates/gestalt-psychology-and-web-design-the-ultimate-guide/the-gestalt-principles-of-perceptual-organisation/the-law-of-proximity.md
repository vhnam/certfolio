## Grouping Laws

### Definition

- Law of Proximity: A Gestalt principle stating that elements placed close to each other are perceived as a group.
- Perceptual bias: Humans naturally group items based on spatial distance.

> **Key Idea**
>
> _The law of proximity is a Gestalt principle of perceptual organisation which observes the effect item proximity has on our perception of grouping._

### How It Works

- Items that are close together are perceived as belonging to the same group.
- Spatial arrangement strongly influences perception.
- Grouping occurs even when elements are visually identical.

### Perceptual Mechanism

- Proximity overrides other cues when organizing visual information.
- The brain uses distance between elements to:
  - Infer relationships
  - Create structure
- Both:
  - Distance within a group
  - Distance between groups
    affect perception.

### Example Insight

#### Group Formation

- Closely spaced dots → perceived as a single group.
- Separated dots → perceived as multiple groups.

#### Group Interaction

- When groups move closer together:
  - Boundaries blur
  - Harder to distinguish separate groups
- Elements may be reassigned to nearby groups based on distance.

### Design Implications

- Use spacing to define relationships:
  - Group related elements closer together
  - Separate unrelated elements with more space
- Improves:
  - Visual hierarchy
  - Readability
  - Information structure

### Key Takeaways

- Proximity is a primary driver of visual grouping
- Spacing communicates structure without additional styling
- Distance between and within groups shapes perception
