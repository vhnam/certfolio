## Using AI (Gemini) as a Creative and Strategic Partner at Work

### AI as a Work Partner

- AI collaborator: A tool that helps transform an initial idea into a complete project plan.
- Creative partner: Helps generate new ideas and explore possibilities.
- Strategic partner: Challenges assumptions and evaluates strategies.
- Execution partner: Assists in building structured plans to achieve goals.

AI can support the entire journey from **idea → strategy → execution**, helping teams handle complex projects more effectively.

### Generating Ideas with AI

AI can help expand thinking and uncover new solutions.

Techniques include:

- Simulating experts: Ask AI to respond as industry experts or specialists.
- Creative exploration: Push beyond obvious ideas to discover unexpected solutions.
- Industry knowledge integration: Bring external knowledge and best practices into the brainstorming process.

These approaches help break creative blocks and reveal possibilities that might otherwise be missed.

### Pressure Testing Solutions

Strong ideas must survive real-world challenges.

AI can act as a **devil’s advocate** to test strategies.

Ways to evaluate ideas:

- Risk identification: Ask AI to highlight possible weaknesses or future problems.
- Scenario testing: Explore how plans might fail or face obstacles.
- Constraint application: Add limits (budget, time, resources) to narrow down the most practical solutions.

This process helps teams choose the **most relevant and resilient path forward**.

### Managing Projects with AI Tools

AI can also help organize and manage project knowledge.

- NotebookLM: A tool that creates a shared information hub for project documents.
- Rapid information access: Teammates can ask questions and receive answers instantly.
- Team alignment: Ensures everyone understands goals, decisions, and project context.

This improves collaboration and keeps teams working from the same source of information.

### Key Principles for Using AI Effectively

#### Problem Framing

Before using AI, clearly define:

- The problem you want to solve.
- The current situation.
- The desired outcome.

Clear framing anchors the entire process.

#### Providing Context

AI lacks internal knowledge about your organization.

Important context includes:

- Customer needs
- Team dynamics
- Leadership priorities
- Organizational constraints

This information must come from the user to guide AI toward useful solutions.

#### Human Judgment

AI should support decisions, not replace them.

Key responsibilities of the user:

- Evaluate AI suggestions
- Reject ideas that are not suitable
- Decide the final direction

Being a **critical evaluator** ensures AI outputs remain relevant and practical.

### AI Across the Project Lifecycle

AI can assist at nearly every stage:

1. Idea generation: Discover new concepts and possibilities.
2. Solution evaluation: Test strategies and identify risks.
3. Project planning: Build actionable execution plans.
4. Team coordination: Organize knowledge and keep teams aligned.

This represents a **mindset shift**, where AI becomes a continuous partner throughout the project lifecycle.

## Build with AI: Strategic Plan

### Lab Overview

- Purpose: Use **Gemini** to brainstorm ideas, analyze them, and track industry insights.
- Requirement: Sign into Gemini with a Google Account before starting the lab.
- Approach: Follow the video instructions and customize prompts to match your own work needs.

### Step 1: Brainstorm New Product Ideas

Use Gemini to generate ideas by assigning it a role and providing context.

Prompt structure:

- Persona: The expert role Gemini should simulate.
- Industry: The market or field.
- Product: The product being developed.
- Brainstorm focus: Items, features, or add-ons to explore.
- Purpose: The goal of the brainstorming.

Example components:

```
You are a [persona, e.g. marketing expert] in the [industry, e.g. audio industry]. My company is developing a [new product, e.g. new line of premium headphones]. Help me brainstorm [items to brainstorm, e.g. accessories or add-ons] that I could [purpose, e.g. bundle with this product].
```

Process:

1. Start a new chat with Gemini.
2. Provide the structured prompt.
3. Review the generated ideas for further evaluation.

### Step 2: Create a Cost-Benefit Analysis Table

Evaluate the ideas generated in the previous step using a structured analysis.

Prompt structure:

- Analysis type: Method used to compare options.
- Table format: Define evaluation columns.

Example columns:

```
Perform a [type of analysis, e.g. cost-benefit analysis] for each of these ideas. Format your response as a table with the following columns:

- [Column 1 name, e.g. Idea]
- [Column 2 name, e.g. Cost (low/medium/high)]
- [Column 3 name, e.g. Benefit (low/medium/high)]
```

Purpose:

- Compare options clearly.
- Identify ideas with **high benefit and low cost**.

### Step 3: Research Industry Trends

Use Gemini to gather contextual information that may influence decisions.

Prompt goal:

- Identify current trends related to the product or industry.

Example analysis type:

```
What are some current industry trends around [your product or industry]? I'd like to factor those insights into my decision.
```

Purpose:

- Understand market direction.
- Integrate external insights into the decision-making process.

### Step 4: Schedule Recurring Research

Automate ongoing research to stay informed about industry changes.

Prompt structure:

- Request the same analysis to run at a defined frequency.

Example:

```
Run this analysis [desired frequency, e.g. once a week] so I can stay up to date on these trends.
```

Process:

1. Ask Gemini to schedule the recurring analysis.
2. Confirm the scheduled action.
3. Edit the action name or frequency if needed.
4. Access results from the conversation list.
5. Manage schedules through **Settings → Scheduled actions**.
