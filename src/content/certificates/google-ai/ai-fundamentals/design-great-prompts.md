## Effective Prompting Techniques for AI

### Why Clear Prompts Matter

AI performs best when instructions are clear and detailed.

- Prompting: The process of giving instructions to an AI system to generate a desired output.

Key principles:

- Be clear about what you want.
- Provide enough context for the AI to understand the task.

Poorly defined instructions can lead to inaccurate or irrelevant responses.

### Prompting Framework

A structured prompt helps guide AI toward better results.

Key components:

- Persona: The role or expertise the AI should assume.
- Task: The specific task the AI should perform.
- Format: The structure or style of the output.
- Context: Background information needed to complete the task accurately.

Example structure:

- Persona: Experienced project leader.
- Task: Provide advice on improving meetings.
- Format: Bullet-point list.
- Context: Cross-functional teams struggling to make decisions during status meetings.

### Reviewing and Refining AI Outputs

Always evaluate AI responses before using them.

Best practices:

- Review the AI output for accuracy and usefulness.
- Add additional context if the response needs improvement.
- Ask AI to update or refine its answer based on new information.

Example refinement:

- Adding details about teams working across different time zones.

### Providing Examples and References

Examples help AI better understand the expected output.

Methods include:

- Attaching sample documents or files.
- Sharing previous meeting agendas or reports.
- Providing reference materials.

This improves the accuracy and relevance of the AI’s response.

### Using Multimodal Inputs

Many AI systems support multiple input types.

- Multimodal prompting: Including different forms of media in a prompt.

Examples of inputs:

- Text instructions
- Documents
- Images
- Websites

Visual references can clarify expectations more effectively than text alone.

Example tool:

- Gemini supports multimodal prompts.

### Iterating to Improve Results

Effective prompting often requires multiple interactions.

- Iteration: Refining prompts through repeated adjustments.

Techniques include:

1. Editing the original prompt:
   - Add more detail or change wording.
2. Breaking tasks into smaller steps:
   - Ask for principles first.
   - Then request specific applications.
3. Asking follow-up questions:
   - Refine outputs based on earlier responses.

This approach helps AI reason through complex tasks more effectively.

### Adding Constraints to Prompts

- Constraints: Specific requirements that limit or guide the AI’s output.

Examples:

- Provide solutions that can be implemented within one week.
- Do not recommend increasing meeting length.

Constraints ensure outputs align with practical requirements.

### Managing Chat Context

AI conversations accumulate context within a chat session.

- Context window: The amount of previous conversation the AI remembers and uses when generating responses.

Best practices:

- Use one chat per topic to maintain clarity.
- Start a new chat when switching to a different task or subject.

This prevents unrelated context from affecting results.

### Developing Strong Prompting Skills

Prompting improves with practice and experimentation.

Benefits of strong prompting skills:

- More accurate AI outputs
- Better collaboration with AI systems
- Ability to solve complex problems through iterative conversations

Over time, prompting evolves from simple commands into collaborative dialogue with AI.

## Prompting Tips and Best Practices

### Importance of Prompt Structure

Effective prompting improves the quality of AI responses.

Key idea:

- Well-structured prompts help AI understand the intended task and generate better outputs.

Example tool:

- Gemini can produce better results when prompts follow clear structure and guidance.

Prompting works best when combined with an iterative approach and a structured framework that includes:

- Persona
- Task
- Format
- Context

### The Three C’s of Prompting

When writing prompts, follow three key principles.

#### Be Concise

- Concise prompts: Short, focused instructions without unnecessary complexity.

Benefits:

- Easier for AI to process
- Reduces ambiguity
- Improves response speed

#### Be Clear

- Clear prompts: Specific instructions that avoid vague or ambiguous language.

Example:

- Vague prompt: “Make this better.”
- Clear prompt: “Rewrite this paragraph to sound more professional and concise.”

#### Be Consistent

- Consistent terminology: Use the same words for the same concepts throughout a conversation.

Example:

- If you refer to a “spreadsheet,” continue using that term rather than switching to words like “matrix” or “sheet.”

Consistency helps AI maintain contextual understanding.

### Evaluating AI Outputs

AI responses should always be reviewed before use.

Evaluation criteria:

- Accuracy: Verify that the information is factually correct.
- Bias: Identify potential unfair perspectives or stereotypes.
- Relevance: Confirm that the response directly addresses the prompt.
- Consistency: Ensure tone, style, and quality remain stable throughout the response.

AI outputs should be treated as drafts rather than final results.

### Improving Results Through Iteration

AI outputs often improve through multiple rounds of refinement.

- Iteration: The process of repeatedly adjusting prompts and responses to achieve better results.

Techniques for iteration:

- Revisit the prompting framework:
  - Add details to persona, task, format, or context.
- Break complex tasks into smaller steps:
  - Ask for components of the task individually.
- Add constraints:
  - Specify requirements the response must follow.

Iteration allows users to guide AI toward more accurate and useful outputs.

### Using References in Prompts

References provide examples of the desired output.

Examples of references:

- Sample text documents
- Images
- Audio
- Video

Purpose:

- Clarify the expected tone, style, format, or structure of the result.

When using references, explain how the example relates to the requested task.

### Managing Chat Context

AI systems rely on a **context window** to remember information within a conversation.

- Context window: The amount of previous conversation the AI can reference when generating responses.

Best practice:

- Start a new chat when switching topics.

Reason:

- Prevent unrelated context from influencing the AI’s response.

### Building a Prompt Library

Prompting skills improve with practice and experimentation.

Recommended strategy:

- Save effective prompts that produce good results.
- Reuse and adapt these prompts for future tasks.

Benefits:

- Faster workflows
- More consistent results
- Improved AI collaboration over time.

## Applying the Prompting Framework in Gemini

### Purpose of the Lab

- Goal: Practice using a structured prompting framework to guide AI responses.
- Tool used: Gemini.
- Recommendation:
  - Sign in with a Google account to save activity and access additional features.
  - Customize prompts based on your personal goals or work context.

### Step 1: Define the Initial Task

Start a new chat and describe a broad goal.

Process:

1. Open a new chat in Gemini.
2. Provide a high-level objective.
3. Ask AI to brainstorm possible ideas.

Example task:

```
I want to learn a new skill to [your high-level goal, e.g. advance my career, get a new job], but I'm not sure where to start. Help me brainstorm some ideas.
```

Purpose:

- Generate initial ideas that will be refined later.

### Step 2: Use Meta-Prompting to Improve Suggestions

- Meta-prompting: Asking AI how to improve its own response.

Process:

1. Ask Gemini what additional questions it needs answered.
2. Review the questions generated by the AI.

Purpose:

- Identify missing context needed to produce better suggestions.

Example task:

```
What questions can I answer to help you tailor your suggestions?
```

### Step 3: Add Personal Context

Provide detailed information about yourself to refine AI results.

Process:

1. Answer the questions generated in Step 2.
2. Share relevant details such as interests, goals, or experience.
3. Ask AI to recommend a small number of relevant skills.

Purpose:

- Tailor AI recommendations to your specific situation.

Example task:

```
Here are some more details about me:

- [Answer 1]

- [Answer 2]

- [Answer 3]

- [Answer 4]

Based on this information, suggest [number, e.g. three] skills that would be relevant to me.
```

### Step 4: Apply a Persona

Use a role-based perspective to evaluate the results.

- Persona: A role or expertise assigned to AI to guide its reasoning.

Process:

1. Ask AI to adopt a specific role.
2. Request an evaluation of the suggested skills.

Example personas:

- Career coach
- Hiring manager

Purpose:

- Receive recommendations from a specialized perspective.

Example task:

```
Now, act as a [specific persona, e.g. career coach or hiring manager]. Which of the skills above would you recommend I focus on first? Explain your reasoning.
```

### Step 5: Define Actionable Next Steps

Turn the chosen skill into a practical plan.

Process:

1. Ask AI for specific actions to start learning the skill.
2. Set a timeframe for completing the actions.
3. Specify a format for the output.

Example format:

- Table with defined columns such as tasks, resources, and deadlines.

Purpose:

- Convert ideas into concrete actions.

Example task:

```
Suggest [number, e.g. three] actions I can take [timeframe, e.g. this week] to start learning that skill. Format this as a [output format, e.g. table] with [format details, e.g. column names].
```

### Applying the Prompting Framework

This lab demonstrates how to combine several prompting techniques:

- Task definition
- Meta-prompting
- Context enrichment
- Persona-based reasoning
- Structured outputs

Practicing these techniques helps guide AI toward more relevant and actionable results.

## Improving AI Prompts with Follow-Up Questions

### Adding Context to Prompts

Providing detailed context at the beginning of a prompt helps AI generate more relevant outputs.

Benefits include:

- Better understanding of the task
- More accurate recommendations
- Reduced need for repeated corrections

Context can include:

- Project goals
- Constraints
- Background information
- Desired outcomes

### Encouraging Clarifying Questions

A useful prompting technique is asking AI to request clarification before completing a task.

Example instruction:

- Ask the AI to pose follow-up questions if important details are missing.

Purpose:

- Identify gaps in information before generating a full response.
- Allow the user to provide additional details early in the process.

### Benefits of Clarification Prompts

Encouraging AI to ask questions improves collaboration.

Advantages include:

- More accurate final outputs
- Fewer revisions after the result is generated
- More efficient workflow

Instead of producing a full plan immediately, the AI first verifies whether it has enough information to proceed.

### Improving Iterative Collaboration

This approach reduces unnecessary iterations.

Typical workflow:

1. Provide context in the initial prompt.
2. Ask AI to identify missing information.
3. Answer follow-up questions.
4. Let the AI generate the final output.

Result:

- The output is more likely to meet expectations on the first attempt.
