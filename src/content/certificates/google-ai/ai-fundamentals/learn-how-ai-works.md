## Understanding How AI Works

### AI in Everyday Work

- AI usage example: Creating a first draft of a follow-up email after a meeting.
- Benefits of AI assistance:
  - Capturing action items automatically
  - Organizing and formatting information
  - Saving time when preparing communications

Important reminder:

- AI may feel powerful or “magical,” but it is ultimately a computer program with both capabilities and limitations.

### What Artificial Intelligence Means

- Artificial Intelligence (AI): The concept of building intelligent machines that can perform tasks such as reasoning, problem-solving, and making recommendations.

AI systems simulate human-like abilities by processing large amounts of data and identifying patterns.

### AI Models

- AI models: Computer programs trained on large datasets such as articles, images, or videos.

Functions of AI models:

- Detect patterns in training data
- Apply those patterns to new situations
- Generate predictions or new content

### Model Training

- Training: The process where AI models learn patterns from data to improve performance over time.

Example:

- Email spam filters learn from large datasets to distinguish important emails from spam.
- User feedback (e.g., marking emails as spam) helps improve accuracy.

### Importance of Training Data

- Training data: The dataset used to teach an AI model how to perform tasks.

Training data affects model performance.

Example scenario:

- An AI model trained only on images of red apples may struggle to recognize green or yellow apples.

Implication:

- Limited or biased training data can lead to incorrect predictions or decisions.

### Differences Between AI Models

AI models can produce different results because of:

- Training data: The information used to train the model.
- Model architecture: The design and rules used to process and learn from data.

Different combinations of these factors lead to varying model capabilities.

### Large Language Models (LLMs)

- Large Language Model (LLM): An AI model trained on massive amounts of text data to understand and generate human-like language.

Capabilities include:

- Understanding complex requests
- Generating written content
- Producing responses to unfamiliar prompts

Example:

- Gemini uses advanced AI models to process and generate language-based responses.

### Generative AI

- Generative AI (GenAI): AI systems capable of creating new content based on learned patterns.

Examples of generated content:

- Blog posts
- Poems
- Reports
- Images

Generative AI predicts patterns from training data to produce original outputs.

### Multimodal AI

- Multimodal AI: AI systems capable of working with multiple types of data beyond text.

Supported input and output formats:

- Text
- Images
- Audio
- Video

Example tasks:

- Summarizing a chart from an image
- Converting meeting recordings into action items
- Creating visuals for presentations.

### AI Agents

- AI agents: Systems that combine advanced AI models with tools such as calendars, email, or other applications.

Capabilities of AI agents:

1. Accept a high-level goal.
2. Break the goal into smaller tasks.
3. Execute actions using available tools.

Example scenario:

- Organizing a project kickoff meeting.

Possible agent tasks:

- Checking team calendars
- Booking meeting rooms
- Drafting emails
- Sending calendar invitations

### Importance of Understanding AI

Understanding how AI works helps users:

- Use AI tools more effectively
- Recognize AI limitations
- Make informed decisions when collaborating with AI systems.

## Fundamentals of Machine Learning

### Artificial Intelligence vs Machine Learning

- Artificial Intelligence (AI): A broad field focused on building systems that perform tasks typically associated with human intelligence.

Examples of AI tasks:

- Writing emails
- Recommending driving routes
- Generating content or suggestions

Key idea:

- AI systems rely on the data they were trained on to produce results.
- Machine Learning (ML): A technique used to train AI systems so they can learn patterns from data and improve automatically.

Relationship:

- AI: The overall field.
- Machine learning: One of the main methods used to build AI systems.

### How Machine Learning Works

Machine learning enables systems to learn from large datasets rather than being explicitly programmed for every scenario.

Example process:

1. Training: A model is given many examples (e.g., images of zebras and horses).
2. Pattern detection: The model learns identifying features (e.g., zebra stripes).
3. Inference: The trained model applies what it learned to new, unseen data.

- Inference: The process where a trained AI model makes predictions or decisions using new data.

### Approaches to Machine Learning

There are three main approaches used to train machine learning models.

#### Supervised Learning

- Supervised learning: Training a model using datasets that have been labeled by humans.

Characteristics:

- Each training example has a known correct answer.
- The model learns to map inputs to specific outputs.

Example:

- Images labeled **“zebra”** or **“horse”** are used to train the model to classify new images.

#### Unsupervised Learning

- Unsupervised learning: Training a model using data that has not been labeled.

Characteristics:

- The model discovers patterns and structures on its own.
- There is no predefined output.

Example:

- Analyzing images of animals and grouping them based on shared visual features such as striped or solid patterns.

#### Reinforcement Learning

- Reinforcement learning: Training a model through trial and error using a reward system.

Characteristics:

- Correct decisions receive rewards.
- Incorrect decisions receive penalties.
- The model gradually improves its strategy over time.

Example:

- A system learning to identify zebras in videos by receiving rewards when predictions are correct.

### Combining Machine Learning Approaches

Modern AI systems often combine multiple machine learning approaches.

Benefits:

- Improved accuracy
- Better ability to handle complex tasks
- Capability to generate multiple forms of content

Applications include generating:

- Text
- Images
- Video
- Other types of digital content.

## Understanding AI Limitations

### Why AI Limitations Matter

Responsible AI usage requires understanding how AI works and where it can fail.

Common limitations include:

- Bias in outputs
- Knowledge cutoff in training data
- Performance changes over time (drift)

Recognizing these limitations helps users evaluate AI responses more critically and use AI responsibly.

### Bias in AI Systems

- Data bias: When training data is incomplete, skewed, or reflects societal or historical biases.

Impact:

- AI models may reproduce stereotypes or unfair patterns present in their training data.

Example scenario:

- Asking AI to generate an image of an office may repeatedly produce high-rise buildings because the training data mostly reflects urban workplaces.

Human responsibility:

- Guide the AI toward fair, balanced, and inclusive outputs.

Techniques to reduce bias:

- Be specific about the output you want:
  - Provide context about the audience and use case.
  - Include balanced examples or references.
- Use follow-up prompts:
  - Identify potential stereotypes.
  - Ask the AI to correct biased or inaccurate responses.

### Knowledge Cutoff

- Knowledge cutoff: The point in time when a model’s training data stops.

Implication:

- AI may lack information about events or discoveries that occurred after that date.

Key distinction:

- What the model **knows**: Information from its training data.
- What the model can **look up**: Information retrieved through external tools such as web search.

Best practices for working with knowledge cutoffs:

- Look up the cutoff date:
  - Check when the AI model’s training data ends.
- Verify time-sensitive information:
  - Cross-check news, statistics, or recent events using reliable sources.
- Specify the timeframe:
  - Ask questions with a clear time reference.
- Refine with follow-up prompts:
  - Request updated or corrected information when responses appear outdated.

### Changes in AI Performance (Drift)

- Drift: A gradual decline in model accuracy or changes in behavior as real-world conditions evolve.

Types of drift:

- Factual drift:
  - Occurs when information becomes outdated over time.
  - Example: Advice about fashion trends becoming less relevant.

- Behavioral drift:
  - Changes in AI responses caused by updates to the model.
  - Example: Different tone, formatting, or style even with the same prompt.

### Managing AI Drift

Techniques to reduce the impact of drift:

- Provide up-to-date context in prompts, especially for fast-changing topics.
- Keep chats focused by starting a new conversation for each task.
- Use clear and specific instructions when writing prompts.

### Responsible AI Usage

Effective AI use combines human judgment with AI capabilities.

Best practices include:

- Evaluating AI outputs critically
- Identifying potential biases
- Verifying information with reliable sources
- Refining prompts to improve accuracy and fairness

Human oversight ensures AI outputs are accurate, responsible, and appropriate for real-world use.

## Using AI Effectively at Work

### Common Ways AI Supports Work

AI can assist with several everyday professional tasks.

Key capabilities include:

- Understanding information:
  - Summarizing long reports
  - Extracting insights from spreadsheets
  - Learning about unfamiliar industries
- Creating new content:
  - Drafting communications
  - Building presentation outlines
  - Generating images or visual materials
- Improving existing work:
  - Reviewing project plans
  - Suggesting improvements to proposals
  - Offering alternative ideas and perspectives

These capabilities help people make better decisions and improve productivity.

### Communicating with AI Using Natural Language

AI systems understand instructions written or spoken in everyday language.

- Natural language: Human language used in normal communication.

Examples of interaction:

- Typing instructions in a chat interface
- Speaking commands to an AI assistant

This makes AI accessible without requiring technical expertise.

### Prompts, Inputs, and Outputs

Effective AI usage depends on clear instructions.

- Prompt: The instruction given to AI describing the task.
- Input: All information included in the prompt, such as text, files, or documents.
- Output: The response or result generated by AI.

Clear and specific prompts improve the quality of AI outputs.

### AI Models and AI Applications

AI systems consist of underlying models and user-facing applications.

Example:

- Gemini: A set of advanced AI models capable of reasoning and generating responses.
- Gemini: The application interface where users interact with the AI model.

Analogy:

- AI model: Like a car engine performing the computation.
- AI app: The car itself that users operate.

Gemini models also power other products such as:

- Google Search
- Google Maps
- Google Pixel

### Choosing the Right AI Model

Different AI models are optimized for different tasks.

Examples:

- General-purpose models:
  - Quick assistance with everyday tasks
- Specialized models:
  - Advanced research
  - Coding
  - Complex decision analysis

More complex tasks may require more computational processing time.

### Customizing AI Behavior

Some AI tools provide settings to control how responses are generated.

Example platform:

- Google AI Studio

One common parameter:

- Temperature: A setting that controls the creativity of AI responses.

Temperature effects:

- Low temperature:
  - More predictable
  - More consistent responses
- High temperature:
  - More creative
  - Less predictable responses

### Key Principle of AI Responses

AI systems generate answers by predicting likely outputs based on patterns in training data.

Implication:

- AI does not “know” information in the human sense.
- It produces responses by estimating the most probable answer from available patterns.

Understanding this principle helps users experiment with prompts and settings to achieve better results.
