## Collaborating with AI at Work

### Mindset Shift: From Tool to Collaborator

- Traditional AI usage: Using AI for small, isolated tasks such as generating names or drafting messages.
- Collaborative AI usage: Working with AI to help solve complex problems across multiple steps.

Key idea:

- AI becomes more valuable when used as a thinking partner rather than just a task automation tool.

### Example: Using AI for Business Strategy

Instead of only drafting an announcement message, AI can support broader strategic tasks.

AI can help with:

- Forecasting business outcomes
- Generating ideas for increasing sales
- Pressure-testing assumptions
- Identifying potential risks
- Exploring different revenue scenarios

This approach supports better decision-making rather than simply completing a single task.

### Problem-Solving with AI

Effective AI collaboration involves:

1. Defining the problem: Clearly explain the goal or challenge.
2. Exploring solutions: Ask AI to generate ideas, scenarios, or strategies.
3. Evaluating outputs: Review AI suggestions critically.
4. Refining decisions: Use insights to support informed choices.

AI helps navigate multiple steps of a problem-solving process.

### The Human Role in AI Collaboration

Human expertise remains essential when working with AI.

Key human strengths:

- Context awareness: Understanding the situation and business environment.
- Emotional intelligence: Knowing the needs and expectations of people involved.
- Judgment: Deciding whether AI-generated solutions are appropriate.

Examples:

- Presentations: AI can create slides, but only humans know the audience.
- Customer service: AI can suggest responses, but humans decide what should be shared with customers.

### Limitations of AI

AI should not be used for certain sensitive or high-risk topics.

Examples include:

- Financial advice
- Legal advice
- Compliance decisions

For these cases, consulting a qualified professional is recommended.

### Using AI Effectively in Daily Work

The goal of AI is not to increase workload but to improve how work is done.

AI can help by:

- Supporting existing tasks more efficiently
- Enabling new capabilities that were previously difficult or impossible
- Improving decision-making and problem-solving processes.

## Three Ways to Collaborate with AI

### Collaboration Mindset

- AI collaboration: Working with AI through ongoing back-and-forth interaction to improve ideas and outcomes.
- Traditional AI usage: Using AI only for one-off tasks like writing emails or quick brainstorming.

Key principle:

- Humans remain in control of decisions while AI provides suggestions, options, and perspectives.

Human responsibilities include:

- Providing context
- Evaluating AI outputs
- Choosing the best solutions

### AI for Understanding Information

AI can help process and organize complex information.

Capabilities include:

- Breaking down complex topics into simpler parts
- Highlighting key points
- Revealing connections or patterns

Example use case:

- Summarizing industry trends to support writing a project proposal.

### AI for Creating New Content

AI can act as a creative partner when generating new material.

Examples of creative tasks:

- Writing text or content drafts
- Visualizing data for charts
- Generating images
- Brainstorming phrasing for ideas

Example use case:

- Producing the first draft of a project proposal.

### AI for Improving and Refining Work

AI can assist with reviewing and improving existing work.

Common improvements include:

- Checking clarity and readability
- Detecting errors
- Suggesting stronger messaging

Example use case:

- Reviewing a sales pitch and identifying confusing explanations.

### Benefits of AI Collaboration

Collaborating with AI helps professionals:

- Think more clearly about complex problems
- Create content more confidently
- Refine work to achieve higher quality results

Effective collaboration involves continuous interaction rather than single requests.

### Responsible AI Usage

When using public AI tools, avoid sharing sensitive information.

Sensitive data examples:

- Credit card numbers
- Social security numbers
- Confidential personal or company data

Best practice:

- Before using AI, check whether the information being shared should remain private.

## Using AI to Overcome Difficult Tasks

### Start by Practicing with AI Tools

- Practical use: The best way to learn AI is to start actively using AI tools.
- Skill development: Regular interaction with AI helps train your thinking and workflow.

Key idea:

- Familiarity with AI grows through consistent experimentation and practice.

### Using AI to Tackle Difficult Problems

AI can help when facing complex or frustrating tasks.

Typical signal:

- Feeling resistance toward a task (e.g., “I don’t want to do this”).

In these situations, AI can help by:

- Breaking through the initial mental barrier
- Generating starting ideas
- Making the task feel more manageable

### AI as a Starting Point

AI does not need to complete the entire task.

Instead, it can:

- Help start the process
- Provide early momentum
- Reduce the difficulty of getting started

Key benefit:

- AI helps “get the ball rolling,” allowing you to continue the work more easily.
