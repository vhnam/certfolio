## Why Service Design Matters

Service design focuses on creating **holistic and integrated service experiences** across every interaction a customer has with an organization. Instead of designing isolated products, service design ensures that **all touchpoints—from start to finish—work together to deliver a coherent and effective experience**.

Because people interact with services daily, improving service experiences can significantly benefit both **customers and organizations**. Well-designed services increase efficiency, satisfaction, and competitive advantage.

Service design builds on **human-centered design and UX principles**, expanding them to consider the **entire ecosystem of interactions**, including customers, employees, processes, and business systems.

## Course Structure

The course is divided into **five lessons**, each focusing on a key aspect of service design.

### Lesson 1 — Foundations of Service Design

- What service design is and where it comes from
- Why service design creates business value
- Key terminology and concepts
- How service design compares with UX design
- How designers can transition from UX to service design

### Lesson 2 — Setting Up a Service Design Project

- Establishing collaboration and stakeholder alignment
- Preparing a project structure that supports service innovation

### Lesson 3 — Research and Understanding Users

- Conducting service design research
- Creating **personas**
- Developing **customer journey maps**

### Lesson 4 — Mapping and Prototyping Services

- Designing service blueprints
- Prototyping service interactions
- Testing and refining service concepts

### Lesson 5 — Organizational Service Design Capability

- Evaluating service design maturity
- Developing service design culture and skills inside organizations

## Portfolio Project: Designing a Vending Machine Service

To reinforce learning, the course offers an **optional practical project** where learners design a service around a **food or drink vending machine**—essentially creating the **“Starbucks of vending machines.”**

The project covers the entire service ecosystem, including:

1. **Service ethnography**
   Studying and mapping an existing service to identify user needs and opportunities.

2. **Service concept and business model design**
   Defining the value proposition and creating a **service blueprint**.

3. **Prototyping and testing**
   Developing and evaluating the service design.

The project is divided across lessons:

- **Lesson 3:** Research and value proposition development
- **Lesson 4:** Service design development and testing

Participants are encouraged to **document each step** with notes, photos, and illustrations so the work can become a **portfolio case study**.
