## Understanding Service Design

### Definition

- Service design: The practice of designing **end-to-end service experiences**, including both customer interactions and the systems that support them.
- Service experience: A sequence of **interactions between customers, staff, and systems** across different touchpoints.
- Service interaction: A moment where a customer engages with part of the service ecosystem.

Service design examines two major areas:

- Customer experience: What customers experience throughout the service journey.
- Supporting systems: Internal processes and infrastructure that enable the experience.

## Service Design vs UX Design

### Key Differences

- UX design: Primarily focuses on **digital interactions** with products or interfaces.
- Service design: Focuses on **all interactions across the service ecosystem**, including physical and human interactions.

Service design interactions may include:

- Digital interfaces
- Human-to-human interactions
- Physical environments
- Organizational processes

### Broader Definition of Users

- Users: Not only customers but also **staff delivering the service**.

Service design must consider:

- Customers
- Service employees
- Internal teams
- Supporting systems

## Theater Metaphor in Service Design

Service design is often explained using a **theater metaphor** to understand how services are staged.

### Core Elements

- Setting: The environment where the service interaction occurs.
- Actors: The people involved in the interaction (customers and staff).
- Props: The tools or touchpoints used during the service interaction.
- Script: The actions and behaviors expected from actors.

Service design focuses on designing:

- The **sequence of interactions**
- The elements supporting each interaction

## Frontstage and Backstage in Service Design

### Frontstage

- Frontstage: The parts of the service **visible to the customer**.

Examples:

- Talking to a service employee
- Using a kiosk or website
- Visiting a store or service location

Frontstage interactions form the **customer experience**.

### Backstage

- Backstage: Internal processes and systems **invisible to the customer** but essential for delivering the service.

Examples:

- Databases
- Logistics systems
- Supply chains
- Internal operations

Backstage systems ensure that the **frontstage experience works smoothly**.

## Lines in Service Design

Service designers use two conceptual boundaries to understand services.

### Line of Visibility

- Line of visibility: Separates **what customers can see** from what happens behind the scenes.

Above the line:

- Customer interactions
- Visible staff activities

Below the line:

- Internal systems
- Organizational processes

### Line of Interaction

- Line of interaction: Marks the point where **customers interact with service staff or systems**.

This line helps designers analyze how customers connect with the service.

## Importance of Backstage Systems

Backstage systems play a critical role because they:

- Enable frontstage service delivery
- Coordinate internal operations
- Support staff during interactions

Poor backstage design can cause:

- Service delays
- Miscommunication
- Inconsistent customer experiences

Therefore, service designers must design **both frontstage and backstage elements together**.

## Organizational Readiness for Service Design

Implementing service design often requires **organizational change**.

Challenges include:

- Existing organizational culture
- Legacy systems
- Regulatory constraints
- Technological limitations

Successful service design requires:

- Organizational willingness to adopt new processes
- Collaboration across departments
- Long-term commitment to improving services

## Example: Kudoz Learning Platform

### Overview

- Kudoz: A learning platform that connects people with developmental disabilities to community members who host learning experiences.

Platform features:

- Online platform for discovering experiences
- Mobile app for booking and participation
- Community-based learning activities

## Role of Service Design in Kudoz

Service designers focus on **the entire service ecosystem**, not only the digital platform.

### Service Touchpoints

Key interactions include:

- Community events introducing the platform
- Informational materials and presentations
- Volunteer onboarding processes
- Learning experience coordination

Many of these interactions occur **outside the digital platform**.

## Service Designer Responsibilities

Service designers design elements such as:

- Staff roles and responsibilities
- Interaction scripts for employees
- Communication materials
- Training and onboarding processes
- Safety and verification procedures

Example tasks include:

- Designing how volunteer hosts are introduced to the platform
- Creating materials used during onboarding meetings
- Structuring the interactions between staff and participants

## Collaboration with UX Designers

In the Kudoz example:

- UX designers focus on **digital products** such as the platform and mobile app.
- Service designers focus on **the broader service system**, including offline interactions.

Service designers ensure:

- All interactions across channels align
- The overall experience remains **coherent and seamless**

## Key Takeaways

- Service design focuses on **the entire service ecosystem**, not just interfaces.
- The **theater metaphor** helps explain how services are staged.
- Frontstage interactions create the **customer experience**.
- Backstage systems support the **delivery of that experience**.
- Service designers coordinate **people, processes, and technology** to create a seamless service journey.
