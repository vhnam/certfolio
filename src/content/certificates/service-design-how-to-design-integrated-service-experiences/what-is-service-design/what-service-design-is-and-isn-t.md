## What Service Design Is

### Definition

- Service Design: The discipline of designing **end-to-end service experiences** across multiple channels and touchpoints.
- Omni-channel service: A service experience that spans **multiple channels** such as websites, mobile apps, call centers, physical stores, or printed materials.
- Systems thinking: A perspective that considers the **entire system**, including technology, processes, and people involved in delivering the service.

Key characteristics of service design:

- Considers the **entire service ecosystem**
- Goes beyond UI or digital products
- Designs both **frontstage experiences** and **backstage processes**
- Aligns **users, business goals, and operational systems**

### Requirements of Good Service Design

Service design must be:

- Exact: Prevent failures caused by miscommunication or unclear processes.
- Feasible: Possible for the organization to implement operationally.
- Scalable: Able to serve large numbers of users.
- Financially viable: Sustainable from a business perspective.

## How Service Design Differs from Other Design Approaches

### UX Design vs Service Design

- UX Design: Focuses on the experience of **products**, often digital interfaces.
- Service Design: Focuses on the **entire service journey**, including multiple channels and internal processes.

### CX Design vs Service Design

- Customer Experience (CX): Focuses on improving the **customer’s journey and interactions**.
- Service Design: Includes CX but also designs the **systems, processes, and infrastructure** that enable those experiences.

### Design Thinking vs Service Design

- Design Thinking: A **problem-solving methodology**.
- Service Design: A **specialized discipline** that often uses design thinking methods.

## The Service Concept

![Service Blueprint](../../../../assets/images/service-design-how-to-design-integrated-service-experiences/what-is-service-design/service-blueprint.avif)

### Definition

- Service Concept: The central idea that defines **how a service creates value and how it will be delivered**.

![Service concept](../../../../assets/images/service-design-how-to-design-integrated-service-experiences/what-is-service-design/service-concept.avif)

A service concept typically includes four elements:

1. Customer needs and desires: Understanding what customers want or need.
2. Organizational strategy: The strategic intentions of the service provider.
3. Service benefits (the _what_): The value customers receive from the service.
4. Service delivery (the _how_): The processes and systems that deliver the service.

### Purpose of a Service Concept

A strong service concept:

- Aligns **customers, stakeholders, and service providers**
- Reduces the gap between **expected and delivered experiences**
- Guides **innovation and development of new services**
- Helps organizations transition from the **current state to a future service vision**

### Common Service Concept Artifacts

Service concepts may be represented through design artifacts such as:

- Stakeholder maps
- Customer journey maps
- Service blueprints

These artifacts help teams understand and design the service ecosystem.

## Service Design Is Everywhere

Service design exists in many everyday experiences.

Examples include:

- Airline baggage tracking systems
- Mobile apps for service monitoring
- Restaurant service experiences
- Online purchasing flows

Good service design:

- Improves the **customer experience**
- Optimizes **internal processes and efficiency**
- Generates **return on investment (ROI)** for organizations

Poor service design can cause:

- Confusing pricing or hidden fees
- Broken customer journeys
- Poor customer service interactions
- Loss of customer trust and loyalty

## Service Design at the Intersection of CX, UX and Customer Service

Service design integrates three related areas:

### Customer Service

- Focus: Supporting customers when they need help.
- Examples: Call centers, support desks, problem resolution.

### Customer Experience (CX)

- Focus: The **overall journey and perception** customers have while interacting with a brand.

### User Experience (UX)

- Focus: The **usability and interaction design** of interfaces such as apps or websites.

### Role of Service Design

Service design sits at the **intersection of these three disciplines** and orchestrates them to deliver a **coherent, seamless service experience**.

Key idea:

- Service design manages the **continuity of experience across channels**.

Example of cross-channel interaction:

- Website → mobile app → call center → physical location → printed forms

Unlike UX design, service design may involve **digital interfaces, physical interactions, organizational processes, and policies**.

## Omni-Channel Service Experience

### Definition

- Omni-channel experience: A continuous service journey where users move across different channels without friction.

Examples of channels:

- Websites
- Mobile apps
- Call centers
- Physical offices
- Printed forms or documents

Service design ensures that these channels work **together as a single coherent system** rather than isolated interactions.

## Why Organizations Invest in Service Design

Organizations adopt service design to:

- Improve **customer satisfaction**
- Increase **operational efficiency**
- Reduce **service failures and friction**
- Create **competitive differentiation**

Many industries—including **government services, transportation, and retail**—are increasingly adopting service design to modernize outdated service systems.
