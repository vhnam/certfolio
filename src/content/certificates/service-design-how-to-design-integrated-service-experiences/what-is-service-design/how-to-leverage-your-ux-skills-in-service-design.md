## Relationship Between UX and Service Design

Service design is closely related to UX design and can be seen as an **extension of UX** rather than a completely separate discipline. The transition from UX to service design is often described as a **lateral move**, meaning the skills and methods largely overlap.

Both fields share many techniques such as:

- User research
- Personas and ethnography
- Journey mapping
- Prototyping

Because of this overlap, UX designers can often transition into service design by expanding their existing skills.

## Key Differences

The main difference lies in **scope and focus**.

**UX Design**

- Focuses mainly on **digital products and interfaces** (UI).
- Usually centered on **a specific device or product experience**.
- Often works on **cross-device experiences**.

**Service Design**

- Focuses on the **entire service ecosystem**.
- Considers **both frontstage and backstage processes**.
- Designs **omni-channel experiences**, including digital, physical, and human interactions.
- Looks at how the **organization supports the service internally**.

In short, UX design focuses on **product experiences**, while service design focuses on **holistic service experiences across an organization**.

## Skills That Help UX Designers Transition

Several UX skills are directly useful in service design:

### User Research

UX designers who already conduct **field research and user testing** are well positioned to move into service design.

### Prototyping

Both disciplines rely on **low-fidelity prototyping**, such as:

- Paper prototypes
- Clickable prototypes

In service design, prototypes may also include **service interactions**, physical environments, or organizational processes.

### Cross-Channel Thinking

Service design requires designers to consider **multiple touchpoints**, such as:

- Mobile apps
- Websites
- Call centers
- Physical locations
- Paper forms or documents

This broader view is known as **omni-channel design**.

## Collaboration in Service Design

Service design typically involves **close collaboration across teams**. For example, a team might include:

- A **UX researcher**
- A **service designer**
- A **UX designer**

Together they conduct research, interview users and stakeholders, analyze insights, and design solutions. Service designers often connect **customer insights with organizational processes** to ensure the entire service works effectively.

## Key Takeaway

Service design and UX design share many tools and methods, but **service design expands the focus beyond the interface**. It examines the **entire service system**, including customers, employees, internal processes, and multiple channels, to create a seamless end-to-end experience.
