## Why Value Proposition Matters

- Value proposition = core of service design and business model
- Helps clarify:
  - Customer pain points
  - Business objectives
  - How value is actually delivered
- Prevents jumping too quickly into solutions without clear purpose

## Value Proposition Template (Mad-lib)

- Simple way to articulate value clearly:

> “Our **_ helps _** who want to **_ by _** and **_ (unlike _**).”

- Purpose:
  - Define target users
  - Clarify their goals
  - Explain how the service helps
  - Differentiate from competitors

## Value Proposition Canvas Overview

- A detailed extension of the value proposition in the business model
- Focus: Achieve **fit between customer needs and service offering**
- Requires prior **user research** to avoid assumptions

### Two Main Parts

#### 1. Customer Profile (Right Side)

![Customer Profile](../../../../assets/images/service-design-how-to-design-integrated-service-experiences/how-to-prepare-your-service-design-team/customer-profile.avif)

- **Customer Jobs (Tasks)**
  - Functional: practical needs
  - Emotional: feelings desired
  - Social: perception by others
- **Pains**
  - Problems, risks, frustrations when completing tasks
- **Gains**
  - Desired outcomes and success metrics

#### 2. Value Map (Left Side)

![Value Map](../../../../assets/images/service-design-how-to-design-integrated-service-experiences/how-to-prepare-your-service-design-team/value-map.avif)

- **Products & Services**
  - What you offer to the customer
- **Pain Relievers**
  - How you reduce customer frustrations
- **Gain Creators**
  - How you create additional value or benefits

## Example Insight (Tesla Case)

- Target: women with children
- Findings:
  - Gains matched well (eco-friendly, status)
  - Pain relievers incomplete (cost, usability, space)
- Result:
  - Only **partial fit** → requires redesign

## Achieving Customer–Value Fit

- Goal: Align **value map** with **customer profile**
- If mismatch:
  - Adjust product/service
  - Refine value proposition

### Types of Fit

- **On-paper fit**
  - Internal alignment during design
- **Market fit**
  - Real-world validation after launch
- **Business model fit**
  - Ensures scalability and profitability

## Key Takeaways

- Always base value proposition on **real user research**
- Focus on **most important jobs**, not everything
- Use the canvas to:
  - Structure thinking
  - Validate assumptions
  - Improve service–customer alignment
- Strong value proposition = higher chance of product/service success
