---
title: "Co-Creation and Company Culture Are Important to Service Design"
---

## Service Design Requires Organizational Change

- Service design is not only about customer experience
- It also transforms:
  - Internal processes
  - Decision-making
  - Company culture
- Success depends on building a **collaborative organization**

## Workshops as the Core Practice

- Much of service design happens in **workshops**
- Common activities:
  - Stakeholder discussions
  - Journey mapping, personas
  - Business model exploration
  - Prioritization exercises
- Workshops enable:
  - Shared understanding
  - Faster alignment
  - Collective problem-solving

## Co-Creation Is Central

- Service design is always done **with stakeholders**, not alone

- Involves:
  - Cross-functional teams
  - Executives and operational roles
  - Customers (through testing and feedback)
- Benefits:
  - Diverse perspectives → better solutions
  - Stronger ownership → smoother implementation
  - Richer insights from real experiences

## Role of Customers in Co-Creation

- Customers are part of:
  - Ideation
  - Prototype testing
- Helps uncover:
  - Real pains and gains
  - Constraints and expectations
- Ensures solutions are grounded in reality

## Building a Service Design Culture

- Goal: create a **culture of service design**
- Characteristics:
  - Customer-centered thinking
  - Collaboration across teams
  - Openness to experimentation
  - Alignment between strategy and execution
- Requires:
  - Shifting mindset (not jumping to solutions)
  - Encouraging empathy and divergent thinking

## Aligning Teams and Execution

- Challenges:
  - Multiple teams working differently
  - Fragmented service delivery
- Key needs:
  - Consistency (congruency) in execution
  - Alignment between:
    - UX and Agile
    - Strategy and delivery

## What Good Collaboration Looks Like

- Not imposing solutions on teams
- Instead:
  - Focus on **value exchange**
  - Understand others’ goals and incentives
  - Work toward shared outcomes
- Important mindset:
  - Move from “me vs you” → “we”
  - Act as a facilitator, not a disruptor

## Practical Collaboration Strategies

### Start Small

- Avoid trying to redesign everything at once
- Focus on:
  - Small experiments
  - Quick wins
- Build trust before scaling impact

### Understand Incentives

- Different teams have different goals (e.g., shipping features vs reducing complexity)
- Identify and resolve misalignments early

### Listen First

- Approach with:
  - Curiosity
  - Empathy
- Learn:
  - What has been tried
  - What works / doesn’t
  - Where opportunities exist

### Build Trust Gradually

- Collaborate through:
  - Shared decisions
  - Joint successes
- Trust enables tackling bigger problems later

## Decision-Making in Service Design

- Always start with **customer needs**
- Then evaluate:
  - Business impact
  - System complexity
  - Cost and feasibility
- Multiple solutions may work → choose based on:
  - Overall value (customer + business)
  - Long-term efficiency

## Key Takeaways

- Co-creation is **essential**, not optional
- Collaboration drives:
  - Alignment
  - Better solutions
  - Successful implementation
- Service designers must:
  - Facilitate collaboration
  - Balance customer and business needs
  - Build trust and culture over time
- Real impact comes from:
  - Small wins → shared ownership → large transformation
