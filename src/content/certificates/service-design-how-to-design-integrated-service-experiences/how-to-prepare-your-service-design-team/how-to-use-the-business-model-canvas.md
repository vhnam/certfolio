## Business Model Canvas

### Purpose of Business Model Canvas

- Business Model Canvas: Tool to map and understand how a service fits into a business.
- Goal:
  - Create shared understanding of the business model
  - Support discussion and alignment across stakeholders
  - Analyze and improve existing or new services

### Why It Matters in Service Design

- Service design: Focuses on entire ecosystems, not just interfaces.
- Key difference from UX:
  - Requires understanding business models and value delivery
- Outcome:
  - Align service experience with business objectives
  - Ensure value is delivered across multiple touchpoints and channels

### Core Components of the Canvas

#### Infrastructure

- Key partners: External entities that support operations.
- Key activities: Essential actions required to deliver value.
- Key resources: Tools, systems, and assets needed to perform activities.

#### Value

- Value proposition: The core value delivered to customers.
  - Examples:
    - New solutions
    - Cost reduction
    - Added benefits

#### Customers

- Customer segments: Target users (similar to personas in UX).
- Customer relationships:
  - Acquisition: How to get customers
  - Retention: How to keep customers
  - Growth: How to increase customer value
- Channels: Ways to deliver value (e.g., online, offline, hybrid)

#### Financials

- Cost structure: All costs involved in delivering the service.
- Revenue streams: How the business earns money.

### Relationships Between Components

- Cause-effect structure:
  - Partners → enable activities → use resources → deliver value
- Value proposition ↔ customer relationships:
  - Strongly interconnected
  - Focus on long-term customer lifecycle

### Sustainability Consideration

- Sustainability: Additional dimension to evaluate:
  - Environmental impact
  - Long-term business viability
  - Ethical implications of revenue models

### Practical Use

- Use cases:
  - Design new services
  - Analyze existing business models
  - Identify opportunities for innovation and improvement

### Key Insight

- Understanding business models early:
  - Critical for impactful service design
  - Helps align design decisions with business strategy
