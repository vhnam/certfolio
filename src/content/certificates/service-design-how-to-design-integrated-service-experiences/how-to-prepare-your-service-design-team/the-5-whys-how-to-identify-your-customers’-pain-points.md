## Identifying the Right Problem & Business Context

### The Importance of the Right Problem

- Right problem: Ensure you are solving the correct underlying issue.
- Root cause: Focus on fundamental causes, not surface symptoms.
- Impact:
  - Directly affects service success or failure
  - Determines relevance of the value proposition

### The 5 Whys Method

#### Definition

- 5 Whys: Iterative questioning technique to uncover root causes of a problem.
- Origin: Developed at Toyota in the 1930s.

#### Purpose

- Identify deep customer pain points
- Understand motivations and dependencies
- Validate whether the problem is worth solving

#### Process

1. Define problem: Start with an observed issue.
2. Ask why: Identify immediate cause.
3. Repeat why (≈5 times): Drill deeper into cause-effect chain.
4. Reach root cause: Identify fundamental issue behind the problem.

#### Example (Robot Failure)

1. Problem: Robot stopped.
2. Why: Circuit overloaded → fuse blew.
3. Why: Bearings lacked lubrication.
4. Why: Oil pump not circulating properly.
5. Why: Pump intake clogged.
6. Root cause: Missing filter.

### Applying 5 Whys in Service Design

- Use before designing solutions
- Focus on:
  - Customer pain points
  - Behavioral motivations
  - System dependencies
- Outcome:
  - Better alignment between problem and solution
  - Stronger value proposition

## Business Objectives & Context

### Why Business Context Matters

- Business objectives: Define why the service exists.
- Alignment:
  - Connect user needs with company goals
  - Avoid solving irrelevant or low-impact problems

### Business Objectives & Context Canvas

#### Purpose

- Clarify goals and decision-making criteria
- Reduce assumptions and bias
- Align stakeholders on priorities

#### Key Questions

- How do we measure success?
  - Satisfaction scores
  - Repeat usage
  - Behavioral metrics
- Why is this important?
  - Relation to core service experience
- Who are stakeholders?
  - UX team
  - Development team
  - Service designers
- What enables success?
  - Existing systems (e.g., booking flow, support)
- What restricts success?
  - Lack of data or empathy
  - Business model limitations
  - Incorrect assumptions

### Example: Airbnb

#### Problem Focus

- Onboarding experience: Entering and accessing accommodation.
- Issue:
  - Difficulty accessing rented spaces
  - Breaks core service promise

#### Metrics

- Customer satisfaction scores
- Repeat bookings
- Usage frequency

#### Insights

- Core experience failures damage trust
- Platform mindset (“just a connector”) can harm service quality
- Users perceive the entire experience as part of one brand

### Key Insight

- Combine problem discovery + business alignment:
  - Use 5 Whys to find the right problem
  - Use business context to ensure it matters
- Result:
  - More strategic, impactful service design decisions
