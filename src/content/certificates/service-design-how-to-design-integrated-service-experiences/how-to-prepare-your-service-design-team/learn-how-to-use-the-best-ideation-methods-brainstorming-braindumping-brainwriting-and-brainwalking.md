## What Brainstorming Is

- Brainstorming: Group ideation technique to generate many ideas
- Goal:
  - Leverage collective thinking
  - Build on each other’s ideas
  - Shift from evaluation → generation
- Used for:
  - Solving design problems
  - Exploring opportunities
  - Planning and strategy

## Common Pitfalls

- Dominant participants limit others
- Lack of facilitation → unfocused sessions
- Evaluating ideas too early → kills creativity
- Losing focus on user needs or problem statement

## Core Rules for Effective Brainstorming

### Set Clear Constraints

- Define:
  - Time limit (typically 15–60 mins)
  - Problem statement or **“How Might We” question**
- Focus on one topic at a time

### Defer Judgment

- No criticism (verbal or non-verbal)
- Create a safe space for sharing ideas
- Encourage openness and participation

### Encourage Wild Ideas

- “Crazy” ideas can lead to breakthroughs
- Easier to refine than invent from scratch

### Aim for Quantity

- More ideas → higher chance of strong solutions
- Principle: **quantity breeds quality**

### Build on Others’ Ideas

- Use “yes, and…” instead of “but”
- Combine and expand ideas collaboratively

### Be Visual

- Use sketches, sticky notes, diagrams
- Makes ideas clearer and easier to share

### One Conversation at a Time

- Listen actively
- Avoid parallel discussions

## Beyond Brainstorming: Other Ideation Methods

### Braindump (Individual)

- Silent idea generation
- Write ideas quickly (3–10 mins)
- Share afterward with the group
- Benefits:
  - Clears mental space
  - Gives introverts a voice

### Brainwriting (Hybrid)

- Write ideas → pass to others → build on them
- Done silently
- Benefits:
  - Reduces bias
  - Equal participation
  - More structured idea building

### Brainwalking (Hybrid + Movement)

- Participants move between stations
- Build on ideas physically
- Benefits:
  - Keeps energy high
  - Avoids getting stuck on one idea

## Individual vs Group Ideation

- Creativity works best when combining:
  - Individual thinking
  - Group collaboration
- Recommended flow:
  - Start with individual ideation
  - Then expand with group sessions
  - Iterate between both

## Role of the Facilitator

- Create a safe, creative environment
- Guide focus and energy
- Prevent judgment and dominance
- Encourage participation and diversity of ideas

## Key Takeaways

- Brainstorming is powerful but needs structure
- Follow core rules to avoid common failures
- Combine multiple ideation methods for best results
- Balance:
  - Freedom (creative thinking)
  - Structure (clear goals and facilitation)
- Ultimate goal:
  - Generate diverse, high-quality ideas through collaboration
