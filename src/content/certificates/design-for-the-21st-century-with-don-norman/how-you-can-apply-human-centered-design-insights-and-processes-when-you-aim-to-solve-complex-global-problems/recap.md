## What You Learned in This Lesson

### Applying Human-Centered Design

- Human-Centered Design (HCD): A design approach focused on people, their needs, and real-world contexts.
- Global Application: Use HCD insights and processes to address complex global challenges.
- Designer Responsibility: Designers have the ability—and responsibility—to improve lives and create meaningful change.

## People-Centered Approach

### Focus on People

- Design With Communities: Prioritize the needs, capabilities, and contexts of the people affected.
- Shared Ownership: Encourage collaboration and participation.
- Ethical Commitment: Take responsibility for social and systemic impact.

## Systems Thinking and Feedback

### Feedback Loops

- Feedback Loop: When the results of actions influence future behavior.
- Importance: Helps explain why complex systems are difficult to understand.
- Delayed Consequences: Many global challenges involve long time gaps between cause and effect.

### Systems Thinking

- Everything Is a System: Problems are part of interconnected socio-technical systems.
- Root Cause Focus: Avoid solving only surface symptoms.
- Whole-System Perspective: Analyze interactions and trade-offs.

## Root Cause Analysis

### The 5 Whys Method

- 5 Whys: A technique that repeatedly asks “Why?” to uncover deeper causes.
- Investigative Depth: Continue probing beyond the first explanation.
- System Awareness: Recognize that problems often have multiple root causes.

## Incremental Change Through Muddling Through

### Small and Simple Interventions

- Muddling Through: An incremental approach to solving major problems.
- Small Steps: Address manageable parts of complex challenges.
- Learning Process: Treat each step as a learning opportunity.
- Long-Term Innovation: Build meaningful change gradually rather than through large, risky interventions.

## Key Takeaway

- Solve the Correct Problem: Reframe issues to identify root causes.
- Focus on People: Keep human impact central.
- Think Systemically: Understand interconnected systems.
- Move Incrementally: Create change through small, sustained efforts.
- Embrace Responsibility: Designers have the power to shape a better world.
