## Applying Human-Centered Design to Global Challenges

### Core Philosophy

- Human-Centered Design (HCD): A design approach that focuses on people, their needs, and real-world contexts to solve meaningful problems.
- Real Problem vs. Symptom: Designers must identify and address root causes, not surface-level issues.
- Designer’s Responsibility: Designers have the ability—and therefore the responsibility—to improve lives and create meaningful change.

Quoted verbatim:

> “Engineers and businesspeople are trained to solve problems. Designers are trained to discover the real problems. A brilliant solution to the wrong problem can be worse than no solution at all: solve the correct problem.”
> — Don Norman

### Designer’s Unique Role

- Discover the real problem before solving it.
- Focus on people rather than technology or business constraints alone.
- Take ownership in addressing complex societal and global issues.

## Systems Thinking and Root Cause Analysis

### Feedback Loops

- Feedback Loops: Cyclical processes where outputs of a system influence future inputs.
- Importance: Help designers understand how actions create consequences within a larger system.

### Systems Thinking

- Systems Thinking: Viewing problems as part of interconnected systems rather than isolated events.
- Goal: Identify structural causes instead of reacting to symptoms.

### 5 Whys Method

1. Identify the problem: Clearly define the issue.
2. Ask “Why?”: Determine the cause.
3. Repeat asking “Why?”: Continue at least five times.
4. Analyze the system: Examine relationships between causes.
5. Identify root cause: Focus intervention at the systemic level.

## Muddling Through Complex Problems

### Incremental Problem-Solving

- Muddling Through: Tackling large, complex challenges by starting with small, manageable parts of the root problem.
- Small Steps: Generate practical, testable improvements.
- Iterative Progress: Learn and adapt as you move forward.

### Practical Application

- Start with a limited scope.
- Test solutions in real contexts.
- Refine based on feedback.
- Expand impact gradually.
