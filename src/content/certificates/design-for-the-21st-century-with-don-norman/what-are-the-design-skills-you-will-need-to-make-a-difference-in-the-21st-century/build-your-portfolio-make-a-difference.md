## Purpose of the Project

This activity challenges you to apply **human-centered design (HCD)** to a real problem in your local community. The goal is to:

- Practice research, ideation, prototyping, and testing
- Work with real stakeholders
- Create a strong, portfolio-ready case study
- Address meaningful societal challenges

Although optional, the project is strongly recommended for gaining practical experience and confidence.

## Two Project Options

You can choose between two paths:

### Option 1: Focused Project — Improve Design Education

Aligned with **UN Global Goal #4: Quality Education**, this option asks:

> _How can we change design education in our community to match the needs of the 21st century?_

You should:

- Engage with local educators, students, or organizations
- Identify root problems through interviews and observations
- Develop and test a small intervention (concept or prototype)
- Document your entire process

Collaboration is encouraged, but working alone is acceptable.

### Option 2: Adventurous Project — Address Any UN Sustainable Development Goal

Choose one of the **17 UN Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs)** and tackle a local issue connected to it (e.g., Zero Hunger, Climate Action).

You should:

- Partner with a nonprofit, local leader, or knowledgeable community member
- Define a clear design question
- Conduct research and fieldwork
- Develop and test a prototype solution
- Document everything for your portfolio

Both options follow the same HCD process — the difference is the scope and theme.

## Key Skills You’ll Practice

- User interviews and field observations
- Problem framing and root cause analysis
- Ideation and rapid prototyping
- Stakeholder collaboration
- Systems thinking
- Case study storytelling

These are career-critical skills valued across the design field.

## What You’ll Need

- A computer with internet access
- Basic sketching materials
- A phone or camera to document your work

Documentation is essential. Capture:

- Notes
- Photos
- Sketches
- Iterations
- Testing feedback

This will help refine your ideas and create a compelling portfolio case study.

## Final Outcome

By completing this project, you will:

- Apply human-centered design to real-world challenges
- Build a meaningful, polished portfolio piece
- Strengthen your ability to solve systemic problems
- Gain hands-on experience beyond theory

The core message:
Design is about solving real problems — and this project helps you prove you can do exactly that.
