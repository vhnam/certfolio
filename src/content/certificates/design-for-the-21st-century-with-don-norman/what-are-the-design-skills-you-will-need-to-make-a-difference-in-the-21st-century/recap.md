## Congratulations on Completing the Course

You’ve finished _Design for the 21st Century with Don Norman_. This marks an important step toward becoming a designer capable of creating meaningful, systemic change.

## What You’ve Learned

Throughout the course, you explored:

- Why many current design and success metrics are flawed
- How designers can gain influence and tackle larger challenges
- How to grow within a company and move into positions of authority
- Why failure is a powerful form of learning
- How to communicate clearly and gain support for your ideas
- The power of being a generalist
- The critical skills required for 21st-century designers
- How even a lone designer can begin addressing global issues
- How design education must evolve to meet modern challenges

## The Bigger Message

The world faces complex, interconnected problems. Designers are uniquely equipped to address them — not just by creating products, but by reframing systems, aligning stakeholders, and focusing on human needs.

Real change requires:

- Courage
- Systems thinking
- Cross-disciplinary collaboration
- Continuous learning

## What Comes Next

The responsibility now shifts to you.

Use your skills to:

- Challenge outdated assumptions
- Work across disciplines
- Focus on meaningful problems
- Design for long-term impact

The 21st century needs designers who think broadly, act responsibly, and build solutions that truly improve lives.
