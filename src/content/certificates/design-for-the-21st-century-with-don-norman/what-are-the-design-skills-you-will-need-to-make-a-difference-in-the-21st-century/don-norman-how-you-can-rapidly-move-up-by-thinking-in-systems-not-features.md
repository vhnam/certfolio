title: "Don Norman: How You Can Rapidly Move Up by Thinking in Systems (Not Features)"
---

## The Goal: Gain Authority to Make Real Impact

### Why Move Up?

- Greater influence: Senior roles allow designers to shape company direction.
- Strategic power: Authority enables steering decisions toward meaningful societal problems.
- Broader impact: Decisions can benefit the company, society, and the world.

Quoted verbatim:

> “Think broadly, think in systems. Think about the company, society, the world.”
> — Don Norman

## The Core Shift: From Craft to Systems Thinking

### The Common Limitation

- Narrow focus: Designers prioritize their own work or specific products.
- Attachment to craft: Overemphasis on the design itself.
- Missed perspective: Failure to see the company as an interconnected system.

### Systems Perspective

- No single department is most important.
- A company functions as an integrated system.
- Decisions must consider:
  - Business viability
  - Long-term strategy
  - Organizational alignment

## The Required Knowledge: Learn the Fundamentals of Business

### Essential Business Skills

- Statistics
- Finance
- Accounting
- Marketing
- Supply chain
- Administration

### MBA as One Path

- MBA degree: One way to gain business knowledge.
- Not mandatory: The knowledge matters more than the credential.
- Self-learning: Business fundamentals can be learned independently.

## What Executives Value

### Big-Picture Thinking

- Prioritize what is best for the company.
- Be willing to delay or reject good ideas if they are not strategically appropriate.
- Balance innovation with timing and organizational readiness.

### Leadership Qualities

- Broader viewpoint beyond personal work.
- Understanding of business constraints.
- Ability to integrate design within larger systems.

## Practical Steps to Move Up

1. Learn business fundamentals: Build literacy in finance, marketing, and operations.
2. Think systemically: View design as part of a larger organizational ecosystem.
3. Prioritize company goals: Align design decisions with strategic objectives.
4. Expand impact: Consider implications for society and the world.

## The Bigger Mission

- Company level: Contribute to sustainable business success.
- Societal level: Influence responsible organizational behavior.
- Global level: Design with awareness of long-term human and planetary impact.
