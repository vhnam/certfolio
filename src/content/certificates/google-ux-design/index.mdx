The **Google UX Design Certificate** prepares learners for a career in **user experience (UX) design**, a field focused on improving how people interact with products such as websites, apps, and digital services. UX designers aim to make everyday interactions **useful, enjoyable, and accessible**. The program is beginner-friendly and does not require prior experience or a college degree.

UX design is a **high-demand career** with strong job opportunities. There are more than **58,000 open UX design jobs in the U.S.**, with a **median entry-level salary of $117,000**. After completing the certificate, learners can apply for jobs with **Google and more than 150 partner employers**, including companies like Deloitte, Target, and Verizon. Many graduates report positive career outcomes such as new jobs, promotions, or salary increases within six months of completing the program.

The certificate includes **seven courses and more than 200 hours of instruction**, combining videos, readings, assessments, and hands-on activities. Learners gain practical experience with the **full UX design process**, including understanding user needs, defining problems, generating design ideas, creating wireframes and prototypes, conducting usability testing, and improving designs based on feedback.

Throughout the program, learners create designs using **paper sketches and digital tools like Figma**. By the end of the course, participants will complete **three end-to-end projects** and build a **professional UX portfolio** that can be shared with employers when applying for entry-level UX design roles.
